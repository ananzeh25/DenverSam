Casa Bonita

Quick Info

Type: Dining entertainment complex. Features: Cliff divers, caves, puppet shows, arcades. Cuisine: Elevated Mexican (post-renovation). Reservations: Required, hard to get. Famous from: South Park episode. New owners: Trey Parker and Matt Stone.

Casa Bonita is a Colorado legend—a 52,000-square-foot restaurant and entertainment complex featuring cliff divers, caves, puppet shows, and an elaborate Mexican-themed interior. Famous for its over-the-top atmosphere more than its food (historically), the restaurant has been revamped by South Park creators Trey Parker and Matt Stone.

The Experience

Cliff Divers

Performers dive from a 30-foot waterfall into a pool below. The signature entertainment that defines Casa Bonita.

Caves and Tunnels

Explore winding caves, hidden passages, and mysterious corners throughout the massive complex.

Black Bart's Cave

A dark cave attraction—slightly spooky fun for kids and nostalgic adults.

Puppet Shows

Live puppet performances on a stage near the dining area.

Arcades

Vintage and modern arcade games for pre- or post-dinner entertainment.

The Food

New Menu

After the South Park creators took over, the kitchen was completely revamped with elevated Mexican cuisine. The food is now a feature, not an afterthought.

Sopapillas

The legendary all-you-can-eat sopapillas with honey remain a beloved tradition.

Getting Reservations

Book Online

Reservations are required and released online. They sell out almost immediately when released.

Tips for Scoring Tickets

Follow Casa Bonita on social media for announcements. Be ready when tickets drop. Have your payment information ready. Persistence pays off.

Tips

Book way ahead. Reservations sell out instantly. Keep trying.

Allow time to explore. The complex is huge. Budget time beyond just eating.

Great for kids. The entertainment is magical for children.

Nostalgia factor. If you went as a kid, the renovated version maintains the spirit.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I get reservations?

Check the Casa Bonita website regularly. Reservations are released periodically and sell out fast.

Is the food better now?

Yes—the kitchen was completely revamped under new ownership. It's now quality Mexican cuisine.

Is it worth the hype?

For the experience, absolutely—especially if you have kids or nostalgia for the original.

How long should I plan to spend?

2-3 hours minimum to eat and explore.

Do South Park fans visit?

Yes—the "Casa Bonita" episode made it famous beyond Colorado.
