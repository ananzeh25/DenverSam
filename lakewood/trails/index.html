Bear Creek Trail & Lakewood Trails

Quick Info

Main trail: Bear Creek Trail. Length: 12+ miles through Lakewood. Surface: Paved multi-use. Activities: Biking, walking, running. Connections: Links to foothills trails and Denver. Fee: Free.

Lakewood's trail system centers on Bear Creek Trail—a paved path following Bear Creek from the mountains through the city. The trail connects parks, neighborhoods, and provides access to the foothills for longer adventures.

Bear Creek Trail

The Route

Bear Creek Trail runs east-west through Lakewood, following the creek from Bear Creek Lake Park toward Denver. The paved path is popular for commuting, recreation, and casual strolls.

Connections

The trail connects to the regional trail network. Head east toward Denver or west into the foothills. Multiple access points throughout Lakewood.

Surface

Mostly paved concrete path suitable for bikes, strollers, and wheelchairs. Some natural surface trails branch off.

Green Mountain Trails

Location

William Frederick Hayden Park on Green Mountain offers extensive trail networks for hiking and mountain biking.

Terrain

Mix of easy paths and technical mountain bike trails. Natural surface with elevation gain to the summit.

Views

Summit trails provide panoramic views of Denver and the mountains.

Bear Creek Lake Park Trails

Park Trails

Internal trails throughout the 2,600-acre park. Mix of paved lakeside paths and natural surface trails in the western hills.

Foothills Access

Trails connect to the greater foothills system for extended hiking and biking into the mountains.

Tips

Start at Bear Creek Lake Park. Multiple trailheads and trail options from one location.

Bring water. Limited facilities on most trails.

Watch for bikes. Multi-use trails. Stay alert and keep right.

Early mornings best. Beat the crowds on popular trails.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Bear Creek Trail paved?

Yes—the main Bear Creek Trail is paved concrete, suitable for bikes and strollers.

Are dogs allowed?

Yes, on leash on most trails. Check specific park rules.

Can I mountain bike?

Yes—Green Mountain and Bear Creek Lake Park have designated mountain bike trails.

Are trails free?

Most trails are free. Bear Creek Lake Park charges an entrance fee.
