N Line Light Rail

Quick Info

Service: RTD commuter rail. Route: Union Station to north metro. Northglenn station: 112th Ave. Time to downtown: ~25 minutes. Parking: Free park-and-ride. Best for: Downtown commutes, avoiding I-25 traffic.

The N Line is RTD's commuter rail serving the north metro, connecting Northglenn to downtown Denver's Union Station. The line provides a car-free option for commuting and exploring downtown.

Northglenn Station

Location

Northglenn/112th station serves the city. Located along the I-25 corridor with easy highway access.

Park-and-Ride

Free parking available at the station. Park and take the train downtown to avoid traffic and parking costs.

Facilities

Platform with shelter, ticketing, and accessibility features.

Where It Goes

Union Station

Southern terminus in downtown Denver. Connections to all other rail lines, buses, and Amtrak.

Thornton

Stations in Thornton along the route for north metro access.

Downtown Access

Direct access to LoDo, Coors Field, dining, and entertainment without parking hassles.

Using the N Line

Fares

Standard RTD fares apply. Day passes available. Mobile ticketing through RTD app.

Schedule

Frequent service during peak commute hours. Less frequent midday and weekends.

Travel Time

About 25 minutes from Northglenn to Union Station. Faster than driving during rush hour.

Tips

Peak hours. More frequent service during morning and evening commutes.

Game days. Great for Rockies games—Union Station is near Coors Field.

Buy tickets before boarding. Use the RTD app or station kiosks.

Check schedule. Weekend and evening service runs less frequently.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is parking free at the station?

Yes—free park-and-ride available at Northglenn station.

How long to downtown Denver?

About 25 minutes from Northglenn to Union Station.

Does it go to the airport?

Transfer at Union Station to the A Line for DIA access.

How much does it cost?

Standard RTD fares—check RTD website for current pricing.
