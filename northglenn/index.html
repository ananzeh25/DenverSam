Northglenn, Colorado

Community in the North Metro

Your Guide to Northglenn

Parks, recreation, and suburban life

Northglenn is a working-class community in Denver's north metro area. One of Colorado's first planned cities, it was built in the 1960s and has maintained an affordable, family-oriented character.

E.B. Rains Jr. Memorial Park is the city's centerpiece, with a recreation center, pool, trails, and sports facilities. The city has invested in maintaining and improving its parks system.

Located between Denver, Thornton, and Westminster, Northglenn offers convenient access to the north metro area. The N Line light rail provides transit connections to downtown Denver.

Frequently Asked Questions

Planning your Northglenn visit

How far is Northglenn from Denver?

About 10 miles north of downtown Denver via I-25. The N Line light rail provides transit access.

Is Northglenn a tourist destination?

No, it's primarily a residential community. Visitors typically come for specific events or to see family.

What's Pirate Fest?

An annual community celebration with carnival, vendors, and family activities. Held in late summer at E.B. Rains Park.

Is there shopping in Northglenn?

Basic retail along 104th Avenue. For major shopping, Westminster and Thornton have more options nearby.
