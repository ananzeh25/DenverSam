Woodward Copper

Quick Info

What: Action sports training facility. Activities: Trampolines, foam pits, skateboarding, BMX, skiing progression. Open: Year-round. Ages: All ages and abilities. Sessions: 2-hour drop-in or day camps. Location: Center Village at Copper Mountain.

Woodward Copper is an action sports training facility where athletes of all levels can practice tricks and progression safely. Foam pits and trampolines let you try new moves without the consequences of a hard landing.

Indoor Facilities

Trampolines

Olympic-style trampolines and trampoline lanes for aerial awareness training. Learn flips and rotations before taking them to snow or dirt.

Foam Pits

Ski and snowboard jumps that land in foam. Practice grabs, spins, and tricks in a forgiving environment.

Skatepark

Indoor concrete park with bowls, rails, and street features. Skateboarding and BMX sessions available.

Tumble Track

Spring-loaded track for practicing flips and tricks. Great for building air awareness.

Outdoor Progression

Summer

Outdoor foam pits and jump lines. Mountain bike features with soft landings. Connects to on-mountain biking.

Winter

Snow features designed for progression. Practice terrain park moves in a controlled environment before hitting the main park.

Programs

Drop-In Sessions

2-hour sessions available for open use of facilities. Coaches on hand to help. Good for trying Woodward for the first time.

Camps

Multi-day camps for intensive progression. Summer and winter sessions. Focus on specific disciplines.

Private Coaching

One-on-one instruction for accelerated learning. Available for all disciplines.

Tips

Book ahead. Popular sessions fill up, especially during ski season and summer weekends.

Great rainy day activity. If afternoon storms hit or ski conditions are poor, Woodward keeps kids (and adults) entertained.

All skill levels welcome. Beginners learn basics while advanced athletes push limits. Coaches help everyone progress.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need experience?

No. Complete beginners can learn basic skills. Coaches help you start at your level.

Is there an age limit?

No minimum age for many activities. Young kids can use trampolines and age-appropriate features.
