Quick Facts: EGE to Vail Valley

The stress-free way to reach Vail and Beaver Creek

Airport Code: EGE (Eagle County Regional Airport)

Distance to Vail: 35 miles west on I-70

Distance to Beaver Creek: 25 miles

Drive Time: 25-35 minutes

Why fly into Eagle? EGE is 35 miles from Vail versus 100+ from Denver. You skip I-70 ski traffic entirely and arrive in 30 minutes instead of 2-3 hours.

Why Choose Eagle County Airport

The smart way to reach Vail Valley

Eagle County Regional Airport serves Vail, Beaver Creek, and the entire Vail Valley. During ski season, direct flights operate from major cities across the US.

EGE Advantages

  • Proximity: 35 miles to Vail, 25 to Beaver Creek
  • No I-70 stress: You're already past the tunnel and traffic
  • Quick transfers: 25-35 minutes to your resort
  • Smaller airport: Less crowded, faster baggage
  • Direct flights: Major carriers serve EGE in winter

Flight Availability

  • Peak Season: December-March, daily flights from many cities
  • Summer: Reduced schedule, some routes suspended
  • Airlines: American, United, Delta (seasonal)
  • Origin Cities: Dallas, Chicago, LA, New York, Miami, Atlanta, and more

The Trade-Off

EGE flights cost more than Denver and schedules are limited. But the time saved (2+ hours each way) and stress avoided often justify the premium—especially for a week-long ski trip.

Transportation Options

Getting from EGE to your resort

Shared Shuttles ($45-65/person)

Colorado Mountain Express and Epic Mountain Express operate from EGE. Shuttles coordinate with flight arrivals.

  • Book in advance during ski season
  • Service to Vail Village, Lionshead, Beaver Creek
  • Multiple stops possible—add 15-30 minutes
  • Ski equipment included

Private Car/SUV ($150-250)

For groups or families, private service makes sense—the per-person cost drops quickly.

  • Direct to your lodging address
  • No waiting for other passengers
  • Child seats available
  • Book round-trip for best value

Rental Car ($80-150/day)

Major rental companies operate at EGE. Consider if you want to explore beyond your resort.

  • Hertz, Avis, Budget, Enterprise available
  • AWD recommended in winter
  • Vail has paid parking ($30-50/day at base)
  • Good for day trips to Aspen or other areas

Taxi/Rideshare ($80-120)

Limited Uber/Lyft availability at EGE. Taxis are more reliable but should be arranged in advance.

  • High Country Taxi, Vail Valley Taxi
  • Rideshare availability varies—have backup plan
  • Good for spontaneous travel if available

Once you're in the Vail Valley, ECO Transit provides free bus service connecting all communities from Vail to Leadville. You may not need a car at all.

Frequently Asked Questions

Eagle County Airport transportation

Is it worth flying into Eagle instead of Denver?

For most Vail trips, yes. You save 2+ hours each way and avoid I-70 stress. The flight premium is often $100-200—worth it for a week-long trip. Check both options when booking.

How far is Eagle County Airport from Vail?

35 miles, about 30-35 minutes driving. You're west of Vail on I-70, so you drive east toward Denver—the easy direction, away from ski traffic.

Is there Uber at Eagle County Airport?

Limited. Uber and Lyft operate in the Vail Valley but availability at EGE fluctuates. Book a shuttle or private car for guaranteed service, especially during peak arrival times.

When does Eagle County Airport have flights?

Peak service runs December through March for ski season. Summer has reduced schedules. Some routes are winter-only. Check airline schedules for your specific dates.

Should I rent a car at Eagle?

Only if you want flexibility. Vail and Beaver Creek are walkable resorts with free bus systems. Rent if you plan day trips to Aspen or want to explore. Otherwise, shuttle service is easier.
