Tubing at Keystone

Quick Info

Location: Adventure Point, top of Dercum Mountain. Sessions: 1-hour time slots, day and evening. Access: Gondola ride included. Ages: 3 and up (height requirements apply).

Keystone operates one of Colorado's largest tubing hills. Multiple lanes, a magic carpet lift, and evening sessions under lights make it a standout operation. It's particularly good for families—everyone can participate regardless of skiing ability.

Getting There

The tubing hill sits at Adventure Point, accessible via the River Run Gondola. Your tubing ticket includes the gondola ride up. The hill operates at 11,640 feet elevation—dress accordingly.

From River Run Village, board the gondola and ride to the top. Adventure Point has the tubing hill plus a restaurant and other activities.

How It Works

Sessions: Tubing runs in 1-hour time slots. You get unlimited runs during your session—as many trips down as you can make.

Magic carpet: A conveyor belt carries you and your tube back up the hill. No hiking required.

Lanes: Multiple lanes allow for racing or taking it easy. Some lanes are faster than others.

Tubes provided: Single and multi-person tubes available. No need to bring your own.

When to Go

Day sessions: Run throughout the afternoon. Less crowded on weekdays.

Evening sessions: Lit tubing after dark. A different experience—stars overhead, cold air, festive atmosphere. Popular with families after a ski day.

Book ahead: Sessions can sell out, especially evenings and holidays. Reserve online.

Tips

Dress warm. It's cold at 11,640 feet, especially in the evening. Layers, gloves, and a hat are essential. Goggles help in wind.

Arrive early. Check in takes time, and you want to maximize your hour on the hill.

Try the racing lanes. Some lanes are groomed faster than others. Ask the attendants which are quickest.

Combine with dining. Adventure Point has a restaurant at the top. Eat before or after your session—the gondola is already included.

Other Winter Activities

If tubing is full or you want variety:

  • Ice skating at Keystone Lake
  • Snow fort at the base (free)
  • Snowcat tours for dining
  • Snowshoeing and nordic skiing

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does tubing cost?

Prices vary by season and time slot. Evening sessions typically cost more than afternoon. Check current rates when booking.

What's the age/height requirement?

Ages 3 and up, with a minimum height requirement (typically 42 inches). Smaller children can ride with adults in multi-person tubes.

Can we walk up if we're already skiing?

The tubing hill is at the gondola summit. Skiers can access it from the top of the mountain, though you'll still need a tubing ticket.

How many runs can we do in an hour?

Typically 6-10 runs depending on line length. The magic carpet moves quickly, and runs take about 30-60 seconds.
