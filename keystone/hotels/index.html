Where to Stay in Keystone

Quick Info

Main areas: River Run Village (ski-in/ski-out) and Keystone Lodge (lakeside). Style: Mostly condos, some hotel rooms. Pro tip: Book through Keystone for best locations and Kids Ski Free deals.

Keystone lodging clusters around two areas: River Run Village at the main gondola base, and Keystone Lodge near the lake. Both connect via free shuttle. The choice depends on whether you prioritize slope access or a quieter atmosphere.

River Run Village

The main base area with the most lodging options:

What you get:

  • Ski-in/ski-out access to the gondola
  • Walkable restaurants and shops
  • Steps from Kidtopia activities
  • Access to night skiing

Lodging types: Mostly condo-style units ranging from studios to 4-bedrooms. Many have kitchens, which saves money on dining. Quality varies—newer buildings near the plaza are premium; older units farther out are more affordable.

Best for: Families, convenience seekers, those maximizing ski time.

Keystone Lodge Area

The quieter village near the lake:

What you get:

  • Hotel-style rooms (Keystone Lodge)
  • Lakeside setting with ice skating
  • Spa and fitness facilities
  • Less crowded, more relaxed

Trade-off: Not ski-in/ski-out. Free shuttle to River Run runs frequently, but you're dependent on the schedule.

Best for: Those wanting hotel service, spa access, or a quieter stay.

Beyond the Resort

Some visitors stay outside Keystone proper:

Dillon/Silverthorne: 10-15 minutes away. More dining options, outlet shopping, often cheaper lodging. Requires driving to ski.

Frisco: 15 minutes. Charming downtown with restaurants and shops. Good central location for skiing multiple resorts.

Breckenridge: 20 minutes. Historic downtown, extensive lodging and dining. Ski Keystone during the day, enjoy Breck nightlife.

Booking Tips

Book through Keystone for perks. The resort offers "Kids Ski Free" with lodging packages—children 12 and under ski free with paying adults. Third-party bookings don't qualify.

Location matters. River Run is large. Units near the plaza and gondola are most convenient. Check exactly where your unit is before booking.

Consider parking. Some units include heated underground parking; others don't. Winter parking matters—ask when booking.

Holiday rates spike. Christmas, New Year's, and Presidents' Day weeks are significantly more expensive. Weekdays outside holidays offer best value.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it worth staying ski-in/ski-out?

At Keystone, yes. The convenience is significant, especially for families with kids and gear. Walking to the gondola beats loading cars.

Should we stay in Keystone or Breckenridge?

Breckenridge has more nightlife and restaurants. Keystone is quieter and more family-focused. Both are on Epic Pass—you can ski either regardless of where you stay.

Are there hotels or just condos?

Keystone Lodge offers hotel-style rooms. Most other lodging is condo-style through the resort or rental sites like VRBO.

How far in advance should we book?

For holidays and peak weekends, 2-3 months minimum. Regular weekends and weekdays have more availability closer to dates.
