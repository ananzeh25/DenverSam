Getting to Central City

Quick Info

From Denver: 35 miles, about 45 minutes. Main route: Highway 119 through Black Hawk. Scenic route: Peak to Peak Highway from Boulder or Nederland. Adventure route: Oh My God Road from Idaho Springs (not for nervous drivers).

Central City sits in a narrow canyon above Black Hawk, reached by continuing through town on Highway 119. The drive from Denver climbs through Clear Creek Canyon into historic mining country. Several routes offer different experiences.

Highway 119 (Main Route)

From Denver

Take I-70 west to Highway 6/Clear Creek Canyon. Follow to Highway 119 and climb through Black Hawk. Central City is just beyond Black Hawk. About 45 minutes total.

Road Conditions

Paved highway with some curves. Can be icy in winter. Standard passenger car is fine.

Peak to Peak Highway

Coming from Boulder or Nederland, the Peak to Peak Highway (Highway 72/119) offers a scenic mountain route. Longer but beautiful drive through high country.

Oh My God Road

The Adventure Route

Virginia Canyon Road connects Central City to Idaho Springs via a narrow, unpaved mountain road. Known as "Oh My God Road" for its steep drop-offs and tight corners.

Not for Everyone

High-clearance vehicle recommended. Steep, narrow, and no guardrails in places. Beautiful views and historic mining ruins but requires confident driving.

Tips

Highway 119 is the easy choice. Paved, straightforward, and scenic enough.

Park in Central City. Smaller and easier to navigate than Black Hawk for walking around.

Free shuttle between towns. Park once and use the shuttle to explore both.

Winter driving. Mountain roads can be icy. Check conditions before going.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Oh My God Road really that scary?

It's narrow with steep drop-offs. If you're comfortable on mountain roads, it's manageable in dry conditions. Skip it if heights make you nervous.

Can I take a bus from Denver?

Limited public transit. Most visitors drive or arrange private transportation.
