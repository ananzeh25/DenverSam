Central City, Colorado

The Richest Square Mile on Earth

Your Guide to Central City

Opera, casinos, and gold rush history

Central City claims the title "Richest Square Mile on Earth" from its 1859 gold rush days. The town preserves more Victorian character than neighboring Black Hawk, with the famous Central City Opera House as its crown jewel.

The Opera House, built in 1878, hosts summer opera and theater performances in one of the oldest opera houses west of the Mississippi. The surrounding historic district features original Victorian buildings, many now housing casinos and restaurants.

Like Black Hawk, Central City legalized gambling in 1991 to preserve its historic buildings. The town is smaller and more historic-feeling than its neighbor, connected by a free shuttle. Together they offer Colorado's mountain casino experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

Planning your Central City visit

What's the difference between Central City and Black Hawk?

Central City is smaller with more preserved Victorian character and the famous opera house. Black Hawk has larger modern casino resorts. A free shuttle connects them.

When is the opera season?

Summer, typically July and August. The festival features multiple productions in repertory. Book tickets in advance for popular performances.

How far is Central City from Denver?

About 35 miles, roughly 45 minutes via Highway 119 through Black Hawk or the scenic Peak to Peak Highway.

Is Oh My God Road safe to drive?

It's a narrow, unpaved road with steep drop-offs. High-clearance vehicles recommended. Not for nervous drivers, but passable in summer with care.
