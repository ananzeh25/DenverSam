Where to Stay in Central City

Quick Info

Options: Limited in Central City; more hotels in Black Hawk. Casino lodging: Some casinos have rooms. Historic B&Bs: Small inns in Victorian buildings. Alternative: Stay in Black Hawk, use free shuttle.

Central City has limited lodging compared to neighboring Black Hawk. Most visitors either stay in Black Hawk's larger casino resorts and shuttle to Central City, or make the trip a day outing from Denver. For overnight stays, a few options exist within Central City itself.

Central City Options

Casino Hotels

Some Central City casinos offer rooms, though fewer and smaller than Black Hawk properties. Check individual casino websites for current availability.

Historic Inns

A handful of B&Bs and small inns operate in restored Victorian buildings. Intimate atmosphere with period character. Book ahead during opera season.

Black Hawk Hotels

Black Hawk offers more lodging options, from large casino resorts to chain hotels. Free shuttles run between the two towns, making Black Hawk a practical base for Central City exploration.

Major Properties

Ameristar, Monarch, and other Black Hawk casino resorts offer full hotel amenities. Modern rooms, restaurants, and entertainment under one roof.

Opera Season

Summer opera festival significantly impacts lodging availability. Book well in advance if visiting during July and August productions.

Tips

Black Hawk is easier for lodging. More options and modern amenities, with shuttle to Central City.

Day trip from Denver is viable. Only 45 minutes away—evening return is easy.

Book early for opera. Summer festival fills limited rooms quickly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I stay in Central City or Black Hawk?

Black Hawk for lodging convenience. Central City for historic atmosphere if you find availability.

Is the shuttle between towns really free?

Yes. Regular service connects Central City and Black Hawk casinos.
