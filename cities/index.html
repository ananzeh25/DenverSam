Colorado Cities & Towns

Your Guide to the Centennial State

Explore Colorado

From the Front Range to the high country

Colorado offers incredible diversity—from the urban energy of Denver to remote mountain villages, hot springs towns to historic mining communities. Each destination has its own character, seasons, and reasons to visit.

Front Range Cities

Where most Coloradans live

CITY

Denver

The Mile High City. Colorado's capital with world-class dining, craft beer, professional sports, and easy mountain access.

CITY

Boulder

College town with incredible outdoor access. Flatirons hiking, Pearl Street Mall, and a thriving food scene.

CITY

Fort Collins

Craft beer capital of Colorado. Historic Old Town, Colorado State University, and access to Cache la Poudre Canyon.

CITY

Colorado Springs

Garden of the Gods, Pikes Peak, and Olympic training center. Military town with stunning red rock formations.

CITY

Golden

Coors Brewery, Colorado School of Mines, and gateway to the foothills. Small-town charm minutes from Denver.

CITY

Loveland

The Sweetheart City. Sculpture gardens, Devil's Backbone hiking, and gateway to Estes Park through Big Thompson Canyon.

CITY

Pueblo

Chile capital of Colorado. Historic Riverwalk, Lake Pueblo State Park, and steel town heritage. Warmer and more affordable than Denver.

CITY

Greeley

Home of the Greeley Stampede, one of the world's largest rodeos. UNC campus, agricultural roots, and Pawnee Grassland access.

CITY

Longmont

Boulder County's rising star. Left Hand Brewing, revitalized downtown, and Button Rock Preserve hiking.

Denver Metro

Suburbs and neighboring cities

CITY

Aurora

Colorado's most diverse city. Stanley Marketplace, Cherry Creek State Park, and growing food scene from around the world.

CITY

Lakewood

Home to Casa Bonita and Bear Creek Lake Park. Belmar shopping district and easy mountain access via 6th Avenue.

CITY

Arvada

Historic Olde Town with craft breweries and local dining. G Line light rail connects to Denver. Apex Park hiking nearby.

CITY

Westminster

Butterfly Pavilion, Standley Lake, and The Orchard shopping. North metro community with mountain views.

CITY

Thornton

Carpenter Park recreation complex and extensive trail system. Growing north metro city with N Line light rail.

CITY

Centennial

South metro community with Streets at SouthGlenn and Centennial Center Park. Light rail access to downtown Denver.

CITY

Broomfield

Flatiron Crossing shopping and 1stBank Center entertainment. Positioned between Denver and Boulder with mountain views.

CITY

Commerce City

Dick's Sporting Goods Park (Colorado Rapids) and Rocky Mountain Arsenal National Wildlife Refuge with bison and wildlife.

CITY

Northglenn

E.B. Rains Jr. Park and community recreation. North metro city with N Line light rail access.

South Metro

Douglas County and south suburbs

CITY

Littleton

Historic downtown along the South Platte. Hudson Gardens, Chatfield Reservoir, and light rail to Denver.

CITY

Castle Rock

Outlets at Castle Rock and iconic rock formation downtown. Philip S. Miller Park with challenge hill and trails.

CITY

Parker

Small-town Main Street feel with local shops and restaurants. PACE Center arts venue and Cherry Creek Trail access.

Mountain Ski Towns

World-class skiing and alpine villages

SKI

Vail

Colorado's largest ski resort. European-style village, legendary Back Bowls, and world-class dining.

SKI

Aspen

Four mountains, celebrity culture, and Victorian charm. Maroon Bells, fine dining, and summer festivals.

SKI

Breckenridge

Historic Main Street, five peaks of terrain, and highest chairlift in North America. Affordable compared to Vail and Aspen.

SKI

Telluride

Box canyon setting, free gondola, and film festival fame. Remote location keeps crowds lower than other resorts.

SKI

Steamboat Springs

Champagne Powder, hot springs downtown, and authentic Western character. Family-friendly with Olympic heritage.

SKI

Winter Park

Denver's closest major resort. Mary Jane's legendary bumps, Winter Park Express train, and budget-friendly options.

SKI

Keystone

Night skiing, family programs, and three mountains of terrain. Close to Breckenridge on the Epic Pass.

SKI

Crested Butte

Wildflower capital of Colorado. Extreme terrain, colorful Victorian downtown, and remote mountain beauty.

SKI

Beaver Creek

Vail's upscale neighbor. Manicured runs, ski-in/ski-out luxury, and famous chocolate chip cookies.

SKI

Snowmass

Aspen's family-friendly mountain. More terrain than Aspen Mountain, modern base village, and varied skiing.

Gateway Towns

Adventure, history, and natural wonders

PARK

Estes Park

Gateway to Rocky Mountain National Park. Elk roaming downtown, Trail Ridge Road, and mountain town charm.

TRAIN

Durango

Historic narrow gauge railroad to Silverton. Gateway to Mesa Verde, college town energy, and Four Corners access.

SPRINGS

Glenwood Springs

World's largest hot springs pool. I-70 corridor stop, adventure park, and Hanging Lake trailhead.

ALPINE

Ouray

Switzerland of America. Box canyon hot springs, ice climbing, and Million Dollar Highway views.

HISTORY

Silverton

Authentic mining town at 9,318 feet. Durango train terminus, jeep trails, and preserved Victorian character.

PEAK

Manitou Springs

Pikes Peak gateway, mineral springs, and Incline challenge. Quirky downtown, Garden of the Gods access.

CASINO

Black Hawk

Colorado's casino capital. Gaming resorts, historic mining town, and scenic drive through Clear Creek Canyon from Denver.

OPERA

Central City

Historic opera house and Victorian casinos. More preserved character than Black Hawk, with gold rush heritage.

MINE

Cripple Creek

Mollie Kathleen Mine tours and mountain casinos. Wild donkeys roam this former gold rush boomtown near Colorado Springs.

DUNES

Alamosa

Gateway to Great Sand Dunes National Park. North America's tallest dunes in the San Luis Valley high desert.

SPRINGS

Pagosa Springs

World's deepest hot springs and Wolf Creek's legendary snowfall. Remote relaxation in southwest Colorado.

I-70 Corridor

Mountain towns along the highway west

SPRINGS

Idaho Springs

Indian Hot Springs and Mount Evans access. First mountain town on I-70 with Beau Jo's pizza and mining history.

TRAIN

Georgetown

Georgetown Loop Railroad and Victorian silver mining town. Guanella Pass access to Mount Bierstadt.

LAKE

Frisco

Lake Dillon hub with access to multiple ski resorts. Central Summit County base at lower prices than Breckenridge.

Arkansas Valley & High Country

Rivers, peaks, and mountain character

PEAK

Leadville

America's highest city at 10,152 feet. Silver mining history, Mount Elbert access, and Leadville 100 ultramarathon.

RAFT

Buena Vista

Arkansas River rafting capital with hot springs. Collegiate Peaks access and adventure-focused mountain town.

ART

Salida

Creative arts district and downtown whitewater park. Monarch skiing and genuine mountain-town character.
