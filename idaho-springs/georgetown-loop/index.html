Georgetown Loop Railroad Review: Is It Worth It?

The Short Answer: Yes, the Georgetown Loop Railroad is worth it for families. It's touristy, it's not cheap ($32 adults, $22 kids), but kids genuinely love it and the mountain scenery is beautiful. Best for ages 3-10. Add the mine tour ($12 extra) if you go.

Quick Facts

  • Distance from Denver: 45 miles (about 50 minutes via I-70)
  • Train ride duration: 1.5 hours round trip
  • Total visit time: 3-4 hours (with mine tour and exploring)
  • Cost: $32 adults, $22 kids (3-15), under 3 free
  • Mine tour add-on: $12 adults, $10 kids
  • Season: May through December
  • Elevation: 8,500 feet
  • Parking: Free at both stations
  • Best for: Families with kids ages 3-10, grandparent visits

My Honest Review

I put off the Georgetown Loop Railroad for years. Seemed too touristy. Too expensive. Too boring for adults.

Finally went when family visited with kids. And honestly? It was actually great.

Here's the real review of doing Georgetown Loop with kids (ages 5 and 8 when we went).

What Is the Georgetown Loop?

Historic narrow gauge railroad from the 1880s. Train goes between Georgetown and Silver Plume through the Colorado mountains.

The "loop" is where the track crosses over itself on a high bridge—engineering that was impressive in 1884 and still kind of impressive now. The train climbs 600 feet in just 2 miles by making this spiraling loop.

The Train Experience

Getting Started

Two stations: Georgetown and Silver Plume. We started at Georgetown (bigger parking lot, more facilities).

Tickets available online or at the station. Online is slightly cheaper and guarantees your spot—summer weekends sell out.

Choosing Your Car

Open-air or enclosed cars available. Choose open-air unless weather is bad. Much better experience for kids and photos. You'll want to see the views and feel the mountain air.

The Ride Itself

Train goes slow—walking speed at times. But that's the point. Kids don't care about speed; they care about the experience.

The conductor does historical narration. Adults might tune out, but kids love the stories about mining, bandits, and Old West drama.

The Characters (Kids Love This Part)

They have actors. Old West characters who board the train.

Sheriff looking for outlaws. "Bandits" who stage a train robbery (fake, obviously).

Cheesy? Absolutely. Kids ate it up. Even our cynical 8-year-old was into it.

Lebanon Silver Mine Tour

Extra $12 adults, $10 kids

Thought about skipping it to save money. Glad we didn't.

You go 500 feet into an actual 1870s silver mine. Our guide was a former miner who knew everything about the operation.

Kids got to wear hard hats, see real mining tools, and learn about what mining life was like (including the awkward-but-educational parts about child labor).

Important: It's 44°F inside the mine year-round. Bring jackets even in summer.

Tour adds 30 minutes to your visit. Worth it for the experience.

What Kids Actually Liked

  • The "robbery" - even knowing it's fake, they loved it
  • The high bridge crossing - genuinely impressive views
  • Hard hats in the mine - instant coolness factor
  • The loop itself - minds blown by "train over train"
  • Random wildlife - we saw mountain goats from the train
  • The gift shop - of course

What Parents Actually Liked

  • Kids contained for 1.5 hours - they can't run off on a train
  • Legitimately pretty scenery - Instagram-worthy mountain views
  • Real history - educational despite the cheese
  • No hiking required - mountain experience without the effort
  • Beer in the dining car - yes, they sell beer
  • Georgetown afterward - cute town for lunch

Age-by-Age Guide

  • Under 3: Free but might get bored or scared (loud whistle, dark tunnel)
  • Ages 3-6: Perfect age—everything is magical
  • Ages 7-10: Still fun, especially with the mine tour
  • Ages 11-14: Eye rolls but secretly enjoying it
  • Ages 15+: On phones unless you confiscate them

Best Times to Visit

By Season

  • Summer (June-August): Most departures, best weather, most crowded
  • Fall (September-October): Spectacular aspen colors, very popular—book way ahead
  • Winter (November-December): Santa trains, Polar Express theme, cold but magical
  • Spring (May): Less crowded, weather can be unpredictable

Time of Day

  • First train (10am): Less crowded, cooler temps in summer
  • Midday trains: Most crowded, hottest
  • Last train (around 2:30pm): Good if you want to explore Georgetown first

Exploring Georgetown

The town is legitimately charming. Victorian buildings, antique shops, decent food.

Where to eat:

  • Happy Cooker: Best breakfast, go early (lines form)
  • Lucha Cantina: Good Mexican, solid kids menu
  • Rocky Mountain Chocolate Factory: For bribes and rewards

Walk the historic downtown afterward. Takes 30 minutes. Kids tolerate it for chocolate promises.

Make It a Full Day

  • Before the train: Gold panning at Clear Creek (~$10/kid, they love it)
  • After the train: Walk to Georgetown Lake (easy, pretty views)
  • On the drive: Stop at the buffalo overlook on I-70
  • Make it bigger: Continue to Loveland Pass for insane mountain views

The Real Cost

For a family of 4 with mine tour:

  • Train tickets: ~$108
  • Mine tour: ~$44
  • Parking: Free
  • Lunch in Georgetown: ~$60
  • Treats and gift shop: ~$30

Total day: Around $240

Expensive? Yes. Worth it? Actually yes.

Insider Tips

  • Train is LOUD: Whistle blows constantly. Prep sensitive kids.
  • Motion sickness: Face forward, sit in middle of car
  • Bathrooms: Use station facilities first—train bathrooms are rustic
  • The robbery: Can scare little ones. Tell them it's pretend beforehand.
  • Sunscreen: You're in the sun most of the ride. Altitude = stronger UV.
  • Layers: Mountain weather changes fast, and the mine is cold

Should You Go?

Skip If...

  • Kids are under 3 (won't remember, might hate the noise)
  • Anyone has mobility issues (lots of steps)
  • You hate touristy things (this is peak tourist)
  • Budget is super tight (many free mountain activities exist)

Go If...

  • Kids are into trains (obviously)
  • Grandparents are visiting (perfect pace)
  • You want mountain views without hiking
  • Weather is good (makes everything better)
  • It's fall and you want aspen colors

The Final Verdict

I wanted to dismiss it. Too expensive, too touristy, too slow.

But watching the kids' faces when we crossed the high bridge? When the "bandits" appeared? In the mine wearing hard hats?

Worth every penny.

The history is actually interesting. The views are legitimately beautiful. Georgetown is charming. And yes, adults had fun too (beer helps).

It's touristy because it's good. Sometimes that's okay.

Would I do it again? With kids, absolutely. Without kids? Probably not. But as a family experience? One of our better Colorado adventures.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Georgetown Loop Railroad worth the money?

For families with kids ages 3-10, yes. The combination of the train ride, mountain scenery, historical experience, and optional mine tour creates a memorable day. Adults without kids might find it slow-paced and overpriced. The total cost for a family of four runs about $150-240 depending on add-ons and lunch.

Is the Georgetown Loop family-friendly?

Very much so. It's designed for families. Kids love the train, the characters, and especially the mine tour with hard hats. The pace is relaxed, there are bathrooms at the stations, and Georgetown has family-friendly restaurants. Best age range is 3-10 years old.

What's the weather like at the Georgetown Loop?

Georgetown sits at 8,500 feet elevation. Summer days are warm (70s°F) but evenings cool down quickly. Afternoon thunderstorms are common June-August. Fall is crisp and clear. Winter is cold (20s-40s°F). Always bring layers—mountain weather changes fast. Inside the mine is 44°F year-round regardless of outside weather.

How long does the Georgetown Loop take?

The train ride itself is about 1.5 hours round trip. Add 30 minutes for the mine tour if you do it. With parking, boarding, exploring Georgetown, and lunch, plan for 3-4 hours total.

Should I book Georgetown Loop tickets in advance?

Yes, especially for summer weekends and fall foliage season (September-October). Online tickets are slightly cheaper and guarantee your spot. Winter Santa trains sell out weeks ahead.

Is the mine tour worth the extra money?

Yes. It's only $12 adults/$10 kids and adds genuine educational value. Kids love wearing hard hats and seeing inside a real 1870s mine. The 44°F temperature inside is a nice break on hot summer days.

Open-air or enclosed train car?

Open-air unless the weather is bad. Better views, better photos, more fun for kids. You'll want to experience the mountain air and see the scenery without glass in the way.

Can little kids handle the train robbery scene?

Most kids love it, but very young or sensitive children might be scared. The "bandits" board the train with cap guns (loud but obviously fake). Prep your kids ahead of time that it's all pretend—like a play.
