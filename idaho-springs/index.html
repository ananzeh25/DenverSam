Idaho Springs, Colorado

Gateway to the High Country

Your Guide to Idaho Springs

Hot springs, mining history, and mountain access

Idaho Springs sits right on I-70, making it the first real mountain town Denver visitors encounter. Just 30 miles from Denver, it serves as a gateway to the ski resorts while offering its own attractions—hot springs, historic downtown, and access to Mount Evans.

The Indian Hot Springs have drawn visitors since the 1860s. Historic Miner Street features locally-owned shops, restaurants, and the famous Beau Jo's pizza. The Argo Gold Mine offers tours of one of Colorado's most productive gold operations.

Mount Evans Scenic Byway, the highest paved road in North America, starts here. In summer, you can drive to 14,130 feet. The town also connects to Central City via the infamous Oh My God Road.

Explore Idaho Springs

Springs, mines, and mountain roads

SPRINGS

Indian Hot Springs

Natural hot springs resort since 1863. Outdoor pool, private baths, and cave pools. Perfect stop after skiing.

PEAK

Mount Evans

Highest paved road in North America climbs to 14,130 feet. Mountain goats, alpine tundra, and summit views. Summer only.

MINE

Argo Gold Mine

Historic mine tour with gold panning. Learn about the tunnel that connected Idaho Springs to Central City.

TOWN

Miner Street

Historic downtown with local shops and restaurants. Beau Jo's mountain-style pizza is a Colorado institution.

TRAIL

Chicago Lakes Trail

Popular hike to alpine lakes beneath Mount Evans. Moderate difficulty with stunning high-altitude scenery.

ROAD

Oh My God Road

Dramatic unpaved road connecting to Central City. Historic mining ruins and spectacular views for adventurous drivers.

Frequently Asked Questions

Planning your Idaho Springs visit

How far is Idaho Springs from Denver?

About 30 miles, roughly 40 minutes via I-70. It's the first major town after the Eisenhower Tunnel approach.

When is Mount Evans road open?

Typically late May through early October, depending on snow. The summit section may close earlier. Check current conditions before going.

Is Indian Hot Springs worth visiting?

Yes, especially after skiing or hiking. The cave pools are unique. It's not a luxury spa—it's rustic and historic, which is part of the charm.

What's special about Beau Jo's?

Mountain-style pizza with thick honey-drizzled crust. The original location is here. It's a Colorado tradition, not gourmet—expect hearty portions.
