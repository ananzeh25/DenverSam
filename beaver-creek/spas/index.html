Spas in Beaver Creek

Quick Info

Top options: Ritz-Carlton Spa (Bachelor Gulch), Spa Anjali (Park Hyatt). Best for: Post-ski recovery, couples treatments, mountain relaxation. Book ahead: Weekends and holidays fill up.

Beaver Creek's spa offerings match its refined reputation. The major resort hotels operate full-service spas with mountain-inspired treatments, extensive facilities, and the kind of service you'd expect from luxury properties. After a day on the slopes, few things feel better than a skilled massage at altitude.

Ritz-Carlton Spa

Located at the Ritz-Carlton Bachelor Gulch, this spa is the area's most comprehensive:

Facilities:

  • 21,000 square feet of spa space
  • Steam rooms, saunas, and hot tubs
  • Indoor/outdoor heated pool
  • Full fitness center
  • Relaxation lounges with mountain views

Treatments: Full menu of massage, facials, body treatments, and couples services. Mountain-inspired treatments incorporate local ingredients. The après-ski massage is designed specifically for tired skiers.

Access: Open to hotel guests and outside visitors. Day access to facilities is available with a treatment booking.

Spa Anjali at Park Hyatt

Located at the Park Hyatt in Beaver Creek Village:

Facilities:

  • 30,000 square feet spanning three levels
  • Men's and women's relaxation areas
  • Hot tubs with slope views
  • Eucalyptus steam rooms
  • Meditation and yoga spaces

Treatments: Extensive menu including massage, skincare, body treatments, and wellness programs. Known for integrating Eastern and Western techniques.

Location advantage: Right in the village, making it convenient if you're staying nearby or want to combine a treatment with village activities.

Tips

Book ahead. Both spas fill up during peak periods. Reserve treatments before you arrive, especially for weekends and holidays.

Ask about day passes. Even without a treatment, some facilities offer day access. Check current policies.

Schedule for late afternoon. After skiing, before dinner is prime spa time. Book early if you want this window.

Consider couples treatments. Both spas offer dedicated couples rooms. A ski vacation is a good occasion.

Other Options

Beyond the major resort spas:

  • In-room massage: Some services bring therapists to your hotel or condo
  • Avon: The nearby town has additional spa and massage options at lower price points
  • Vail: A short drive expands options considerably

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to be a hotel guest?

No. Both major spas accept outside guests. Booking a treatment typically includes facility access for the day.

What should I tip?

15-20% is customary for spa services in Colorado. Some spas add gratuity automatically for larger treatments.

Are there budget options?

Not really in Beaver Creek proper. For more affordable massage, check Avon or Vail for independent practitioners.

Can we use the pool without a treatment?

Pool access varies. The Ritz-Carlton sometimes offers day passes; Park Hyatt facilities are primarily for guests and spa visitors. Call ahead to confirm current policies.
