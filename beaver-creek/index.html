Beaver Creek, Colorado

Vail's Upscale Neighbor

Your Guide to Beaver Creek

Luxury skiing done right

Beaver Creek takes the Vail experience and adds another layer of polish. The grooming is legendary—corduroy so perfect you almost hate to ski on it. The village is compact and walkable, with ski-in/ski-out access from most lodging.

The resort is famous for its complimentary chocolate chip cookies, served warm at 3pm daily. It's a small touch that captures the Beaver Creek approach: premium experiences at every turn.

Located just 10 miles west of Vail, Beaver Creek shares the Epic Pass. The Birds of Prey downhill course—home to World Cup racing—offers some of the steepest sustained pitch in Colorado. But beginners and intermediates find equally excellent terrain.

Explore Beaver Creek

Manicured slopes and village luxury

SKI

Ski Resort

1,800 acres of immaculately groomed terrain. Birds of Prey for experts, Centennial for cruisers, and dedicated learning areas.

COOKIE

Chocolate Chip Cookies

Fresh-baked chocolate chip cookies served daily at 3pm. A Beaver Creek tradition since opening day in 1980.

VILLAGE

The Village

Ski-in/ski-out pedestrian village with upscale shops, restaurants, and galleries. Ice skating rink at the village center.

RITZ

Bachelor Gulch

The quieter side of Beaver Creek. Home to the Ritz-Carlton with ski-in/ski-out access and fewer crowds.

SPA

Spas

World-class spas at the Ritz-Carlton and Park Hyatt. Post-ski massage and mountain wellness experiences.

DINE

Dining

From on-mountain lunch to fine dining. Beaver Creek's restaurants match the resort's refined reputation.

Plan Your Visit

Lodging and getting there

STAY

Where to Stay

Ski-in/ski-out luxury at the village, Ritz-Carlton at Bachelor Gulch, or value lodging in nearby Avon.

GO

Getting There

2 hours from Denver via I-70. Rental car, shuttle, or private car service options from the airport.

Frequently Asked Questions

Planning your Beaver Creek visit

Is Beaver Creek more expensive than Vail?

Same lift ticket price (shared Epic Pass), but lodging and dining at Beaver Creek tend to run higher. The resort caters to a luxury market.

How far is Beaver Creek from Denver?

About 120 miles, roughly 2 hours via I-70. It's 10 miles west of Vail, just off the interstate at the Avon exit.

Can I ski both Vail and Beaver Creek?

Yes. Both are on the Epic Pass and connected by free shuttle bus. Many visitors split their days between the two resorts.

Is Beaver Creek good for beginners?

Excellent. The resort has dedicated learning terrain with gentle grades and patient instructors. The grooming makes everything feel easier.
