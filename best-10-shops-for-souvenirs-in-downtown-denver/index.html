Best 10 Shops for Souvenirs in Downtown Denver

Looking for Denver souvenirs that aren't terrible? Here's where locals actually shop when their out-of-town friends visit.

Half these places are tourist traps selling the same made-in-China junk. The other half? Actually worth your money. Let me show you which is which.

The Actually Good Spots

1. I Heart Denver Store

1530 Blake St (RiNo)
This is the real deal. Over 200 Colorado artists sell their stuff here. Not mass-produced garbage - actual handmade items from people who live here.

Best buys: Local artist prints ($20-40), handmade jewelry ($30-100), Denver neighborhood maps ($25). Skip the t-shirts unless you find a unique design.

2. Tattered Cover Book Store

Multiple locations (Union Station, McGregor Square, Colfax)
Denver's legendary bookstore. The souvenir section is small but solid. Colorado history books make great gifts for readers.

Best buys: "Colorado: A History of the Centennial State" ($30), local author books, vintage Denver postcards ($2-5).

3. Cry Baby Ranch

1422 Larimer St (LoDo)
Western wear that's actually cool. Not costume cowboy stuff - things people actually wear in Colorado.

Best buys: Vintage belt buckles ($40-80), authentic cowboy hats ($100+), retro Denver signs ($30-50). Expensive but unique.

4. Artisan Center

2757 Larimer St
All local makers. Prices range from $10 stocking stuffers to $500 art pieces. Quality varies but generally good.

Best buys: Pottery ($25-60), Colorado-themed candles ($20), small art prints ($15-30).

The Tourist Traps (But Sometimes You Need Them)

5. Rocky Mountain Chocolate Factory

Downtown locations everywhere
Look, it's overpriced chocolate. But if you need edible souvenirs for coworkers, it works. The fudge is actually decent.

Expect to pay: $8-15 per box. Get the chocolate-covered Oreos if you're going to cave.

6. Where the Buffalo Roam

1616 16th St Mall
Peak tourist shop energy. Colorado flag everything. But sometimes you just need a $15 Denver t-shirt and they have every size.

What's actually decent: The Colorado flag socks ($10), beer koozies ($5), magnets for grandma ($3-7).

7. Welcome to Denver Store

Multiple downtown locations
Generic souvenir warehouse. Same stuff as everywhere else but they have 3XL sizes and decent prices.

Only go if: You forgot to buy something and your flight is in 2 hours.

Hidden Gems Worth Finding

8. Five Green Boxes

1529 S Pearl St (Not downtown but worth the trip)
Quirky home goods and gifts. Half the stuff makes no sense but in a good way.

Best finds: Weird Colorado-themed kitchen stuff ($20-40), actually funny greeting cards ($5), random vintage finds.

9. Talulah Jones

1122 E 17th Ave
Not specifically Denver-themed but great for unique gifts. Good spot if you need something nice for kids.

Worth it for: Fancy stationery ($15-30), unique kids' books ($20), jewelry that doesn't scream "tourist" ($40-100).

What to Actually Buy

Good souvenirs that won't embarrass you:
• Local hot sauce from Savory Spice Shop ($8-12)
• Colorado craft beer (if you can transport it)
• Local coffee from Huckleberry Roasters ($16/bag)
• Actual art from local artists ($30+)
• Colorado-made spirits from Stranahan's (at liquor stores)

Classic tourist stuff that's fine:
• Colorado flag anything (everyone has it)
• Denver Broncos gear (only if they actually like football)
• Rocky Mountain magnets (safe grandparent gift)
• "Native" stickers (ironic if you're not from here)

Skip completely:
• Shot glasses (unless collecting)
• Cheap dreamcatchers (offensive)
• "Mile High Club" shirts (not funny)
• Fake turquoise jewelry
• Snow globes (they leak on planes)

Other Places to Check

Union Station - The Tattered Cover there has a good selection. Plus you can grab a drink while shopping.

Denver Art Museum Shop - Expensive but actually nice stuff. Good for art books and unique jewelry.

16th Street Mall - Every other store is souvenirs. Most are identical. Pick the least crowded one.

Airport Shops - Last resort. Everything costs 3x more but they have Colorado Avalanche gear if you forgot.

Pro Tips

Buy local food products at regular grocery stores - King Soopers has a Colorado section that's way cheaper than gift shops.

The best "souvenir" might be dinner at a Denver restaurant instead of more stuff to pack.

If you're buying for a lot of people, hit up the tourist shops for bulk discounts. They'll negotiate on 10+ items.

Cannabis products can't leave the state. Don't be that person at the airport.

The Bottom Line

For real Colorado stuff: I Heart Denver, Artisan Center, or Cry Baby Ranch.

For standard souvenirs: Any shop on 16th Street Mall - they're all the same.

For edible gifts: Local hot sauce, coffee, or (legally) chocolate.

Your out-of-town friends probably just want a magnet anyway.
