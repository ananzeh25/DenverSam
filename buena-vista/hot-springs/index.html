Hot Springs Near Buena Vista

Quick Info

Main options: Mount Princeton Hot Springs Resort, Cottonwood Hot Springs. Best for: Post-rafting/hiking recovery. Character: Princeton is larger and resort-style; Cottonwood is intimate and rustic.

Buena Vista sits in a geothermal hotspot, with natural hot springs emerging from the mountain slopes. Two main facilities offer developed soaking experiences, each with distinct character. Both provide the perfect recovery after rafting the Arkansas or climbing a 14er.

Mount Princeton Hot Springs Resort

Location: County Road 162, about 8 miles from downtown Buena Vista.

The larger and more developed option. A full resort with lodging, spa services, and multiple pool options. The creekside pools are the highlight—natural hot springs mix with cold creek water, letting you find your perfect temperature.

Pools

Creekside pools: The signature experience. Rock-lined pools along Chalk Creek where hot spring water meets cold mountain stream. Adjust your temperature by moving between pools.

Upper pools: Historic pool area with traditional soaking pools at various temperatures.

Lap pool: Heated pool for swimming.

Facilities

Day use: Available with admission fee. Check current rates and hours.

Lodging: Resort rooms and cabins. Stay overnight for extended pool access.

Spa: Massage and spa services available.

Dining: On-site restaurant and bar.

Cottonwood Hot Springs

Location: County Road 306, about 6 miles from downtown Buena Vista.

Smaller, more rustic, and more intimate than Mount Princeton. Natural pools in a quieter setting. Popular with those seeking a more authentic hot springs experience rather than resort amenities.

Pools

Natural pools: Multiple pools at varying temperatures. Some clothing-optional areas during certain hours.

Indoor pool: Available during colder weather.

Facilities

Day use: Available with admission. More affordable than Princeton.

Lodging: Rustic cabins and lodge rooms. Simple accommodations focused on the hot springs experience.

Atmosphere: More of a retreat center vibe. Quieter, more contemplative.

Which to Choose?

Mount Princeton if: You want more pools, more amenities, and don't mind a busier atmosphere. Great for families.

Cottonwood if: You prefer rustic authenticity, smaller crowds, and a more peaceful vibe.

Both are excellent. Different experiences, both worth visiting. Try both if you have time.

Tips

Go after rafting or hiking. Hot springs are the perfect recovery. Sore muscles love the mineral water.

Weekdays are quieter. Summer weekends get busy, especially at Princeton. Weekday visits offer more relaxation.

Bring water. Hot water is dehydrating. Drink plenty before, during, and after soaking.

Evening soaks are magical. Sunset and stargazing from the pools is special. Night hours may have restrictions—check schedules.

Check policies. Hours, pricing, and rules change. Call ahead or check websites before visiting.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is better for families?

Mount Princeton—more space, more pools, more family-friendly facilities. Cottonwood is quieter and more adult-oriented.

Are they open year-round?

Yes, both operate year-round. Winter soaking with snow falling is a memorable experience.

Can I visit without staying overnight?

Yes, both offer day passes for pool access. Reservations may be required at busy times.

Is Cottonwood really clothing-optional?

During certain hours in certain areas, yes. Check their current policies. Most times, swimsuits are required.

How hot are the pools?

Temperatures range from about 95°F to 130°F+ at the source. Different pools offer different temperatures.
