Buena Vista, Colorado

River Running Capital

Your Guide to Buena Vista

Rafting, peaks, and Arkansas Valley adventure

Buena Vista—locals say "BEW-nuh Vista"—sits in the Arkansas River Valley surrounded by Colorado's highest peaks. The Arkansas River runs through town, making this the epicenter of Colorado whitewater rafting with rapids for beginners through experts.

The Collegiate Peaks Wilderness rises to the west, with more 14ers accessible from here than anywhere in Colorado. Mount Princeton Hot Springs and Cottonwood Hot Springs offer natural soaking after a day of adventure.

Downtown has grown into a destination with breweries, restaurants, and outdoor shops. The town attracts climbers, rafters, and mountain bikers seeking adventure without resort prices. It's authentic Colorado—active, unpretentious, and surrounded by mountains.

Explore Buena Vista

Rivers, peaks, and hot springs

RAFT

Arkansas River Rafting

Brown's Canyon and Numbers sections offer world-class whitewater. Multiple outfitters run trips from family floats to Class IV rapids.

PEAK

Collegiate Peaks

Eight 14ers within range including Mount Princeton, Yale, and Harvard. Trailheads for some of Colorado's best high-altitude hiking.

SPRINGS

Hot Springs

Mount Princeton and Cottonwood hot springs offer natural soaking. Perfect recovery after rafting or hiking.

BIKE

Mountain Biking

Midland Hill, Whipple Trail, and extensive singletrack network. Growing trail system for all skill levels.

TOWN

Downtown

Local breweries, restaurants, and outdoor shops. Eddyline Brewery and others serve après-adventure crowds.

FISH

Fishing

Arkansas River gold-medal waters. Excellent trout fishing with guided and walk-wade options.

Frequently Asked Questions

Planning your Buena Vista visit

How far is Buena Vista from Denver?

About 115 miles, roughly 2.5 hours via Highway 285 through South Park. Scenic drive through mountain valleys.

When is the best time for rafting?

May through August. Peak flows in June offer the biggest rapids. July and August are warmer with mellower water—better for families.

How do you pronounce Buena Vista?

Locals say "BEW-nuh Vista," not the Spanish pronunciation. It's a shibboleth—say it wrong and they'll know you're a tourist.

Which hot springs should I visit?

Mount Princeton is larger and more developed with a resort. Cottonwood is smaller and more rustic. Both are excellent—different vibes.
