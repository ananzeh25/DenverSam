Mountain Biking Buena Vista

Quick Info

Main areas: Midland Hill, Whipple Trail, Fourmile area. Character: High-desert singletrack and mountain trails. Season: April-October (dry conditions). Growing scene: Trail network expanding.

Buena Vista's mountain biking scene has grown significantly in recent years. The high-desert terrain offers dry conditions and long seasons. Trails range from flowy singletrack to technical challenges, with stunning views of the Collegiate Peaks throughout.

The community has invested in trail development, creating loops and connections that rival more famous mountain bike destinations. It's less crowded than Front Range trails and offers a distinctly Arkansas Valley riding experience.

Trail Areas

Midland Hill

The most accessible trail system, located right in town. Multiple interconnected trails offer options for all skill levels. Good for quick rides and after-work sessions.

Trails: Midland Trail, various connector loops.

Character: Flowy singletrack, some technical sections. Views of town and mountains.

Best for: Intermediate riders, local laps.

Whipple Trail

Classic Buena Vista singletrack with excellent views and varied terrain. One of the most popular rides in the area.

Length: Approximately 10 miles with various routes.

Character: Climbing, descending, and everything in between. High-desert terrain.

Best for: Intermediate to advanced riders.

Fourmile Recreation Area

Network of trails north of town with options ranging from beginner to advanced. Good trail signage and well-maintained.

Character: Mix of terrain types. Some machine-built flow trails.

Best for: All levels, depending on trail choice.

Cottonwood Area

Higher elevation trails toward the Collegiate Peaks. More technical, more climbing, more reward.

Character: Mountain terrain, alpine views.

Best for: Strong climbers, adventure rides.

Bike Shops & Rentals

Several shops in town offer bike rentals, repairs, and local knowledge. They can point you to the best trails for your skill level and current conditions.

Rentals: Full-suspension mountain bikes, hardtails, e-bikes available.

Local knowledge: Shop staff ride these trails regularly. Ask for recommendations.

Tips

Check conditions. Trails can be muddy after rain. High-desert means fast drying, but give wet trails time.

Sun protection. High altitude and open terrain mean intense sun. Sunscreen, glasses, and hydration are essential.

Afternoon storms. Summer thunderstorms are common. Start early for big rides.

Combine with other activities. Morning ride, afternoon raft trip, evening hot springs—the perfect Buena Vista day.

Respect the trails. This growing system depends on community support. Follow posted rules and yield appropriately.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does Buena Vista compare to Salida for biking?

Salida has more established trails and the famous Monarch Crest ride. Buena Vista's scene is growing and offers a different feel. Try both.

What's the best trail for beginners?

Midland Hill has beginner-friendly options. Ask at a local shop for current recommendations based on conditions.

Is it worth bringing my bike?

If you're serious about riding, yes. If you just want to try it, rentals are available. The trails are worth riding.

When's the best time to ride?

Late spring through fall. June-September is prime. Early mornings are best in summer to avoid heat and storms.

Are e-bikes allowed?

Policies vary by trail. Check local regulations. Some trails welcome e-bikes, others restrict them.
