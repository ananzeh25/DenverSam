Central Park Boulder: Playground, Splash Pad & Creek Access

What is Central Park Boulder? A 10-acre community park near downtown with a large playground, splash pad (summer), sports courts, and Boulder Creek access. Popular with families. Band shell hosts free concerts. Walking distance from Pearl Street. Free entry with on-street parking.

Overview

Location: 1236 Canyon Blvd, Boulder
Size: 10 acres
Entry Fee: Free
Hours: 5am-11pm

Central Park is Boulder's go-to family park. The large playground keeps kids entertained while parents relax on the grass. The splash pad is a summer essential. Boulder Creek runs along the south edge, offering tubing access and creek play. Location near downtown makes it easy to combine with Pearl Street visits.

Features

Playground

Large, modern playground suitable for ages 2-12. Climbing structures, slides, swings, and a sand play area. Shaded by mature trees. Usually busy on weekend afternoons.

Splash Pad

Free water play area open Memorial Day through Labor Day. Ground-level fountains and sprayers—no standing water. Perfect for toddlers and young children. No swimsuits required but recommended.

Boulder Creek Access

The park borders Boulder Creek with easy access points for wading and tubing. Summer water levels allow casual floating through town on inner tubes. Rent tubes at local shops or bring your own. Water is cold—mountain runoff.

Band Shell

The band shell hosts free concerts throughout summer, including the popular Bands on the Bricks series that sometimes relocates here. Check Boulder Parks & Rec schedule for events.

Sports Facilities

Basketball courts, tennis courts, sand volleyball, and open grass areas for pickup games. First-come, first-served.

Facilities

  • Public restrooms
  • Drinking fountains
  • Picnic tables and benches
  • Bike racks
  • Boulder Creek Path access
  • On-street parking (free, limited) and nearby paid lots

Tips

Parking: On-street parking fills quickly on nice days. Try the parking garage at 15th and Spruce (paid) or bike from wherever you're staying.

Creek safety: Boulder Creek can be swift and cold, especially in spring. Supervise children closely. Check water conditions before tubing.

Combine trips: Walk to Pearl Street Mall (5 minutes), Dushanbe Teahouse (adjacent), or Boulder Public Library.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there a fee for the splash pad?

No, the splash pad is free and open to everyone during summer months.

Can I tube Boulder Creek from Central Park?

Yes. Central Park is a popular put-in spot. Float downstream toward Eben G. Fine Park. Check water levels before going.

Are dogs allowed?

Yes, but must be on leash. For off-leash play, try Foothills Dog Park or Valmont Dog Park.
