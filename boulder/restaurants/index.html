Boulder Restaurants

Farm-to-table pioneer, fine dining destination

Boulder punches above its weight in dining. The city helped pioneer the farm-to-table movement, and that ethos—local, seasonal, sustainable—runs through nearly every kitchen. From James Beard-nominated fine dining to beloved local haunts, here are the essential Boulder restaurants.

Frasca Food and Wine

James Beard-Winning Italian

Friuli-Venezia Giulia cuisine with one of America's best wine programs. Boulder's crown jewel of fine dining.

$$$$ Fine Dining

The Kitchen

Farm-to-Table Pioneer

The restaurant that helped define Boulder's farm-to-table ethos. Community tables, local ingredients, perfect execution.

$$$ American

Flagstaff House

Mountaintop Fine Dining

Elegant dining on Flagstaff Mountain with panoramic Boulder views. Special occasion destination since 1971.

$$$$ Fine Dining

Oak at Fourteenth

Modern American on Pearl

Wood-fired cooking with seasonal focus. The bar scene is excellent, and the patio is prime Pearl Street real estate.

$$$ American

Blackbelly Market

Whole Animal Butcher & Kitchen

In-house butchery meets refined cooking. Chef Hosea Rosenberg's meat-forward menu honors the whole animal.

$$$ Steakhouse

Dushanbe Teahouse

Tajik Art & Global Cuisine

Hand-carved teahouse gifted by Boulder's sister city. Persian-inspired food in an architectural wonder.

$$ Global

The Mediterranean

Pearl Street Institution

Boulder's best rooftop and 30 years of consistent Mediterranean fare. Tapas, paella, and excellent cocktails.

$$ Mediterranean

Jax Fish House

Rocky Mountain Oyster Bar

Fresh oysters and seafood in landlocked Colorado. Excellent happy hour and the best raw bar in Boulder.

$$$ Seafood

Leaf Vegetarian

Plant-Based Excellence

Boulder's best vegetarian restaurant. Creative plant-based dishes that satisfy meat-eaters too.

$$ Vegetarian

Illegal Pete's

Boulder Burrito Institution

The Colorado burrito chain that started here. Mission-style burritos, good beer, and community vibes.

$ Mexican
