Howard Heuston Dog Park: Quiet Neighborhood Spot

Howard Heuston Dog Park at a glance: A smaller, neighborhood dog park within Martin Park. Less crowded than Foothills or East Boulder. Good shade from mature trees. Ideal for dogs who get overwhelmed at larger parks or owners wanting a mellower vibe. South Boulder location.

Overview

Location: Martin Park, 36th Street & Dartmouth Ave, Boulder
Size: ~1 acre
Hours: 5am-11pm
Entry Fee: Free

Howard Heuston is Boulder's "local's secret" dog park. While the bigger parks draw crowds, this smaller spot stays relatively quiet. It's perfect for dogs who prefer one or two playmates over a pack, and for owners who want conversation over chaos.

Features

  • Fenced area: Fully enclosed off-leash space
  • Mature trees: Natural shade throughout the park
  • Grass surface: Easier on paws than gravel or dirt
  • Benches: Seating for owners
  • Waste stations: Bags and trash cans

The Vibe

This is a neighborhood park with neighborhood regulars. You'll see the same dogs and owners regularly. The smaller size and quieter atmosphere attracts people who aren't looking for dog park mayhem. Good for shy dogs, senior dogs, or dogs who get overstimulated easily.

Considerations

No separate small dog area: Unlike the larger parks, there's no dedicated space for small dogs. Visit during off-peak times if you have a small or timid dog.

Smaller size: Not ideal for high-energy dogs who need lots of running room. Consider East Boulder for marathon fetchers.

Parking: Street parking only. The neighborhood is residential, so please be respectful.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Howard Heuston Dog Park fenced?

Yes, it's fully fenced with a secure entrance.

Is there a small dog area?

No, unlike larger Boulder dog parks, there's no separate small dog section.
