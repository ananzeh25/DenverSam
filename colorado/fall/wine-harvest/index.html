Wine Harvest

Colorado wine country in fall

Crush Season

When Palisade celebrates the harvest

Colorado wine country is real, and it's centered in Palisade on the Western Slope. This high-desert region near Grand Junction produces award-winning wines from grapes grown at elevation. Fall harvest season brings crush festivals, grape stomping, and the best time to visit.

Harvest typically runs mid-August through October, depending on the variety. The Colorado Mountain Winefest in September is the signature event - two days of tastings, grape stomping, food, and live music. Individual wineries host their own crush events throughout the season.

The Palisade area has over 20 wineries within a few miles, making it easy to taste your way through the region. The combination of warm days, cool nights, and intense sunshine creates conditions that produce surprisingly excellent wines - a secret that's slowly getting out.

Fall Wine Events

FEST

Colorado Mountain Winefest

September weekend celebration. Grand tastings, grape stomp, food pairings. Multiple venues in Palisade. The signature Colorado wine event.

HARVEST

Harvest Parties

Individual wineries host release parties and harvest celebrations. Special tastings, new vintages, and behind-the-scenes tours.

STOMP

Grape Stomping

Get in the barrels and stomp grapes the old-fashioned way. Fun photo ops and a unique harvest experience. Several wineries offer this.

TOUR

Harvest Tours

See the winemaking process in action. Crushing, pressing, and fermentation happening in real time. Educational and delicious.

Wine Country Tips

STAY

Book Ahead

Winefest weekend sells out hotels in the area. Book months in advance for September visits. Grand Junction has more options.

DD

Designate a Driver

Wine tours require someone to stay sober. Consider a tour company with transportation. Uber/Lyft limited in rural areas.

PEACH

Try the Peaches

Palisade is famous for peaches too. Late summer into early fall has fresh fruit. Orchards and fruit stands everywhere.

SHIP

Ship Wine Home

Most wineries can ship. Colorado wines are hard to find out of state. Stock up and have it delivered. Check your state's shipping laws.
