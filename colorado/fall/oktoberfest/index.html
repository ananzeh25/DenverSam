Colorado Oktoberfest

Prost to fall!

Bavarian Traditions in the Rockies

German heritage meets mountain culture

Colorado's strong German heritage and thriving craft beer culture make it natural Oktoberfest territory. From late September through October, festivals across the state celebrate with German beer, food, music, and dancing - often with Rocky Mountain backdrops.

Denver's Oktoberfest on Larimer Street draws tens of thousands for a weekend of oompah bands, stein-hoisting contests, keg bowling, and all the bratwurst you can eat. Mountain towns like Breckenridge, Vail, and Steamboat host their own celebrations with alpine village atmospheres.

Major Celebrations

DENVER

Denver Oktoberfest

Larimer Street between 20th and 22nd. Two weekends in September. Largest celebration in the state. Free admission.

BRECK

Breckenridge

Weekend celebration on Main Street. German food, beer, and music in a historic mountain town setting.

VAIL

Vail

European-style village hosts authentic celebration. Lederhosen, dirndls, and Alpine atmosphere.

STEAM

Steamboat

Laid-back mountain town celebration. Combines cowboy culture with Bavarian traditions.

What to Expect

BEER

Beer

German imports plus Colorado Oktoberfest-style brews. Liter steins, half-liters, and flights. Many include stein-hoisting contests.

FOOD

Food

Bratwurst, schnitzel, pretzels, sauerkraut, strudel. Traditional German fare from multiple vendors.

MUSIC

Entertainment

Oompah bands, polka dancing, contests, and games. Family-friendly during day, more party atmosphere at night.

DRESS

Attire

Lederhosen and dirndls encouraged but not required. Many attendees dress up. Rentals available in some locations.
