Fall Photography

Golden light, golden trees

Prime Photo Season

When Colorado is most photogenic

Colorado fall is a photographer's dream. The combination of golden aspen groves, dramatic mountain peaks, stable weather, and lower sun angles creates ideal conditions for landscape photography. Professionals book accommodations a year in advance to capture peak color.

The light is different in fall. The sun sits lower in the sky, creating warm golden tones even at midday. Morning and evening golden hours stretch longer. The air is often crystal clear, allowing sharp focus on distant peaks. Afternoon thunderstorms that plague summer shoots disappear.

Iconic Colorado images - Maroon Bells with golden aspens, Dallas Divide at sunrise, Crystal Mill in its canyon - are almost always captured in fall. If you're serious about landscape photography, late September should be blocked on your calendar.

Iconic Photo Locations

MAROON

Maroon Bells

Most photographed peaks in Colorado. Lake reflection with aspens. Shuttle required. Arrive before sunrise for the classic shot.

DALLAS

Dallas Divide

Sneffels Range with golden aspens in foreground. Between Ridgway and Telluride. Sunrise and sunset both work. The Colorado postcard shot.

CRYSTAL

Crystal Mill

Historic mill perched over Crystal River. High-clearance 4WD required. Best in morning light. October color is spectacular.

KEBLER

Kebler Pass

Largest aspen grove in Colorado. Endless compositions. Dirt road but passable. Late September peak. Less crowded than Maroon Bells.

Photography Tips

EARLY

Arrive Before Dawn

Popular spots require pre-sunrise arrival on peak weekends. Set up in darkness to capture first light. Headlamps essential.

TRIPOD

Bring a Tripod

Low light at sunrise/sunset requires stable support. Reflections need long exposures. Wind can be an issue - sturdy equipment helps.

FILTER

Polarizer Essential

Cuts glare on water for reflections. Deepens blue skies. Saturates fall colors. The most useful filter for Colorado fall.

REPORT

Check Color Reports

Peak varies by location and year. Color hotline and online reports help time your visit. One week can make the difference.
