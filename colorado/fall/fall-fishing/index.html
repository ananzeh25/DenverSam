Fall Fishing

When big browns get aggressive

Prime Brown Trout Season

October's territorial fish

Fall transforms Colorado fishing. Brown trout spawn in October and November, becoming territorial and aggressive. These normally cautious fish suddenly attack streamers and chase smaller fish from their redds. It's the time of year when trophy browns are most catchable.

The spawn brings browns from deep pools and undercut banks where they hide all summer. They move to gravelly shallows, becoming visible and accessible. Large fish that would never look at a fly in July will hammer a streamer in October. It's thrilling fishing.

Beyond the spawn, fall brings perfect fishing conditions. Crowds disappear after Labor Day. Rivers run at optimal levels - clear and cold. Blue-winged olive hatches bring fish to the surface on overcast days. Fewer anglers, active fish, and beautiful scenery make fall many anglers' favorite season.

Top Fall Waters

S PLATTE

South Platte

Multiple sections offer fall fishing. Deckers and Cheesman for technical dry fly. Dream Stream for spawning browns. Eleven Mile Canyon for variety.

ARK

Arkansas River

100+ miles of Gold Medal water. Big browns on the move. Salida and Buena Vista sections fish well. October streamer fishing is outstanding.

ROARING

Roaring Fork

Aspen-area river with fall colors bonus. Brown trout move upstream to spawn. Scenic setting during peak foliage. Combine fishing with leaf peeping.

BLUE

Blue River

Tailwater below Dillon holds big fish. Fall kokanee salmon run attracts big browns. Pink/orange patterns imitate salmon eggs.

Fall Fishing Tips

STREAMER

Think Streamers

Aggressive browns eat big flies. Woolly Buggers, sculpin patterns, and articulated streamers. Strip aggressively to trigger strikes.

BWO

Watch for BWOs

Blue-winged olives hatch on cloudy fall days. Small dry flies (size 18-22) can produce excellent dry fly fishing. Best on overcast afternoons.

RESPECT

Respect Spawning Fish

Avoid walking on redds (gravel nests). Handle fish carefully for quick release. Consider skipping obvious spawning areas. Protect the resource.

LAYER

Dress Warm

Fall mornings are cold. Waders with insulation layers. Fingerless gloves help with cold hands. Conditions change quickly - bring extra layers.
