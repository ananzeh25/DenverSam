Great American Beer Festival

The Olympics of American Brewing

America's Premier Beer Event

The largest beer festival in the United States

The Great American Beer Festival is the Super Bowl of craft beer. Every October, over 800 breweries descend on the Colorado Convention Center to pour 4,000+ different beers for 60,000 attendees across three days. Tickets sell out in minutes when they go on sale.

But GABF is more than just a drinking event. It's home to the most prestigious brewing competition in America. Medals awarded here can make or break a brewery's reputation. Judges evaluate thousands of entries across nearly 100 style categories, and winners display their medals with pride for years.

The festival has been held in Denver since 1982, making the city the undisputed capital of American craft brewing culture. The event pumps millions into the local economy and fills hotels throughout the metro area. It's Colorado's beer industry at its most celebratory.

Festival Experience

POUR

Unlimited Sampling

One-ounce pours from 800+ breweries. Sample across the hall or focus on specific regions/styles. Plan your strategy.

MEDAL

Competition

Gold, silver, bronze medals in ~100 categories. Results announced Saturday. Look for "medal winner" signs at booths.

MEET

Meet Brewers

Brewers pour their own beer - talk directly with the people who made it. Ask questions, learn stories, geek out.

FOOD

Food & More

Food vendors throughout. Brewing industry exhibits. Limited edition releases only available at the festival.

Getting Tickets

SELL

Ticket Sale

Tickets go on sale in August and sell out in minutes - sometimes seconds. Set alarms and be ready. No day-of sales.

AHA

Member Presale

American Homebrewers Association members get presale access. Best way to guarantee tickets. Join AHA before tickets drop.

SESSION

Sessions

Multiple 4-hour sessions Thu-Sat. Thursday evening is members-only. Saturday afternoon most competitive for tickets.

HOTEL

Accommodations

Book hotels early - Denver fills up. Stay downtown to walk to/from the convention center. Don't drive after the festival.
