Dog Sledding in Colorado

Mush through mountain wilderness

Bucket-List Winter Adventure

Experience the thrill of mushing

There's nothing quite like the sound of eight eager huskies pulling you through silent, snow-covered wilderness. Dog sledding in Colorado offers an unforgettable connection to winter, history, and these incredible working dogs.

Several outfitters across the Colorado mountains offer dog sledding experiences. Most tours let you choose between riding in the sled basket or learning to drive the team yourself. Half-day tours cover 10-15 miles through national forest land, while full-day adventures venture deeper into the backcountry.

The dogs are the stars - well-trained Siberian and Alaskan huskies bred for this work. You'll learn about dog care, commands, and the history of mushing. Many tours include time to meet and photograph the dogs before and after the run.

Where to Go Dog Sledding

BRECK

Breckenridge Area

Good Times Adventures and other outfitters offer tours through national forest near Breckenridge and Frisco.

VAIL

Vail Valley

Nova Guides and others run tours through scenic backcountry. Some include lunch at remote cabins.

STEAM

Steamboat

Grizzle-T Dog & Sled Works offers authentic backcountry tours. Smaller operation with personal attention.

SNOW

Snowmass

Krabloonik has been operating since 1970. Largest operation in Colorado with 300+ dogs. Includes gourmet lunch option.

What to Know

COST

Pricing

Half-day tours run $200-400 per person. Full-day with lunch can exceed $500. Book in advance - popular tours sell out.

WEAR

What to Wear

Dress very warmly - you're standing still on a sled. Most outfitters provide suits, boots, and gloves if needed.

KIDS

Family Friendly

Most tours welcome kids (usually 4+). Children ride in the sled basket. Great family adventure and memory maker.

DRIVE

Drive or Learn

Most tours let you choose: ride as a passenger or learn to mush. Driving adds to the experience - try it if offered.
