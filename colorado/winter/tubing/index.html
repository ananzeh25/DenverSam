Snow Tubing in Colorado

No skill required - just hold on

The Easiest Winter Fun

Perfect for families and groups

Snow tubing delivers maximum winter fun with zero learning curve. Sit in an inflated tube, push off, and scream down a groomed snow lane. Magic carpets carry you back up the hill, so there's no hiking involved. It's the perfect activity for families, groups, or anyone who wants mountain fun without the ski lessons.

Colorado's tubing parks range from massive resort operations to local hills. Some feature lanes designed for speed, others for spinning, and many are lit for night tubing. Most allow riders as young as 3-4 years old, making this one of the few winter activities the whole family can enjoy together.

Reservations are essential at popular parks - they sell out on weekends and holidays. Sessions typically last 1-2 hours. Dress warmly but expect to warm up fast from the excitement.

Best Tubing Parks

KEY

Keystone

Adventure Point tubing at 11,640 feet. Six lanes with magic carpet. Day and night sessions available. Stunning summit views.

FRISCO

Frisco Adventure Park

Multiple lanes, beginner hill for small kids, and night tubing on Fridays. Most affordable option near Breckenridge.

ADVENT

Colorado Adventure Park

Fraser Valley location (near Winter Park). Long runs and fast speeds. Includes mini snowmobiles for kids.

ECHO

Echo Mountain

Closest tubing to Denver (45 minutes). Small ski area with tubing hill. Great for quick day trips.

Tips for Your Visit

BOOK

Reservations

Book online in advance - popular parks sell out. Weekday sessions less crowded. Night tubing requires separate booking.

WEAR

What to Wear

Waterproof pants essential - you're sitting in snow. Warm jacket, gloves, hat. Goggles optional but fun.

KIDS

Age Requirements

Most parks allow ages 3+ with an adult. Some lanes have height/age minimums. Check specific park policies.

TIME

How Long

Sessions run 1-2 hours. That's usually plenty - you'll be ready for hot cocoa. Allow time for parking and check-in.
