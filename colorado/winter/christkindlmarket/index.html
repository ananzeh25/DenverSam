Christkindlmarket

Denver's German Christmas Market

Old World Holiday Tradition

A taste of Germany in the Mile High City

Denver's Christkindlmarket brings authentic German Christmas market tradition to Civic Center Park. Rows of wooden vendor huts sell handcrafted ornaments, nutcrackers, cuckoo clocks, and European gifts you won't find anywhere else in Colorado.

The food is as authentic as the crafts. Warm up with gluhwein (mulled wine) or hot cocoa. Fill up on bratwurst, schnitzel, strudel, and fresh-baked lebkuchen. The smell of roasted almonds and spiced wine fills the cold air.

Live entertainment includes German music, alpine horn players, and traditional dancers. The market runs from mid-November through December 23rd, making it a perfect holiday tradition for locals and visitors alike.

What to Know

FREE

Admission

Free to enter. Bring cash for vendors and food - some booths are cash-only. ATMs available on site.

TIME

Hours

Open daily from 11 AM. Weekday evenings are less crowded than weekends. Most magical after dark when lights glow.

FOOD

Must-Try Foods

Gluhwein in a collectible mug, bratwurst, potato pancakes, apple strudel, and roasted almonds. Come hungry.

SHOP

Shopping

German ornaments, nutcrackers, handmade toys, candles, and unique gifts. Many items imported directly from Germany.
