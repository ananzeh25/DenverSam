Snowshoeing in Colorado

Explore winter wilderness on foot

Walk Through Winter Wonderland

No experience necessary

Snowshoeing is the easiest way to explore Colorado's winter backcountry. If you can walk, you can snowshoe. Strap on a pair and suddenly you have access to quiet forest trails, frozen meadows, and mountain vistas that few visitors ever see.

Colorado offers snowshoeing for every ability level. Groomed trails at Nordic centers make for easy walking. Rocky Mountain National Park has ranger-led snowshoe walks. Mountain towns like Breckenridge and Vail offer miles of forest trails. Guided tours can take you to backcountry cabins for overnight adventures.

The experience is different from skiing - quieter, slower, more contemplative. You'll hear birds, spot animal tracks, and find solitude even on busy winter weekends. Many people find it meditative, a chance to truly experience the mountain winter.

Top Snowshoeing Destinations

RMNP

Rocky Mountain NP

Ranger-led snowshoe walks, Bear Lake area trails, and backcountry exploration. Free snowshoe loans available on some programs.

BRECK

Breckenridge

Nordic center trails and forest paths. Guided moonlight tours. Equipment rentals on Main Street.

VAIL

Vail

Extensive trail network through aspen groves. Walking Mountains Science Center offers guided naturalist tours.

GOLDEN

Golden Gate Canyon

State park just 45 minutes from Denver. 35+ miles of trails through pine forests. Easiest access from the Front Range.

Getting Started

RENT

Rentals

Rent snowshoes at outdoor shops in mountain towns or REI in Denver. About $15-25/day. Poles recommended for stability.

WEAR

What to Wear

Waterproof boots, warm layers, and gaiters to keep snow out. Dress lighter than you think - you'll warm up quickly.

GUIDE

Guided Tours

Available throughout the mountains. Learn about wildlife, safety, and hidden trails. Some include hot cocoa or yurt lunch.

SAFE

Safety

Stay on marked trails. Watch for avalanche terrain in backcountry. Tell someone your plans. Carry water and snacks.
