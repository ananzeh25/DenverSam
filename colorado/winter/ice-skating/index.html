Ice Skating in Colorado

Skate with mountain views

Outdoor Winter Magic

From frozen lakes to village rinks

Colorado offers some of the most scenic ice skating in the country. Picture gliding across a frozen lake with snow-capped mountains rising in the distance, or skating at a village rink while snow falls gently around you. It's winter magic that belongs in a postcard.

Options range from natural frozen lakes to groomed resort rinks. Evergreen Lake near Denver is the iconic choice - a natural lake that freezes solid each winter. Keystone's lakeside rink is one of the largest outdoor rinks in the country. Mountain village rinks in Vail and Beaver Creek offer European atmosphere.

Denver has urban options too, from the downtown rink at Skyline Park to the Southwest Rink at Civic Center. These make for easy evening outings with holiday lights and hot cocoa nearby.

Best Skating Spots

KEY

Keystone Lake

5-acre rink at Keystone Resort - one of the largest outdoor rinks in North America. Hockey, broomball, and family skating sessions.

LAKE

Evergreen Lake

Natural frozen lake 45 minutes from Denver. Warming house, skate rentals, and hot cocoa. Classic Colorado skating since 1960s.

VAIL

Vail & Beaver Creek

Village rinks with European atmosphere. Surrounded by shops and restaurants. Smaller but magical setting.

DEN

Downtown Denver

Skyline Park rink downtown. Smaller but convenient. Holiday lights, city views, and walkable to restaurants.

Skating Tips

RENT

Rentals

All major rinks offer skate rentals ($5-15). Bring thick socks. Hockey and figure skates usually available.

WEAR

What to Wear

Warm layers, gloves essential, helmet recommended for beginners and kids. Dress warmer than you think - standing on ice gets cold.

TIME

Best Time

Weekday afternoons are least crowded. Evening skating under lights is magical. Check conditions - warm spells can close rinks.

SAFE

Safety

Stick to marked rinks - don't skate on random frozen ponds. Natural lakes are monitored but conditions vary.
