Ice Castles

Dillon, Colorado

A Frozen Fairytale

Hand-crafted ice architecture in the Rockies

Every winter, artists build a massive frozen kingdom on the shores of Dillon Reservoir. Ice Castles features towering frozen spires, winding tunnels, ice slides, fountains, and caverns - all constructed from hundreds of thousands of icicles grown and harvested by hand.

The experience transforms after dark when embedded LED lights illuminate the ice in shifting colors. Walking through the frozen caverns at night, surrounded by blue and purple glowing ice, feels like stepping into another world.

The castle typically opens in late December or January and runs through late February or March, weather dependent. Cold temperatures keep the ice solid; warm spells can close the attraction temporarily or end the season early.

Planning Your Visit

TICKET

Tickets

Advance purchase required - walk-ups rarely available. Weeknight tickets are cheaper and less crowded. Book at icecastles.com.

TIME

Best Time

Visit at dusk to see it in daylight and lit up. Weeknights are less crowded. Allow 1-2 hours to explore.

WEAR

What to Wear

Dress warmly - you're walking through ice at 9,000 feet elevation. Waterproof boots essential. Gloves and hat required.

DRIVE

Getting There

Located in Dillon, about 70 miles west of Denver via I-70. Easy to combine with skiing at nearby Keystone or Breckenridge.
