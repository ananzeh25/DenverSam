Iron Mountain Hot Springs

Modern pools on the Colorado River

Glenwood's Newer Option

A different vibe from the historic pool

Iron Mountain Hot Springs opened in 2015, offering Glenwood Springs a second hot springs option with a completely different character. Where Glenwood Hot Springs Pool is massive and historic, Iron Mountain is intimate and modern. Sixteen smaller pools cascade along the Colorado River, each maintained at different temperatures.

The design emphasizes the natural setting. Pools are built into the riverbank with views across the water to red rock cliffs. The facility maintains a quieter, more relaxed atmosphere - no water slides, no splashing crowds. It's designed for soaking and conversation, not swimming laps.

The mineral water here is different too. Iron Mountain uses freshwater geothermal springs rather than the sulfur-tinged water common elsewhere. The result is clean-feeling water that won't leave a lingering smell on your skin or swimsuit.

RIVER

River Views

Pools sit right on the Colorado River. Watch rafters float by in summer. Winter brings snow-covered banks and peaceful quiet.

FAMILY

Family Friendly

Kids welcome in most pools. Family pool area available. Calmer than big pool facilities. Good for families wanting relaxation.

JETS

Jetted Pool

One large jetted pool for those wanting hydrotherapy. Stronger water pressure for sore muscles. Popular after skiing or hiking.

Planning Your Visit

RESERVE

Reservations

Reservations recommended, especially weekends. Book online in advance. Limited capacity keeps crowds manageable.

TIME

Time Slots

Entry is by time slot. 2-hour sessions standard. Sunset slots popular in winter. Morning slots usually less crowded.

DRINK

Poolside Service

Drinks available poolside. Local beer and wine. Sip something warm while you soak. Nice amenity other springs lack.

COMPARE

Two in One Trip

Visit both Glenwood springs in one trip. Iron Mountain for relaxation, big pool for activity. Different experiences, same town.
