Pagosa Hot Springs

World's deepest geothermal source

The Mother Spring

Geothermal power from over 1,000 feet deep

Pagosa Springs sits atop one of the world's most powerful geothermal sources. The "Mother Spring" pumps water from over 1,000 feet below at temperatures exceeding 140°F - so hot it must be cooled before soaking. The Guinness Book of World Records has certified it as the world's deepest geothermal hot spring.

The town of Pagosa Springs lies in southwest Colorado's San Juan Mountains, about 60 miles east of Durango. It's a destination that rewards the journey - stunning mountain scenery, a charming downtown, nearby Wolf Creek ski area, and multiple hot springs options make it worth the drive.

Several facilities tap into the geothermal bounty. The Springs Resort dominates the scene with 23 pools cascading along the San Juan River. Healing Waters Resort offers a more intimate experience. Each provides a different atmosphere, from luxury resort to rustic retreat.

Hot Springs Options

SPRINGS

The Springs Resort

23 soaking pools from 83-114°F. Terraced along the San Juan River. The main destination with full resort amenities. Day passes available.

HEALING

Healing Waters

More affordable alternative. Outdoor pools and private baths. Less crowded, more casual atmosphere. Good budget option.

OVERLOOK

Overlook Hot Springs

Rooftop pools with mountain views. Adults-only atmosphere. Smaller and more intimate. Different vibe from river-level soaking.

RIVER

Riverside Experience

Pools at The Springs sit right on the San Juan. Watch the river flow past while you soak. Snow-covered banks in winter add magic.

Planning Your Visit

DRIVE

Getting There

About 5 hours from Denver via US-160. Scenic but long drive. Consider combining with Wolf Creek skiing or Durango visit.

SKI

Wolf Creek Nearby

Wolf Creek ski area is 25 miles away. Colorado's snowiest resort. Hot springs après-ski is the perfect combination.

STAY

Lodging

The Springs Resort has on-site rooms. Downtown Pagosa Springs offers hotels and vacation rentals. Book ahead in ski season.

TOWN

Explore Town

Charming downtown with restaurants and shops. Small-town mountain atmosphere. Less touristy than some Colorado destinations.
