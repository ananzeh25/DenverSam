Camping in Colorado

Sleep under mountain stars

Endless Camping Options

From luxury RV sites to wilderness solitude

Colorado offers camping experiences ranging from full-service RV parks to primitive backcountry sites where you won't see another soul. The state has hundreds of developed campgrounds in national forests, state parks, and national parks, plus millions of acres where dispersed camping is free and legal.

The challenge is access - popular campgrounds, especially near Rocky Mountain National Park and major trailheads, fill months in advance. Planning ahead is essential for summer weekends. But with flexibility, you can almost always find a spot somewhere in Colorado's vast public lands.

High-elevation camping stays cool even in summer, with nighttime temps dropping into the 40s. Wildlife encounters are common - store food properly and understand bear country protocols. And be ready for afternoon thunderstorms that build almost daily in July and August.

Types of Camping

CAMP

Developed Campgrounds

Designated sites with fire rings, tables, toilets. Many have potable water. Reserve via Recreation.gov for USFS/NPS sites.

FREE

Dispersed Camping

Free camping on national forest land. Find your own spot, bring everything you need. Follow Leave No Trace. 14-day limit.

STATE

State Parks

Well-maintained campgrounds with amenities. Reserve via CPW website. Day-use fee in addition to camping fee.

RV

RV Parks

Private campgrounds with hookups, showers, WiFi. Concentrated near tourist areas. More expensive but convenient.

Top Camping Destinations

RMNP

Rocky Mountain NP

Five campgrounds in the park. Reservations essential and released months ahead. Backcountry permits for wilderness camping.

MAROON

Maroon Bells Area

Campgrounds near Aspen with stunning scenery. Reserve early. Some first-come, first-served sites available.

TURQ

Turquoise Lake

Near Leadville at 9,900 feet. Multiple campgrounds around a beautiful reservoir. High elevation keeps it cool.
