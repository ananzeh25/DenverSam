Taste of Colorado

Denver's Biggest Food Festival

Labor Day Weekend Tradition

Food, music, and end-of-summer celebration

Every Labor Day weekend, Taste of Colorado transforms Civic Center Park into Colorado's largest food festival. Over 500,000 people descend on downtown Denver across four days to sample from 50+ restaurants, enjoy live music on multiple stages, and celebrate the unofficial end of summer.

The festival showcases the diversity of Colorado's culinary scene. From classic state fair foods to upscale restaurant tastings, food trucks to ethnic cuisines, there's something for every palate. Many restaurants offer sample-sized portions so you can taste widely without filling up on one dish.

Beyond food, the event features national and local music acts performing continuously, art vendors, family activities, and a carnival area. Admission to the festival grounds is free - you pay for food and drinks as you go.

Festival Highlights

FOOD

Restaurant Row

50+ restaurants serving sample portions and full dishes. Colorado favorites plus diverse cuisines. Come hungry.

MUSIC

Live Music

Multiple stages with continuous entertainment. National headliners and local bands. All music free with festival admission.

BEER

Craft Beer

Colorado breweries well represented. Beer gardens throughout the grounds. Wine, spirits, and cocktails also available.

FAMILY

Family Fun

Kids zone with activities and entertainment. Carnival rides. Face painting and games. Fun for all ages.

Tips for Attending

FREE

Admission

Free to enter festival grounds. Bring cash for faster food purchases, though most vendors accept cards.

PARK

Getting There

Light rail recommended - Civic Center station is steps away. Parking downtown is expensive and limited.

TIME

Best Time

Weekday lunch for smaller crowds. Evening for best music. Sunday most crowded. Four days to choose from.

SUN

Weather

Late August/early September typically warm. Sunscreen, hat, and water recommended. Afternoon storms possible.
