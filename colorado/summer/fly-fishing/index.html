Fly Fishing

Gold Medal waters at every turn

Trout Heaven

Some of America's finest fly fishing

Colorado boasts over 9,000 miles of trout streams and 2,000 lakes, including some of the finest fly fishing in North America. The state's "Gold Medal" designation identifies waters with exceptional trout populations - and Colorado has more Gold Medal waters than anywhere else.

Summer brings prime conditions after spring runoff subsides. Rivers clear, flows stabilize, and prolific insect hatches bring trout to the surface. Morning and evening are magic hours - PMDs, caddis, and stoneflies trigger aggressive feeding. Midday dry fly fishing with hoppers and other terrestrials can be equally productive.

From technical tailwaters requiring tiny flies and light tippets to freestone streams where aggressive fish take big dries, Colorado offers every type of fly fishing experience. Many anglers plan entire vacations around the state's legendary waters.

Gold Medal Waters

S PLATTE

South Platte River

Multiple Gold Medal sections from Denver to the mountains. Cheesman Canyon is legendary. Technical fishing for large, wary trout.

FRYING

Frying Pan River

Famous tailwater near Basalt. Year-round fishing, incredible hatches. Trophy rainbow and brown trout. One of America's best.

ARK

Arkansas River

102 miles of Gold Medal water. Brown trout heaven. Diverse access from Leadville to Canon City. Salida is a fishing town hub.

GUNNISON

Gunnison River

Wild western river. Black Canyon section is bucket-list water. Large fish, dramatic scenery. Less crowded than Front Range waters.

Summer Fly Fishing Tips

HATCH

Match the Hatch

PMDs, caddis, and stoneflies dominate summer. Terrestrials (hoppers, ants, beetles) work midday. Local fly shops know what's working.

TIME

Best Times

Morning and evening produce best dry fly action. Midday fish often go deep. Nymphing can work anytime. Adjust tactics to conditions.

GUIDE

Hire a Guide

Local guides know current conditions, hatches, and access points. Worth it for unfamiliar waters. Many offer half and full day trips.

LICENSE

License & Regulations

Colorado fishing license required. Know the regulations - many waters are catch and release, artificial only. Check before you fish.
