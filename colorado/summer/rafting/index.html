Colorado Whitewater Rafting

Ride the Rocky Mountain snowmelt

World-Class Whitewater

Rivers for every skill level

Colorado's rivers run with Rocky Mountain snowmelt, creating some of the best whitewater rafting in the country. From gentle family floats to adrenaline-pumping Class IV+ rapids, there's a river experience for everyone from first-timers to seasoned thrill-seekers.

Peak season runs May through August, with highest water typically in late May and June. The Arkansas River near Buena Vista and Salida is Colorado's most popular rafting destination, offering everything from calm Browns Canyon floats to the intense Royal Gorge. The Colorado River near Glenwood Springs and Clear Creek close to Denver provide additional options.

Dozens of outfitters offer guided trips ranging from two hours to full-day adventures and multi-day expeditions. No experience is necessary for most trips - guides handle the navigation while you paddle and enjoy the ride.

Top Rafting Rivers

ARK

Arkansas River

Colorado's #1 rafting destination. Browns Canyon (Class III) for families, Numbers/Royal Gorge (Class IV+) for adventure.

COLO

Colorado River

Shoshone section near Glenwood Springs - Class III excitement with easy access. Scenic Glenwood Canyon backdrop.

CLEAR

Clear Creek

Closest rafting to Denver (45 minutes). Class III-IV whitewater in Idaho Springs area. Great for day trips.

CACHE

Cache La Poudre

Near Fort Collins. Colorado's only Wild and Scenic River. Class II-IV sections through Poudre Canyon.

Planning Your Trip

WHEN

Best Time

Late May-June for highest water and biggest rapids. July-August warmer and more family-friendly. Season ends September.

BOOK

Reservations

Book popular dates weeks ahead, especially weekends. Many outfitters along Arkansas River. Compare prices and trip lengths.

WEAR

What to Wear

Swimsuit, water shoes, sunscreen. Outfitters provide helmet, life jacket, and wetsuit if needed. You will get wet.

KIDS

Age Requirements

Family trips typically allow ages 6+. More intense rapids require older teens/adults. Check with outfitter for specific policies.
