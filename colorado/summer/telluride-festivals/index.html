Telluride Festivals

World-class events in a box canyon

Festival Paradise

The most stunning venue in America

Telluride sits in a box canyon surrounded by 13,000-foot peaks, and somehow this remote mountain town has become one of America's premier festival destinations. From late spring through early fall, world-class music, film, and cultural events transform the historic mining town.

The setting is unmatched. Town Park, the main festival venue, sits at the end of Colorado Avenue with waterfalls cascading down the mountains behind the stage. The free gondola connects to Mountain Village above. The combination of intimate town, spectacular nature, and world-class programming creates magic.

Festival season defines Telluride's summer. Locals plan their calendars around these events. Book accommodation months in advance - the town's 400 hotel rooms fill instantly, and even camping spots go quickly. But for those who make the pilgrimage, the experience is unforgettable.

Major Festivals

GRASS

Bluegrass Festival

The original Telluride festival since 1974. Four days in late June. Legendary performances and jam sessions. Town Park camping creates community.

JAZZ

Jazz Festival

Three days in early August. Intimate performances by jazz legends. Multiple venues including free concerts in Town Park. Smaller, more refined.

FILM

Film Festival

Four days over Labor Day weekend. Premieres, documentaries, and filmmaker tributes. Star sightings in a mountain town. Essential for cinema lovers.

SHROOM

Mushroom Festival

Unique celebration of fungi. Expert-led foraging walks, cooking demonstrations, and lectures. August weekend attracting mycology enthusiasts worldwide.

Festival Tips

BOOK

Book Early

Lodging sells out months in advance. Festival passes often sell out too. Plan 6+ months ahead for the major events. Last-minute is nearly impossible.

CAMP

Consider Camping

Town Park camping is part of the Bluegrass experience. Other campgrounds in the area. Much more affordable than hotels. Bring warm gear - nights are cold.

DRIVE

Getting There

Six hours from Denver. Small airport with limited flights. Most drive. The journey through mountain passes is spectacular but long.

ALT

Altitude

Town sits at 8,750 feet. Drink water, take it easy the first day. Alcohol hits harder. Give yourself time to acclimate before dancing.
