Alpine scenery from your car

Roads Above the Clouds

When the high country finally opens

Summer unlocks Colorado's most spectacular scenic drives. Passes that remain closed under feet of snow all winter finally open, revealing alpine landscapes accessible only by car a few months each year. These roads offer some of the most dramatic scenery in North America.

Trail Ridge Road through Rocky Mountain National Park crests at 12,183 feet - the highest continuous paved road in the U.S. Independence Pass east of Aspen reaches 12,095 feet with dizzying switchbacks. The Million Dollar Highway between Silverton and Ouray offers cliffside drama.

These roads require respect. Weather changes fast at altitude - afternoon thunderstorms are common. Some passes have steep drop-offs with minimal guardrails. But the rewards are incomparable: wildflower meadows, alpine tundra, wildlife sightings, and views that stretch for a hundred miles.

Must-Drive Routes

TRAIL

Trail Ridge Road

48 miles through RMNP. Highest continuous paved road in U.S. Opens late May (weather dependent). Alpine visitors center at top.

INDEP

Independence Pass

Connects Aspen to Leadville over 12,095 feet. Dramatic switchbacks, alpine lakes, and ghost town remains. Opens Memorial Day.

MILL

Million Dollar Highway

US 550 between Silverton and Ouray. Jaw-dropping cliffs, mining history, three passes. Not for nervous drivers.

PEAK

Peak to Peak Highway

Easy access from Boulder through mountain towns to Estes Park. Open year-round. Less extreme but still beautiful.

Driving Tips

WHEN

Timing

Most high passes open late May to early June, close October. Best conditions July-September. Check status before going.

TIME

Start Early

Beat crowds and afternoon thunderstorms. Popular roads pack up by midday on weekends. Morning light is best for photos.

CAR

Vehicle Prep

Check brakes before steep descents. Use low gear going down. Watch engine temperature. Most roads passable in any vehicle.

ALT

Altitude

You may feel lightheaded at 12,000+ feet. Stay hydrated, don't overexert. Take it easy if you're not acclimatized.
