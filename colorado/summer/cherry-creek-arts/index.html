Cherry Creek Arts Festival

America's Premier Art Festival

World-Class Art in Denver

A July Fourth weekend tradition since 1991

The Cherry Creek Arts Festival consistently ranks among the top art festivals in America. Each July Fourth weekend, 350,000+ art lovers descend on Denver's Cherry Creek North neighborhood to browse work from over 250 juried artists representing every medium imaginable.

Artists are selected through a rigorous jurying process, ensuring quality across painting, sculpture, ceramics, glass, jewelry, photography, fiber arts, and more. Prices range from accessible prints to major investment pieces. Many artists attend personally, offering rare opportunities to discuss their work directly.

Beyond visual arts, the festival features a Culinary Corridor with top Colorado restaurants, live music on multiple stages, performing arts, and hands-on activities in Artivity Avenue for kids. The atmosphere is sophisticated yet accessible - Colorado casual at its best.

Festival Features

ART

Juried Artists

250+ artists in all mediums. Painting, sculpture, ceramics, glass, jewelry, photography, and more. Work for every taste and budget.

FOOD

Culinary Corridor

Top Denver restaurants serve festival fare. Food as art philosophy. Wine and craft beer available.

MUSIC

Entertainment

Live music throughout the weekend on multiple stages. Dance, theater, and performing arts showcases.

KIDS

Artivity Avenue

Hands-on art activities for children and families. Make art, meet artists, and spark creativity.

Visiting Tips

FREE

Admission

Free admission to browse and enjoy. Bring payment for purchases, food, and drinks.

PARK

Getting There

Cherry Creek North, roughly 1st-3rd Ave between Clayton and Steele. Free shuttles and paid parking available.

TIME

Best Time

Morning hours less crowded. Saturday busiest. Three days to explore - take your time.

SUN

Weather

July in Denver is warm. Sunscreen, hat, comfortable shoes essential. Afternoon thunderstorms possible.
