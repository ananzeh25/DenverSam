Denver Arts Week

Ten days of creative celebration

Denver's Creative Scene

Free and discounted access to the city's best art

Denver Arts Week showcases the city's thriving creative community with ten days of gallery openings, studio tours, performances, and special events. Free admission to museums, discounted tickets to shows, and First Friday Art Walks draw art lovers from across the region.

The celebration spans every artistic discipline - visual arts, theater, dance, music, film, and literary arts. Major institutions like the Denver Art Museum offer free days, while smaller galleries open their doors for special receptions with artists.

First Friday Art Walks anchor the week, with the Santa Fe Arts District, RiNo Art District, and Golden Triangle galleries staying open late with free refreshments and live demonstrations. It's the perfect introduction to Denver's art scene.

Arts Week Highlights

WALK

First Friday Art Walks

Galleries across multiple districts open late with receptions. Santa Fe, RiNo, and Golden Triangle are the main destinations. Free admission everywhere.

MUSEUM

Free Museum Days

Denver Art Museum, Museum of Contemporary Art, and others offer free admission during Arts Week. Special programming and extended hours.

STUDIO

Open Studios

Working artists open their studios to the public. See creative processes, meet artists, and purchase work directly. Self-guided and organized tours available.

STAGE

Performance Discounts

Theater, dance, and music venues offer discounted tickets. Colorado Ballet, Denver Center, and smaller companies participate.
