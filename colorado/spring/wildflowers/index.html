Colorado Wildflowers

Nature's spectacular show

Blooming Season

Timing the natural spectacle

Colorado's wildflower season is one of nature's great shows. As snow melts, waves of color sweep across the mountains - starting at lower elevations in late April and climbing to alpine meadows by July. At peak bloom, entire hillsides transform into carpets of blue columbine, red Indian paintbrush, and yellow sunflowers.

The timing varies by elevation and snowpack. Lower valleys (7,000-8,000 feet) bloom May through early June. Montane meadows (8,000-10,000 feet) peak June through mid-July. Alpine tundra above treeline explodes with tiny blooms in late July and August.

Crested Butte has earned the title "Wildflower Capital of Colorado" and hosts an annual festival in July. But spectacular displays occur throughout the mountains - you just need to know when and where to look.

Best Viewing Locations

CRESTED

Crested Butte

Wildflower Capital of Colorado. Snodgrass Trail and Gothic Road are legendary. Festival in July with guided hikes and workshops.

AMERI

American Basin

Near Lake City. Possibly Colorado's most spectacular wildflower display. Requires high-clearance vehicle. Peak late June-July.

RMNP

Rocky Mountain NP

Alpine tundra along Trail Ridge Road blooms late July. Lower trails bloom earlier. Ranger programs on wildflower ID.

GUANELLA

Guanella Pass

Close to Denver (1.5 hours). Beautiful displays June-July along the scenic byway. Easy roadside viewing and short hikes.

Common Colorado Wildflowers

BLUE

Colorado Columbine

State flower. Blue and white blooms June-August. Found in aspen groves and shaded areas. Protected - don't pick.

RED

Indian Paintbrush

Brilliant red-orange clusters. Blooms May-September at various elevations. Often mixed with other wildflowers.

YELLOW

Alpine Sunflower

Old Man of the Mountain. Blooms once after years of growth, then dies. Found above treeline in July-August.

PURPLE

Lupine

Tall purple spikes covering entire meadows. June-July at mid elevations. Especially stunning near Crested Butte.
