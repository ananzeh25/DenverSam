Boulder International Film Festival

BIFF - Four Days of Cinema

Colorado's Premier Film Festival

Independent cinema in a mountain town

Each March, Boulder transforms into a hub for independent cinema. The Boulder International Film Festival screens over 100 films across four days, featuring documentaries, narratives, shorts, and works from Colorado filmmakers. It's become one of the most respected regional festivals in the country.

The festival brings filmmakers to Boulder for Q&As and panels, offering audiences direct access to the creative minds behind the work. Many BIFF selections go on to major distribution or Oscar consideration. The atmosphere is intimate compared to bigger festivals - you might find yourself at a coffee shop table next to a director.

Screenings take place at multiple venues in downtown Boulder, making it easy to catch multiple films and explore the town between showings. The festival culminates in awards presentations and closing night celebrations.

Festival Highlights

FILM

Film Programs

Features, documentaries, shorts, and Colorado-made films. World and US premieres alongside festival circuit favorites.

QA

Filmmaker Q&As

Directors and cast attend screenings for discussions. Panels on filmmaking, industry trends, and Colorado production.

PARTY

Events & Parties

Opening and closing night galas, filmmaker lounges, and networking events throughout the weekend.

PASS

Tickets & Passes

Individual tickets and festival passes available. VIP options include parties and priority seating. Book early for popular screenings.
