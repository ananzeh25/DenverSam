Spring Skiing

The locals' favorite season

Corn Snow Paradise

Warm sun, soft snow, no crowds

Ask Colorado locals their favorite time to ski and many will say spring. The crowds disappear after Presidents' Day, the sun warms the slopes, and the snow transforms into perfect "corn" - soft, forgiving, and incredibly fun to ski.

Spring skiing means T-shirts on the slopes, long lunch breaks on sunny decks, and apr√®s-ski that starts mid-afternoon. Lift lines evaporate. Hotels offer shoulder-season deals. The vibe shifts from intense winter sport to relaxed mountain celebration.

Most Colorado resorts operate through mid-April, with some like Arapahoe Basin often staying open into June. The key to spring skiing is timing - you want warm afternoons to soften the snow, but you need to ski it before it gets too slushy. Late morning to early afternoon is usually prime time.

Best Spring Skiing

A-BASIN

Arapahoe Basin

"The Legend" often stays open into June. Famous Beach parties in the parking lot. High elevation (13,050') means snow lasts. The ultimate spring skiing destination.

LOVE

Loveland

Another high-elevation holdout that often skis into May. Affordable lift tickets. Continental Divide location catches late-season storms.

WOLF

Wolf Creek

Colorado's snowiest resort (465" average) means the best late-season base. Southern location brings warm spring temps. Usually open through mid-April.

WINTER

Winter Park

North-facing terrain holds snow well. Mary Jane's bumps are legendary in spring. Easy train access from Denver for day trips.

Spring Skiing Tips

TIME

Timing

Start around 10-11 AM when snow softens. Best conditions until 2-3 PM. Too early = icy, too late = slushy. Follow the sun around the mountain.

LAYER

Dress Light

Shed the heavy layers. T-shirts or light jackets work. Sunscreen is essential - spring sun reflects intensely off snow. Goggles with lighter lenses.

WAX

Spring Wax

Wet snow needs warmer temperature wax. Many shops offer spring tunes. Without it, you'll stick on flats. Worth the maintenance.

DEALS

Save Money

Shoulder season means deals on lodging and sometimes lift tickets. Less demand = better prices. Quality skiing without peak-season costs.
