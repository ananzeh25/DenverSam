Where to Stay in Arvada

Quick Info

Options: Limited hotels in Arvada proper; more choices nearby. Best location: Near Olde Town or G Line stations. Alternative: Stay in Denver, take G Line to Arvada. Budget range: Moderate chain hotels dominate. Book ahead: During events or summer weekends.

Arvada isn't a major hotel destination—most visitors are day-trippers from Denver or passing through. That said, several chain hotels serve the area, and the G Line makes staying in Denver a viable option for Arvada visits.

Arvada Options

Chain Hotels

Several national chain hotels operate in Arvada, primarily along major corridors like Wadsworth Boulevard and I-70. These offer standard amenities—reliable and predictable.

Typical options: Hampton Inn, Holiday Inn Express, Comfort Inn, and similar mid-range chains. Quality varies by location and management.

Location Considerations

Near Olde Town: Limited options within walking distance. Most hotels are on commercial strips.

Near I-70: More hotel density near the interstate. Convenient for road trips but not for Olde Town access.

Near G Line: Some hotels near transit stations. Check walking distance if you plan to use the train.

Nearby Areas

Westminster/Broomfield

More hotel options just north of Arvada. Chain hotels along Highway 36 corridor. Reasonable drive to Arvada attractions.

Wheat Ridge

Adjacent city with additional options. Along I-70 corridor.

Golden

Charming downtown with some boutique options. About 15 minutes from Arvada. Good if you want to combine Golden and Arvada visits.

Stay in Denver

The G Line connection means staying in downtown Denver is a practical option for Arvada visits. Stay near Union Station, take the train to Olde Town for dinner or brewery hopping, train back.

Advantages: More hotel variety, better restaurants and nightlife, walking access to Denver attractions.

G Line logistics: 25-30 minutes to Olde Town. Check schedule for last train if planning a late night.

Vacation Rentals

Airbnb and VRBO options exist throughout Arvada. Can offer more space and local neighborhood feel. Quality varies—check reviews carefully.

Best for: Families wanting space, longer stays, those preferring residential settings.

Tips

Book ahead for events. Arvada hosts festivals that can strain local lodging. Farmers market season and special events may affect availability.

Check G Line proximity. If you plan to use transit, verify walking distance to stations.

Consider your priorities. If Olde Town is the draw, proximity matters. If Arvada is just a day trip, stay wherever makes sense for your overall itinerary.

Denver may be better. Unless you specifically want to be in Arvada, downtown Denver offers more variety and the G Line makes access easy.

Compare prices. Arvada hotels can be cheaper than downtown Denver. Weigh savings against convenience.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are there boutique hotels in Arvada?

Not really—the lodging scene is dominated by chain hotels. Golden and Denver offer more unique options.

Is Arvada a good base for Denver visits?

It can work with the G Line, but staying in Denver itself is more convenient for city attractions.

Are there hotels walking distance to Olde Town?

Very limited options within walking distance. Most hotels are on commercial strips requiring a short drive.

Is Arvada cheaper than Denver?

Generally yes—suburban hotels often cost less than downtown Denver. Compare specific dates.

Should I stay in Golden instead?

If you want a charming downtown and plan to visit both areas, Golden is a good compromise with more character.
