Ferril Lake at City Park

Denver's Postcard-Perfect View

Ferril Lake sits at the heart of City Park, Denver's largest park. The view from the eastern shore - looking west across the water to the downtown skyline with the mountains behind - is the classic Denver photograph. Add pedal boats, a historic pavilion, and proximity to the zoo and museum, and you have a perfect urban escape.

The View

The Money Shot: Stand on the east side of the lake looking west. Downtown skyline, lake reflection, mountains behind. Best at sunset or on clear mornings.

City Park Pavilion: The historic 1896 structure on the lake's edge hosts summer concerts and adds to the scenic backdrop.

Activities

Pedal Boats: Rent swan-shaped pedal boats from the boathouse in summer. Fun for families, romantic for couples.

Fishing: Stocked with trout and bass. Fish from the shore - the western edge is most popular.

Walking: The path around the lake is about 0.8 miles. Combine with the larger City Park loop for a longer walk.

Combine Your Visit

City Park is home to the Denver Zoo and Denver Museum of Nature & Science. Make it a full day.

Pro Tip: Summer Sundays feature City Park Jazz, a free concert series at the pavilion. Bring a blanket and picnic.
