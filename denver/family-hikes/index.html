10 Epic Hikes for Denver Families with Kiddos

What are the best family hikes near Denver? Top picks: Red Rocks Trading Post Trail (1.4mi, ages 3+, free), Lair o' the Bear Creekside Loop (1.8mi, shaded creek), Staunton State Park Davis Ponds (0.5mi, perfect for toddlers), and Cherry Creek Reservoir Loop (paved, stroller-friendly).

Finding hikes that work for kids is harder than it sounds. Too long and they'll complain, too boring and you're back at the car in ten minutes. I've tested these 10 trails with my four daughters (ages 4-13). They all have what kids actually care about: rocks to climb, creeks to splash in, wildlife to spot, and enough interest to forget about screens for an hour.

Each trail includes quick facts, difficulty ratings, and what makes it worth the drive.

Quick Tips That Make the Difference

Start early. Parking fills up fast at popular trailheads, mornings are cooler in summer, and kids are fresher. Aim for 8-9am on weekends. Bring way more snacks than seems necessaryâ€”hungry kids are miserable kids. Pack variety: salty, sweet, protein. Let kids carry small backpacks with their own snacks and water to build ownership of the experience.

Denver's altitude affects kids differently. These trails start at 5,280 feet and go higher. Kids may tire faster, feel short of breath, or get headaches. Go slower than you would at sea level. Weather changes fast in the mountainsâ€”bring layers even if Denver is warm. Afternoon thunderstorms are common in summer, so plan to be off exposed trails by noon. Most importantly, make it fun, not a forced march. Let kids set some of the pace, stop to look at bugs, throw rocks in streams. The point is getting them to enjoy being outside, not crushing miles.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the easiest family hike near Denver?

Staunton State Park's Davis Ponds Loop (0.5 miles) is the easiestâ€”flat terrain around a scenic pond, perfect for toddlers and strollers. Cherry Creek Reservoir Loop is also fully paved and works for all ages.

Which Denver hikes have water for kids to play in?

Lair o' the Bear Park has the best creek access with multiple spots for rock throwing and splashing. Waterton Canyon follows the South Platte River with fishing opportunities. Cherry Creek trails offer reservoir views.

What age can kids start hiking in Colorado?

Kids as young as 3 can handle easy trails like Red Rocks Trading Post and Lair o' the Bear. For moderate trails with elevation gain, ages 5+ work better. Challenging trails like Mount Falcon are best for ages 8+.

Are Denver hiking trails safe for families?

Yes, all trails listed are well-maintained with clear paths. Main concerns are altitude (go slow), afternoon thunderstorms (finish by noon in summer), and sun exposure (bring sunscreen and water).

Do I need to pay for family hikes near Denver?

Red Rocks Trading Post, Lair o' the Bear, Waterton Canyon, and Genesee Mountain Park are free. State parks like Cherry Creek, Golden Gate Canyon, and Staunton require $10 daily vehicle pass or $80 annual pass.

What should I bring on a family hike in Denver?

Essentials: water (more than you think), snacks, sunscreen, layers (weather changes fast), closed-toe shoes, and small backpacks for kids to carry their own supplies. Let kids bring a magnifying glass for bug hunting.
