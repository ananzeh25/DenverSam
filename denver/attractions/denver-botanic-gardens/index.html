Denver Botanic Gardens

24 Acres of Living Art

Denver Botanic Gardens is one of the top botanical gardens in the US, with 24 acres of themed displays ranging from Japanese gardens to alpine rock gardens. Summer concerts bring world-class music, and the holiday lights transform the space into a winter wonderland.

Garden Highlights

Japanese Garden: One of the most authentic outside Japan. Stone lanterns, a tea house, and carefully manicured plantings create a meditative atmosphere.

Mordecai Children's Garden: Interactive nature play for kids. Not just a playground - real gardening experiences and sensory exploration.

Alpine Rock Garden: Colorado native plants in naturalistic rock formations. Showcases high-altitude flora you'd find in the Rockies.

Tropical Conservatory: Escape winter inside this glass-enclosed tropical paradise. Orchids, palms, and humid warmth year-round.

Events

Summer Concert Series: World-class musicians perform in the gardens on summer evenings. Intimate venue, bring a picnic. Tickets sell out fast.

Blossoms of Light (Winter): The gardens transform into a holiday light spectacular. One million lights, warm drinks, and festive atmosphere.

Chihuly Exhibit: The gardens have hosted major glass sculpture installations. Check the schedule for current special exhibitions.

Pro Tip: Visit in late May or June when the iris and peony collections peak. Fall brings stunning foliage. Winter is quieter but the conservatory stays lush.
