FAQ: RiNo Art District Murals Denver

Where is the RiNo art district in Denver?

RiNo art district sits northeast of downtown Denver between Brighton Boulevard and Broadway, from 20th to 40th Streets. The main mural corridor runs along Larimer Street between 25th-29th Streets. Park at Denver Central Market (2669 Larimer) for easiest access to highest concentration of murals.

What does RiNo stand for in Denver?

RiNo stands for River North Art District, named for its location north of downtown Denver along South Platte River. The neighborhood transformed from industrial warehouses into Denver's primary street art and creative district starting around 2005.

Is there a map of RiNo murals?

Official @rinoartdistrict Instagram posts updated maps quarterly. Denver Arts & Venues also publishes annual street art map available free at Denver Central Market and RiNo businesses. Google "RiNo mural map 2026" for current digital versions with GPS coordinates.

How long does it take to see RiNo murals?

Main corridor takes 2 hours to walk and photograph key pieces. Comprehensive tour covering all 12 locations in this guide requires 4-5 hours with breaks. Quick Instagram highlights (wings, postcard, rainbow stairs) takes 45 minutes if you skip crowds by visiting weekday mornings.

When is the best time to visit RiNo for murals?

Weekday mornings (8am-11am) offer best light and smallest crowds for photos. Overcast days eliminate glare on glossy murals creating better colors. Avoid weekend afternoons when tour groups and photo shoots create 15-20 minute waits at popular walls. First Friday art walks (6pm-10pm) feature live painting but heavy crowds.

Are RiNo murals permanent or do they change?

RiNo murals change constantly with pieces painted over monthly. Iconic works like Love This City wings and Greetings postcard get maintained and refreshed regularly. Legal walls under Broadway Viaduct rotate new pieces weekly. Photograph any murals you like immediately since most last 6-18 months before replacement.

Where should I park for RiNo mural tour?

Denver Central Market lot (2669 Larimer) charges $3 flat rate and sits centrally located for walking tour. Metered street parking on Larimer costs $2/hour with 2-hour limits. Free parking available on Walnut Street and side streets but requires 5-10 minute walk to main murals.

Can I take a guided RiNo mural tour?

Denver Graffiti Tour offers guided walking tours Saturdays at 10am ($25/person) departing from Finn's Manor at 2927 Larimer. RiNo Art District also organizes free First Friday walking tours monthly at 6pm. Self-guided tours using this map and GPS coordinates work perfectly for independent exploration.
