Top Best Dog Parks Around Denver, Colorado

What are the best dog parks in Denver? Top picks: Greenway Off Leash (large sand field, 24hrs), Lowry Dog Park (agility course, separate energy areas), Fuller Dog Park (shaded benches, sandy surface), and Berkeley Dog Park (gravel surface, separate areas).

Denver's vast outdoor spaces offer plenty of options for your four-legged friends to run, socialize, and play. Whether you're looking for a sandy terrain, agility courses, or separate areas for different energy levels, the Mile High City has something for every pup.

Greenway Off Leash Dog Park

2002 Spruce St, Denver, CO 80238 Show more
Hours: Open 24 hours

Location: 2002 Spruce St, Denver, CO 80238

Greenway Off Leash Dog Park is a massive, open sand field that gives dogs plenty of space to run, play, and socialize. Despite being about 20 minutes away for some visitors, regulars insist it's completely worth the drive. The park is immaculately maintained with clean grounds and always has a good number of friendly dogs to interact with.

There are water fountains for both humans and dogs, though they aren't always operational year-round. The park features several benches scattered throughout, and there are plenty of tennis balls and toys lying around for communal use. Regular visitors appreciate the well-behaved dogs and attentive owners.

Lowry Dog Park

Yosemite Way & E 4th Pl, Denver, CO 80230 Show more
Hours: Mon-Sun: 6AM-10PM

Location: Yosemite Way & E 4th Pl, Denver, CO 80230

Get Directions

Lowry Dog Park stands out for its thoughtful layout with separate areas based on dog energy levels rather than just size. There's a high-energy section for active dogs, a low-energy area for calmer pups, and an agility course where dogs can test their skills on various obstacles.

The park offers beautiful views of the mountains and has trails outside the off-leash area for walking. Water bowls are provided throughout the park, though you'll need to bring your own water as fountains aren't always available. The park tends to get busy during peak hours.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best dog parks in Denver?

Top picks include Greenway Off Leash Dog Park (large sand field, open 24 hours), Lowry Dog Park (agility course, separate energy areas), Fuller Dog Park (sandy surface with shade), and Kennedy Dog Park (spacious with multiple entrances).

Are Denver dog parks fenced?

Most Denver dog parks are fully fenced including Greenway, Lowry, Fuller, Berkeley, Kennedy, and Rail Yard. Cheesman Park welcomes leashed dogs but has no off-leash area.

Do Denver dog parks have separate areas for small dogs?

Many parks divide by energy level rather than size. Lowry, Fuller, Barnum, Berkeley, and Kennedy all have separate areas for high-energy and low-energy dogs, which works better for different temperaments.

What are the hours for Denver dog parks?

Most Denver dog parks are open 5AM-11PM daily. Greenway Off Leash Dog Park is unique in being open 24 hours. Lowry Dog Park hours are 6AM-10PM.

Do Denver dog parks have water fountains?

Water access varies and is often seasonal or unreliable. Greenway has fountains but they're not always working. It's best to bring your own water and portable bowl to any dog park.

Which Denver dog park has grass?

Most Denver dog parks use sand, gravel, or pebble surfaces to prevent mud. Cheesman Park has grass but requires leashes. For grassy off-leash areas, consider parks outside Denver city limits.
