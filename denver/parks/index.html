Best Parks in Denver

Urban Green Spaces for Everyone

Denver's 300 days of sunshine beg to be spent outdoors. The city's park system offers everything from historic urban squares to sprawling lakeside retreats. Here are the best parks for walking, picnicking, sports, and soaking up Colorado sun.

Washington Park

Denver's Beloved Classic

165 acres of lawns, lakes, gardens, and the city's most popular running loop. Denver's favorite park.

2.6mi Loop 2 Lakes Gardens

City Park

Denver's Largest

330 acres home to the zoo, museum, and the iconic skyline-with-mountains view. Denver's Central Park.

330 Acres Zoo Museum

Sloan's Lake Park

Mountain Views & Water

Denver's largest lake surrounded by a popular loop trail. Paddleboarding, kayaking, and stunning sunsets.

177 Acres Lake Access Sunsets

Cheesman Park

Historic & Scenic

80 acres of rolling hills, the neoclassical pavilion, and mountain views. Adjacent to Botanic Gardens.

80 Acres Historic Views

Commons Park

Urban Downtown Oasis

Riverside park near Union Station with skyline views. Perfect for downtown lunch breaks and evening walks.

Riverside Downtown Trail Access

Civic Center Park

Heart of Denver

12 acres between the Capitol and City Hall. Hosts festivals, food trucks, and the famous Greek amphitheater.

Festivals Food Trucks Central

Ruby Hill Park

Sledding & Mountain Biking

83 acres with Denver's best sledding hill in winter and mountain bike park year-round. Great city views.

Sledding MTB Park Views

Confluence Park

Where Rivers Meet

Historic site where Cherry Creek meets the Platte. Kayakers play in the whitewater park. Trail hub.

Kayaking Trail Hub Historic

Governor's Park

Quiet Capitol Hill Escape

12-acre neighborhood park in Capitol Hill. Shaded paths, tennis courts, and a peaceful alternative to crowds.

Quiet Tennis Shaded

Alamo Placita Park

Charming Neighborhood Green

Small historic park in Speer neighborhood. Community gardens, playground, and neighborhood gatherings.

Community Historic Charming
