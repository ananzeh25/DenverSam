Alamo Placita Park

Spanish Colonial Charm

Alamo Placita Park is a small but stunning neighborhood park with Spanish colonial architecture, a beautiful fountain, and meticulously maintained flower gardens. It's a photographer's favorite and one of Denver's best-kept secrets.

The Architecture

The park features Spanish colonial revival architecture, including a pergola, decorative walls, and a stunning central fountain. The design dates to 1929 and has been beautifully preserved.

The Gardens

The flower gardens are among the best-maintained in Denver. Local volunteers help keep them in peak condition from spring through fall. The annuals change with the seasons.

Photography Spot

Engagement photos, senior portraits, and family shoots are common here. The architecture, gardens, and fountain provide multiple backdrops in a small space. Early morning and golden hour are prime times.

Pro Tip: The park is surrounded by beautiful historic homes in the Speer neighborhood. Take a walk around the blocks to see some of Denver's finest residential architecture.
