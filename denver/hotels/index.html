Denver Hotels

Quick Info

Best for walkability: Downtown/LoDo. Best for luxury: Cherry Creek. Best value: DTC/Airport area. Best for mountains: Golden/West Denver. Book early: Convention season, ski weekends, and major events.

Denver's hotel scene ranges from historic properties downtown to modern luxury in Cherry Creek. Where you stay depends on what you're here for—downtown for urban exploration, airport area for early flights, or west side for mountain access.

Downtown & LoDo

The Oxford Hotel

Denver's oldest hotel (1891), steps from Union Station. Historic character with modern updates. The Cruise Room bar is a destination itself.

The Crawford Hotel

Inside Union Station with train station elegance. Rooms range from Pullman-style bunks to luxury suites. Central location for restaurants and transit.

The Maven

Modern boutique hotel at Dairy Block. Walkable to downtown attractions with rooftop bar and local vibe.

Kimpton Hotel Born

Contemporary design near Union Station. Restaurant, spa, and easy access to Larimer Square.

Cherry Creek

Halcyon

Boutique luxury with rooftop pool. Walking distance to Cherry Creek shopping and dining.

JW Marriott Cherry Creek

Full-service luxury adjacent to shopping district. Pool, spa, and upscale dining.

The Jacquard

Autograph Collection hotel with sophisticated design. Cherry Creek's newest luxury option.

RiNo & Five Points

The Source Hotel

Industrial-chic hotel above The Source market hall. Rooftop bar with city views. Walking distance to breweries and street art.

The Ramble Hotel

Boutique property with Death & Co bar. RiNo location for nightlife and arts district access.

Airport Area

Westin Denver International Airport

Connected to the airport terminal. Best for early flights or late arrivals. Train to downtown in 37 minutes.

Gaylord Rockies Resort

Massive resort with indoor water park near DIA. Good for families or conventions. Far from downtown.

Budget Options

Downtown Budget

Hostel Fish for budget travelers. Basic chains along Broadway for lower downtown rates.

Tech Center

Chain hotels in the Denver Tech Center offer lower rates with highway access. 20-30 minutes to downtown.

Tips

Book downtown for walkability. Union Station area puts you in walking distance of LoDo, Larimer Square, and transit.

Cherry Creek for shopping. If you're here to shop and want an upscale feel, Cherry Creek delivers.

Airport hotels work. The A Line train makes airport-area hotels viable. Good rates and easy transit.

Convention season fills up. Check for major events at the convention center. Rates spike and availability drops.

Ski weekends book early. Friday nights fill up with mountain-bound travelers. Book well ahead in winter.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best neighborhood to stay in Denver?

Downtown/LoDo for first-timers. Cherry Creek for shopping and luxury. RiNo for arts and nightlife.

Should I stay near the airport?

Only for early flights or late arrivals. The A Line train connects DIA to downtown in 37 minutes.

How far ahead should I book?

2-4 weeks for normal weekends. 1-2 months for ski season weekends or major events.

Do I need a car if I stay downtown?

No. Downtown is walkable and the light rail covers most destinations. You'd only need a car for mountain trips.
