I Soaked in All 8 Hot Springs Near Denver - Here's My Honest Ranking

After a rough week at work, nothing fixes me like sitting in hot mineral water surrounded by mountains.

Lucky for us, Colorado has tons of natural hot springs. I've been to all the ones within driving distance of Denver, and honestly? Some are worth the drive, some aren't.

Here's my real take on each one, starting with the closest.

The Close Ones (Under an Hour)

1. Eldorado Springs Pool (30 miles)

Address: 255 Eldorado Springs Dr., Eldorado Springs, CO

Phone: (303) 604-3055

Email: pool@eldoradosprings.com

Website: eldoradospringsresort.com

Season: Open daily 10am-6pm beginning May 24th through September 1st, weekends only thereafter through September.

Temperature Range: 60-80°F

Admission: $12 adults, $8 children (3-12), Free for children under 3

Distance from Denver: 30 miles (approx. 45 min drive)

Key Amenities: Family-friendly swimming areas, historic facility, natural canyon setting, picnic areas, changing rooms

Directions

Okay, let's be real - this isn't a "hot" spring. It's more like a naturally heated pool that stays around 70°F. Been around since 1905 though, which is pretty cool.

Perfect for families with kids who want to swim, not just soak. The canyon setting is gorgeous. But if you're looking for that therapeutic hot spring experience? This ain't it.

Pro tip: Pack lunch. Their snack bar is basically chips and candy bars. And on summer weekends? Get there before noon or forget about it.

2. Indian Hot Springs (35 miles) - My Go-To

Address: 302 Soda Creek Rd, Idaho Springs, CO 80452

Phone: (303) 989-6666

Website: indianhotsprings.com

Hours: 8:30 AM – 9 PM (Mon-Fri), 8 AM – 9:30 PM (Sat-Sun)

Check-in: 3:00 PM

Check-out: 11:00 AM

Temperature Range: 90-100°F (main pool), 104-112°F (geo-thermal caves)

Admission: $22-28 adults, $16-20 children (varies by pool access)

Distance from Denver: 35 miles (approx. 45 min drive)

Key Amenities: Tropical dome-covered pool, private mineral baths, geo-thermal caves, spa services, lodging options

Directions

This is my favorite quick escape from Denver. The geo-thermal caves are INSANE - like sitting in your own private 110°F underground sauna.

The main pool under the dome stays around 90-100°F. Perfect for actually swimming, not just sitting there like a human tea bag.

Reality check: Weekends are packed. I'm talking shoulder-to-shoulder in the caves. Tuesday afternoons? Paradise. Also, bring your own towel - theirs are basically washcloths.

3. Raspberry Hot Springs (35 miles) - The Secret Spot

Address: 899 Chinook Rd, Idaho Springs, CO 80452

Phone: (303) 718-7971

Website: raspberryhotsprings.com

Hours: 10 AM – 8 PM (Fri-Sun), 11 AM – 8 PM (Mon), Closed (Tue-Thu)

Note: Reservations required in advance, no walk-ins accepted.

Temperature Range: 98-106°F

Admission: $55-75 per person (private reservation required)

Distance from Denver: 35 miles (approx. 1 hour drive with mountain roads)

Key Amenities: Private experience, stone bath, sauna, panoramic mountain views, secluded setting

Directions

This place is COMPLETELY different. It's private - like, just you and whoever you bring private.

Warning: You absolutely need 4WD or AWD. That last mile up Chinook Road? I white-knuckled it in my Subaru. Steep, narrow, and if you meet another car, someone's backing up.

But once you're there? Total isolation with mountain views. Worth the stress if you hate crowds. Book Your Soak Here - they fill up weeks out.

The Weekend Trip Ones (2-3 Hours)

4. Mount Princeton Hot Springs (135 miles)

Address: 15870 County Rd. 162, Nathrop, CO 81236

Phone: (719) 395-2447 | Toll Free: (888) 395-7799

Email: reservations@mtprinceton.com

Website: mtprinceton.com

Temperature Range: 95-105°F (varies by pool)

Admission: $25-30 adults, $20-25 children (3-15)

Distance from Denver: 135 miles (approx. 2.5 hour drive)

Key Amenities: Multiple soaking pools, creek-side hot springs, waterslide, lodging, restaurant, spa services, fitness center

Directions

If you only do one overnight hot springs trip from Denver, make it this one.

They have pools right along the Arkansas River. And get this - a waterslide. Yes, a hot springs waterslide. My inner child was thrilled.

Best move: Stay overnight in their cabins. Wake up, walk to hot springs in your robe, soak before the day-trippers arrive. Then hit their spa. You'll drive home feeling like a new person.

5. Glenwood Hot Springs Resort (157 miles) - The Tourist Classic

Address: 415 E 6th St, Glenwood Springs, CO 81601

Phone: (970) 945-6571

Website: hotspringspool.com

Amenities: Free Wi-Fi, Breakfast, Free parking, Outdoor pool, Spa, Fitness center

Temperature Range: 90°F (main pool), 104°F (therapy pool)

Admission: $32-36 adults, $22-24 children (3-12)

Distance from Denver: 157 miles (approx. 3 hour drive)

Key Amenities: World's largest hot springs pool, therapy pool, lodging, spa services, restaurant, water slides, fitness center

Directions

This is the world's largest hot springs pool. It's basically two city blocks of warm water. Cool to see once.

But honestly? It feels more like a public pool that happens to be warm. Kids everywhere, people playing volleyball, not exactly zen.

The smaller therapy pool (104°F) is where you want to be. But good luck finding space on weekends.

Pro tip: If you stay at the lodge, your room key = unlimited pool access. Worth it to avoid the entry lines.

6. Strawberry Park Hot Springs (157 miles) - The Hippie Vibe

Address: 44200 Co Rd 36, Steamboat Springs, CO 80487

Phone: (970) 879-0342

Email: info@strawberryhotsprings.com

Website: strawberryhotsprings.com

Important: 4-wheel or AWD with snow tires/chains required Nov 1st to May 1st (local Routt County Law)

Temperature Range: 101-106°F (hot pools), 40°F (cold plunge)

Admission: $20 adults, $10 teens, $5 children (3-12)

Distance from Denver: 157 miles (approx. 3 hour drive)

Key Amenities: Natural stone pools, rustic accommodations, massage services, clothing-optional after dark (adults only), watsu therapy

Directions

This is what you picture when you think "Colorado hot springs." Natural stone pools, surrounded by forest, totally rustic.

Best part? Only $20. Worst part? That dirt road to get there. In winter you NEED 4WD - it's actually the law.

Important heads up for families: After dark it's clothing-optional and adults-only. Found that out the awkward way with my parents visiting. Plan accordingly!

The cold plunge stream is brutal but amazing. Going from 105°F pool to 40°F stream makes you feel superhuman.

The Road Trip Ones (5+ Hours)

7. Pagosa Springs (276 miles) - Worth the Drive

Address: Pagosa Springs, CO 81147

Website: visitpagosasprings.com

Elevation: 7,126 feet

Temperature Range: 83-114°F (varies by pool)

Admission: $35-49 adults, $13-22 children (depending on facility)

Distance from Denver: 276 miles (approx. 5 hour drive)

Key Amenities: World's deepest geothermal hot spring, multiple soaking options, riverside location, lodging, spa services, dining options

Directions

Five hour drive but hear me out - they have the world's DEEPEST geothermal hot spring. It's in the Guinness Book of World Records!

Multiple pools ranging from 83-114°F. You can literally find your perfect temperature.

I went in early October. Summer crowds gone, fall colors insane, still warm enough to soak comfortably. If you can swing late September or early May, that's the sweet spot.

Make it a long weekend. The drive alone through Wolf Creek Pass is worth it.

8. Ouray Hot Springs Pool (366 miles) - The Switzerland Experience

Address: 1230 Main St, Ouray, CO

Phone: (970) 325-3954

Email: info@visitouray.com

Website: visitouray.com/ourayhotspringspool

Hours: 9:00 AM – 4:00 PM daily

Temperature Range: 88-105°F (varies by pool section)

Admission: $18-24 adults, $8-12 children (4-17)

Distance from Denver: 366 miles (approx. 6.5 hour drive)

Key Amenities: Recently renovated facility, multiple temperature pools, adult-only soaking section, lap lanes, kids' area, mountain views

Directions

They call Ouray the "Switzerland of America" and holy crap, they're not exaggerating. Mountains literally surround you on all sides.

6.5 hour drive but if you're doing a San Juan Mountains road trip, this is your reward. Four different pool areas, including an adults-only section (thank god).

Recently renovated so everything's clean and new. Plus the town itself is adorable - like someone dropped a Swiss village in Colorado.

Not a day trip. But for a proper Colorado adventure? Absolutely worth it.

My Honest Rankings

Best Quick Escape: Indian Hot Springs (45 mins, actual hot water, caves)

Best for Families: Mount Princeton (waterslide wins)

Best Natural Setting: Strawberry Park (feels most authentic)

Best to Avoid Crowds: Raspberry Hot Springs (private, but that road...)

Best for Special Trip: Pagosa Springs (make it a long weekend)

Most Overrated: Glenwood (it's just a big warm pool)

Skip Unless Nearby: Eldorado Springs (not actually hot)

Real Tips Nobody Tells You

Bring your own towel. Always. Hot springs towels are tiny and gross.

Weekdays > weekends. Tuesday-Thursday is golden.

Hydrate like crazy. Altitude + hot water = dehydration city.

Don't eat a huge meal before. Trust me on this.

Winter soaking > summer soaking. Cold air, hot water = perfection.

Book overnight stays directly with the resort. Third-party sites often don't include pool access.
