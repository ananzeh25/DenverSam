8 Closest Hot Springs to Denver: Complete 2026 Guide

Quick Answer: Closest Hot Springs to Denver

Closest (45 min): Indian Hot Springs in Idaho Springs. Geothermal caves, dome pool, $22-28.

Best Overall (2.5 hrs): Mount Princeton. Creek-side pools, waterslide, 14er views.

Most Natural (3 hrs): Strawberry Park. Stone pools in forest, rustic vibe.

After a rough week, nothing fixes me like sitting in hot mineral water surrounded by mountains. I've visited every hot springs within driving distance of Denver, and some are worth the drive - others aren't.

Here's my honest guide to each one, ranked by distance from Denver.

Closest Hot Springs (Under 1 Hour)

1. Indian Hot Springs

35 mi / 45 min 90-112°F $22-28

My go-to spot for a quick escape from Denver. The geothermal caves are the star - underground chambers reaching 112°F, like sitting in your own private sauna carved into the mountain.

The dome-covered pool stays around 90-100°F and allows actual swimming. Private mineral baths available for couples. Been operating since 1863.

Reality check: Weekends are packed, especially the caves. Tuesday-Thursday afternoons are paradise. Bring your own towel - theirs are thin.

Note on Eldorado Springs: Some lists include Eldorado Springs Pool (30 min from Denver), but it's not a true hot spring - water stays around 70°F. Great for families who want to swim, but skip it if you want that therapeutic hot soak.

Weekend Trip Hot Springs (2-3 Hours)

2. Mount Princeton Hot Springs

135 mi / 2.5 hrs 95-140°F $25-30

If you only do one overnight hot springs trip from Denver, make it this one. Multiple pools with direct views of Mount Princeton (14,197 ft), a waterslide, and the magic of creek-side soaking.

Walk down to Chalk Creek, find where the hot springs bubble up through the creek bed, and create your own perfect temperature by mixing hot spring and cold creek water. This is the real experience.

Best move: Stay overnight in their cabins. Wake up, walk to hot springs in your robe before day-trippers arrive. Worth every penny.

3. Glenwood Hot Springs

157 mi / 2.5 hrs 90-104°F $32-36

The world's largest hot springs pool - two city blocks of mineral water. It's the most famous Colorado hot springs and holds the Guinness World Record for size.

The main pool (90-93°F) is huge and allows actual swimming. The therapy pool (104°F) is where you get the real hot soak experience.

Honest take: It's impressive to see once, but feels more like a crowded public pool than a serene hot springs. Weekdays are significantly better. Iron Mountain Hot Springs nearby offers a more intimate alternative.

Full Glenwood Hot Springs Guide →

4. Strawberry Park Hot Springs

157 mi / 3 hrs 101-106°F $20-25

This is what you picture when you think "Colorado hot springs." Natural stone pools, surrounded by forest, steam rising through the trees. Most authentic experience in the state.

Multiple pools at different temperatures, cold plunge stream, rustic vibes. Located 7 miles up a mountain road from Steamboat Springs.

Important: Clothing-optional and adults-only after dark. During the day, families welcome. The access road requires 4WD/AWD in winter (it's the law). Cash only.

Road Trip Hot Springs (5+ Hours)

5. Pagosa Springs

276 mi / 5 hrs 83-114°F $35-49

Home to the world's deepest geothermal hot spring (Guinness World Record). Multiple resort options along the San Juan River with stunning mountain views.

The Springs Resort has 25 pools ranging from 83°F to 114°F - literally find your perfect temperature. The drive through Wolf Creek Pass is spectacular on its own.

Worth it? Absolutely - if you make it a weekend. Not a day trip at 5 hours. Combine with Wolf Creek skiing in winter.

Full Pagosa Springs Guide →

6. Ouray Hot Springs

366 mi / 6.5 hrs 88-106°F $18-24

Soak surrounded by 13,000+ foot peaks on all sides. They call Ouray "The Switzerland of America" and it delivers. Recently renovated municipal pool with multiple temperature sections.

The setting is unmatched - dramatic canyon walls rising directly above you. Best value in Colorado at $18-24.

Note: The Million Dollar Highway to get there is one of America's most scenic (and nerve-wracking) drives - steep cliffs, tight curves, no guardrails. Worth it for a San Juan Mountains road trip, not a quick soak.

Tips Nobody Tells You

  • Bring your own towel. Hot springs towels are tiny and overpriced. A full beach towel from home is worth the space.
  • Weekdays beat weekends. Tuesday-Thursday is golden. Saturday morning at any popular hot springs is shoulder-to-shoulder.
  • Hydrate aggressively. Altitude + hot water = dehydration fast. Bring water bottles and drink before, during, and after.
  • Don't eat a big meal before. Trust me on this one. Light snack is fine, full lunch is not.
  • Winter soaking is best. Cold air, hot water, steam rising, snow on the mountains. Peak Colorado experience.
  • Book overnight stays directly. Third-party sites often don't include pool access. Call the resort.
  • Check road conditions in winter. Mountain passes close. I-70 gets brutal. CDOT website before you go.

Winter Driving: Several of these hot springs require mountain passes that can be treacherous in winter. Strawberry Park requires 4WD by law. Wolf Creek Pass (to Pagosa) gets heavy snow. The Million Dollar Highway (to Ouray) is not for the faint of heart. Always check CDOT conditions and give yourself extra time.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the closest natural hot springs to Denver?

Indian Hot Springs in Idaho Springs is the closest true natural hot springs at 35 miles / 45 minutes from Denver. It has geothermal caves reaching 112°F and a dome-covered pool.

Are there any free hot springs near Denver?

The closest free hot springs are primitive and require significant effort to access. Commercial facilities like Indian Hot Springs ($22-28) or Strawberry Park ($20) are the most accessible options. Some hot springs along remote creeks exist but require hiking and local knowledge.

Which Colorado hot springs are best for couples?

Strawberry Park (natural, clothing-optional after dark), Mount Princeton (private creek pools, overnight cabins), and the private baths at Indian Hot Springs are the most romantic options.

Which hot springs are best for families with kids?

Mount Princeton wins for families - waterslide, multiple pool temperatures, and the creek is fun for kids. Glenwood is also family-friendly with its massive pool. Avoid Strawberry Park after dark (adults only).

Are Colorado hot springs open in winter?

Yes, all major hot springs are open year-round. Winter is actually the best time to visit - cold air, hot water, steam rising, fewer crowds. Just be prepared for winter driving conditions on mountain roads.
