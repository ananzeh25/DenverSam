Aspen Airport Transportation

Just 4 Miles to Downtown Aspen

Quick Facts: Aspen/Pitkin County Airport

The closest airport to Aspen—when flights operate

Airport Code: ASE (Aspen/Pitkin County Airport)

Distance to Aspen: 4 miles

Distance to Snowmass: 9 miles

Transfer Time: 10-15 minutes to downtown Aspen

The reality: ASE is incredibly convenient when flights operate. But weather delays and cancellations are common, and flight options are limited.

About Aspen Airport

Convenience with caveats

Aspen/Pitkin County Airport is one of the most scenic—and challenging—airports in the country. Nestled in the Roaring Fork Valley at 7,820 feet, it's just minutes from downtown but subject to mountain weather.

ASE Advantages

  • Proximity: 4 miles to Aspen, 10 minutes to downtown
  • Skip the drive: Avoids 4-hour drive from Denver
  • Quick transfers: Free bus to downtown in 15 minutes
  • Small airport: Fast baggage, easy navigation

ASE Challenges

  • Weather delays: Clouds, wind, and snow cause frequent disruptions
  • Limited flights: Only United and American serve ASE regularly
  • Small planes: Usually regional jets, bumpy rides
  • Expensive: Flights cost significantly more than Denver
  • Cancellations: Have a backup plan for weather days

When to Consider ASE

  • Premium trips where time matters
  • Good weather windows
  • When you can handle potential delays
  • VIP and private aviation (ASE handles many private jets)

Transportation Options

Getting from ASE to Aspen and Snowmass

RFTA Bus (Free)

The Roaring Fork Transportation Authority runs free buses from the airport to downtown Aspen. This is the easiest option for most travelers.

  • Buses timed to flight arrivals
  • 15 minutes to Rubey Park Transit Center (downtown)
  • Connects to Snowmass Village service
  • Luggage space available

Hotel Shuttles (Free-$25)

Many Aspen hotels offer airport pickup. Contact your hotel before arrival.

  • St. Regis, Little Nell, and other luxury hotels often provide service
  • Arrange in advance with your concierge
  • Direct to hotel entrance

Taxi ($30-50)

Taxis wait at the airport. For such a short distance, this is a convenient option.

  • High Mountain Taxi, Aspen Taxi
  • Fixed rates to most Aspen destinations
  • To Snowmass: $50-70

Private Car/Limo ($75-150)

For a luxury arrival matching the Aspen experience.

  • Arrange in advance
  • Meet-and-greet at baggage claim
  • Luxury vehicles available

Rental Car ($100-200/day)

Available but expensive. Consider whether you really need one—Aspen has excellent free transit.

  • Hertz, Avis at airport
  • Premium pricing (it's Aspen)
  • Useful for day trips to Independence Pass, Maroon Bells
  • Downtown parking is limited and expensive

Getting Around Aspen

You probably don't need a car

Aspen has excellent free public transit. Most visitors never need a car.

Free RFTA Bus System

  • Within Aspen: Free circulator buses
  • Aspen to Snowmass: Free express service
  • Four Mountains: Free ski shuttle to Aspen Mountain, Highlands, Buttermilk
  • Maroon Bells: Required shuttle in summer (small fee)

Walkable Downtown

Downtown Aspen is compact. Most hotels, restaurants, and shops are within walking distance of each other. Aspen Mountain gondola is downtown.

When You Might Want a Car

  • Independence Pass (summer only, spectacular drive)
  • Day trips to Glenwood Springs or Vail
  • Staying in outlying areas

Frequently Asked Questions

Aspen Airport transportation

How do I get from Aspen Airport to downtown Aspen?

The easiest way is the free RFTA bus, which takes about 15 minutes to downtown. Taxis are also available at the airport for $30-50. Many hotels offer complimentary pickup.

Is Aspen Airport often delayed or cancelled?

Yes, more than most airports. Mountain weather, wind, and visibility cause frequent issues. Have a backup plan—driving from Denver or rebooking through Eagle or Denver if needed.

Should I fly into Aspen or Denver?

Aspen is convenient but risky. Denver is reliable but requires a 4-hour drive. For short trips or when time is premium, try Aspen with a backup plan. For reliability, fly to Denver or Eagle.

Is there Uber at Aspen Airport?

Limited availability. Uber and Lyft operate in Aspen but there aren't many drivers. Use the free bus or arrange a taxi for reliable service from the airport.

How far is Snowmass from Aspen Airport?

About 9 miles, 15-20 minutes by car. RFTA buses connect the airport to Snowmass Village. Taxis cost $50-70.
