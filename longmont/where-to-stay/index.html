Where to Stay in Longmont

Quick Info

Options: Chain hotels, budget motels, vacation rentals. Best area: Near downtown or Highway 119 corridor. Prices: Lower than Boulder, comparable to Denver suburbs. Book ahead: For summer weekends and events.

Longmont has limited but functional lodging options. Most visitors stay in chain hotels along the highway corridors or opt for vacation rentals. The selection isn't as extensive as Boulder or Denver, but prices are more reasonable, and you're well-positioned for exploring the region.

Hotels

Chain hotels dominate Longmont lodging:

Mid-range chains: Hampton Inn, Best Western, and similar properties offer reliable, predictable stays. Located primarily along Highway 119 and near I-25 access. Standard amenities include breakfast, WiFi, and parking.

Budget options: Several budget motels serve travelers looking for basic accommodations. Quality varies - check recent reviews before booking.

Extended stay: A few extended-stay properties work for longer visits, with kitchenettes and more space.

Longmont lacks boutique hotels or historic properties. If you want unique accommodations, look to Boulder or vacation rentals.

Location Considerations

Near downtown: Best for walking to Main Street restaurants and breweries. Limited hotel options in this area, but some exist.

Highway 119 corridor: Most chain hotels cluster here. Easy access to both Longmont attractions and Boulder. Typical suburban hotel strip environment.

Near I-25: Good for regional exploration if you're using Longmont as a base for day trips. Less convenient for downtown Longmont itself.

Vacation Rentals

Airbnb and VRBO listings in Longmont offer alternatives to hotels:

  • Houses and apartments throughout residential areas
  • Often better value for groups or families
  • Kitchen access for longer stays
  • More residential neighborhood experience

Check local regulations and reviews. Quality and availability vary seasonally.

Alternative Base Locations

Consider staying elsewhere and day-tripping to Longmont:

Boulder: More hotel options and nightlife. 15 minutes to Longmont. Higher prices but more selection.

Denver/Denver suburbs: Abundant lodging options. 45-60 minutes to Longmont. Good if combining with Denver activities.

Estes Park: If Longmont is part of a Rocky Mountain National Park trip, staying in Estes puts you closer to the park with Longmont accessible for a day.

Booking Tips

Summer weekends: Book ahead, especially during events or if you want specific properties.

Last minute: Weeknights usually have availability. Walk-in rates may be higher than online booking.

Compare Boulder: Sometimes Boulder hotels aren't much more expensive and offer more amenities and location benefits.

Check inclusions: Breakfast, parking, and WiFi are usually included at Longmont hotels but verify.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I stay in Longmont or Boulder?

Longmont for lower prices and brewery focus. Boulder for more dining/nightlife options and walkability. They're close enough that either works as a base.

Are there hotels downtown?

Limited options near downtown. Most hotels are along highway corridors. Check vacation rentals for downtown-adjacent options.

Is Longmont a good base for RMNP?

Possible but not ideal. Estes Park is closer to the park. Longmont works if you're combining RMNP with Boulder County activities.

How far in advance should I book?

A few days to a week is usually fine except for major events. Summer weekends benefit from earlier booking.
