Parker, Colorado

Small Town Feel, Big City Access

Your Guide to Parker

Downtown charm and family recreation

Parker maintains a small-town feel despite significant growth. The walkable downtown on Main Street offers local restaurants, shops, and community events. The town has invested heavily in trails and recreation.

Cherry Creek Trail runs through Parker, connecting to the regional system. Salisbury Park and other recreation facilities provide sports, fitness, and family activities.

Located in Douglas County southeast of Denver, Parker offers excellent schools and family-oriented amenities. The Parker Arts, Culture & Events (PACE) Center hosts performing arts and community programs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Planning your Parker visit

How far is Parker from Denver?

About 25 miles southeast of downtown Denver via I-25 and E-470, roughly 35 minutes depending on traffic.

Is there light rail to Parker?

Not directly. The closest stations are in Lone Tree and Centennial. Parker is primarily car-dependent.

What's special about downtown Parker?

It maintains a genuine small-town feel with local businesses and community events. Not a tourist destination, but charming for what it is.

When is Parker Days?

Usually mid-June. The festival includes a carnival, parade, live music, and community activities over several days.
