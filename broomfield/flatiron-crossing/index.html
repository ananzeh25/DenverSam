Flatiron Crossing

Quick Info

Type: Open-air shopping center. Location: US-36 at Flatiron Parkway. Anchors: Macy's, Dillard's, Dick's Sporting Goods. Dining: 20+ restaurants. Hours: 10am-9pm Mon-Sat, 11am-6pm Sun. Parking: Free.

Flatiron Crossing is the premier shopping destination along the US-36 corridor between Denver and Boulder. The open-air design takes advantage of Colorado's weather, with covered walkways connecting major retailers and restaurants.

Unlike enclosed malls, Flatiron Crossing feels more like a village center—outdoor plazas, landscaping, and mountain views make shopping a more pleasant experience than typical suburban retail.

Shopping

Department Stores

Macy's and Dillard's anchor the center, offering full-service department store shopping. Dick's Sporting Goods provides extensive outdoor and athletic gear.

Specialty Retail

Mix of national chains and specialty stores. Fashion, home goods, electronics, and lifestyle retailers throughout. Apple Store, Sephora, H&M, and similar brands.

The Village

Outdoor lifestyle section with additional retailers and restaurants. More relaxed atmosphere than the main shopping areas.

Dining

Sit-Down Restaurants

Multiple full-service restaurants ranging from casual to upscale casual. Good options for lunch during shopping or dinner destination.

Quick Service

Food court and quick-service options throughout. Coffee shops, fast casual, and grab-and-go choices.

The Village Dining

Additional restaurant options in the Village section. Often less crowded than main center restaurants.

Entertainment

AMC Theatre

Multi-screen movie theater with latest releases. IMAX and premium formats available. Makes Flatiron Crossing a dinner-and-movie destination.

Events

Seasonal events, holiday activities, and promotions throughout the year. Check the center's calendar for current programming.

Location & Access

Getting There

Exit US-36 at Flatiron Parkway. Well-signed from the highway. Central location between Denver and Boulder.

Parking

Extensive free parking throughout. Can get busy on weekends and holidays—arrive early or use outer lots.

Transit

RTD bus service to Flatiron Crossing. Check routes from Denver or Boulder for car-free access.

Tips

Weekday mornings best. Less crowded for serious shopping. Weekends get busy, especially afternoons.

Outdoor dress. The open-air design means weather exposure when walking between stores. Dress for the season.

Holiday planning. Extended hours during holidays but also extended crowds. Plan accordingly.

Combine with hiking. Flatiron Vista trailhead is nearby. Shop then hike, or hike then shop.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Flatiron Crossing better than Park Meadows?

Different character—Flatiron is open-air, Park Meadows is enclosed. Flatiron is better for Boulder-area access; Park Meadows for south metro.

Is there an Apple Store?

Yes—Apple Store with full service and Genius Bar.

How long should I plan?

2-4 hours for shopping. Add time for meals or movies.

Is it good for kids?

Yes—family-friendly with play areas and kid-oriented stores. Movie theater good for families.

Are there hotels nearby?

Several hotels in the Broomfield/Interlocken area within minutes of the mall.
