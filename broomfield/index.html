Broomfield, Colorado

Between Denver and Boulder

Your Guide to Broomfield

Shopping, events, and central location

Broomfield is Colorado's unique city-county—the only place in the state where city and county boundaries are identical. Positioned midway between Denver and Boulder on US-36, it offers convenient access to both cities.

Flatiron Crossing is the region's premier shopping destination, with major retailers and dining options. The 1stBank Center hosts concerts and sporting events, bringing entertainment to the north metro.

The city has developed extensive trail systems connecting neighborhoods and open spaces. The Flatiron Vista Loop offers hiking with views of the iconic Boulder Flatirons just minutes from shopping.

Frequently Asked Questions

Planning your Broomfield visit

How far is Broomfield from Denver and Boulder?

About 15 miles from downtown Denver and 10 miles from Boulder. US-36 express lanes make commutes to both cities predictable.

Why is Broomfield a city-county?

Broomfield originally spanned four counties. In 2001, voters created a consolidated city-county to simplify governance.

Is Flatiron Crossing good for shopping?

Yes, it's one of the metro area's best shopping destinations with major retailers, restaurants, and an open-air layout.

What's at the 1stBank Center?

Concerts, sporting events, and shows. It's a mid-size venue that hosts national touring acts and local events.
