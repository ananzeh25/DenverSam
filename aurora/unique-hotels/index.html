Practical Information: Hotels in Aurora CO

Location: Aurora sits immediately east of Denver adjacent to Denver International Airport. Most hotels offer free airport shuttles running every 20-30 minutes. Downtown Denver is 20-30 minutes west via I-70 or I-225.

Pricing: Budget hotels average $100-120/night. Mid-range properties run $120-180/night. Upscale resorts like Gaylord Rockies cost $200-400/night. Prices spike during conventions and peak ski season December-February.

Best Times: Book 2-3 months ahead for summer (June-August) and winter ski season (December-February). Shoulder seasons (April-May, September-October) offer better rates and availability. Avoid major convention dates when hotels sell out.

Transportation: Most hotels provide free airport shuttles. Rent a car if exploring Denver attractions or mountain towns. Uber and Lyft operate throughout Aurora. RTD light rail connects to downtown Denver.

Dining: Hotels with free meals (Homewood Suites breakfast and dinner) or kitchens (TownePlace Suites, Woolley's) save significant money. Aurora has diverse restaurant options along East Colfax Avenue and Havana Street.
