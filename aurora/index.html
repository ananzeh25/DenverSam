Aurora, Colorado

Colorado's Most Diverse City

Your Guide to Aurora

Culture, cuisine, and outdoor access

Aurora is Colorado's third-largest city and its most diverse. Over 160 languages are spoken here, creating one of the state's best international dining scenes. Ethiopian, Korean, Vietnamese, and Mexican restaurants line Colfax Avenue and Havana Street.

Stanley Marketplace transformed a former airplane hangar into a community hub with restaurants, shops, and gathering spaces. Cherry Creek State Park offers a lake beach, trails, and wildlife viewing minutes from downtown Aurora.

The city stretches from Denver's eastern edge toward the plains. Buckley Space Force Base and Anschutz Medical Campus are major employers. Light rail connects Aurora to downtown Denver and DIA.

Explore Aurora

Diverse dining and urban parks

FOOD

International Dining

Havana Street's global restaurants span Ethiopian, Korean, Vietnamese, and more. Colorado's best international food scene.

MARKET

Stanley Marketplace

Former airplane hangar now housing 50+ restaurants, shops, and services. Community gathering space with events year-round.

PARK

Cherry Creek State Park

4,000-acre urban park with swimming beach, hiking trails, and wildlife. Off-leash dog area and campground.

TRAIL

High Line Canal

71-mile trail running through Aurora. Walking, biking, and horseback riding through urban and natural landscapes.

GOLF

Golf Courses

Multiple public courses including Murphy Creek and Saddle Rock. Affordable golf with mountain views.

MUSEUM

Aurora History Museum

Free museum exploring Aurora's growth from farming community to diverse city. Rotating exhibits and local history.

Frequently Asked Questions

Planning your Aurora visit

How far is Aurora from downtown Denver?

Aurora borders Denver on the east. Stanley Marketplace is about 15 minutes from downtown; Cherry Creek State Park about 20 minutes.

What's special about Aurora's food scene?

Aurora is Colorado's most diverse city, with authentic international restaurants hard to find elsewhere in the state. Havana Street is the epicenter.

Is Cherry Creek State Park worth visiting?

Absolutely. The beach, trails, and wildlife make it one of the metro area's best outdoor escapes. Arrive early on summer weekends.

How do I get to Aurora from DIA?

Aurora is between DIA and Denver. Take Peña Boulevard south to I-70 or I-225. Light rail also connects the airport to Aurora.
