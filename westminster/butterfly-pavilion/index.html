Butterfly Pavilion

Quick Info

Type: Invertebrate zoo and butterfly conservatory. Butterflies: 1,600+ free-flying. Highlights: Tropical Wings, touch tanks, Rosie the tarantula. Time needed: 1-2 hours. Best for: Families, nature lovers. Tickets: Timed entry recommended.

Butterfly Pavilion is one of the few standalone invertebrate zoos in the country and Westminster's top attraction. The tropical conservatory lets you walk among 1,600+ free-flying butterflies from around the world.

Tropical Wings

The Experience

Enter a warm, humid tropical conservatory filled with exotic butterflies. They land on plants, flowers, and sometimes on visitors. Wear bright colors to attract them.

Species

Butterflies from Asia, Africa, and the Americas. Species rotate seasonally. Look for Blue Morphos, Owl Butterflies, and Zebra Longwings.

Emergence Window

Watch butterflies emerge from chrysalides in the emergence lab. Staff can explain the metamorphosis process.

Other Exhibits

Rosie the Tarantula

The pavilion's famous Chilean Rose Tarantula. You can hold Rosie with staff assistance—a bucket-list experience for many visitors.

Touch Tanks

Interactive tide pool exhibits with sea stars, hermit crabs, and other invertebrates. Staff supervise and educate.

Crawl-Through Exhibits

Kid-sized tunnels and interactive displays teach about insect life from a bug's perspective.

Outdoor Gardens

Butterfly Garden

Native plants attract Colorado butterflies in warm months. Different experience than the tropical conservatory.

Stingray Exhibit

Seasonal outdoor stingray touch pool. Additional fee may apply.

Visiting Tips

Book timed tickets. Popular times sell out. Reserve online in advance.

Wear bright colors. Butterflies are attracted to reds, oranges, and yellows.

Go slow. Stand still and butterflies may land on you. No touching them.

Allow 1-2 hours. Enough time for all exhibits without rushing.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost?

Adult tickets typically $15-18. Children, seniors, and members get discounts. Check website for current pricing.

Is it good for kids?

Excellent for kids. Interactive exhibits, touch tanks, and the wonder of walking among butterflies.

Can I hold a tarantula?

Yes—Rosie is available for holding during designated times. Staff will assist you.

How warm is the conservatory?

Around 80°F and humid. Dress in layers you can remove.

Is there parking?

Free parking lot at the facility.
