Standley Lake Regional Park

Quick Info

Size: 1,000-acre reservoir. Activities: Swimming, fishing, paddling, hiking. Season: Beach open Memorial Day-Labor Day. Fees: Park entry fee; annual passes available. Views: Front Range mountain panorama. Best for: Water recreation, wildlife.

Standley Lake is Westminster's outdoor gem—a 1,000-acre reservoir with swimming beaches, excellent fishing, and mountain views. It's one of the best urban water recreation spots in the Denver metro.

Swimming Beach

Beach Area

Sandy beach with designated swimming area. Lifeguards on duty during peak season. Perfect for cooling off on hot summer days.

Season

Beach open Memorial Day through Labor Day. Hours vary—check before visiting.

Facilities

Restrooms, changing areas, and picnic facilities near the beach. Concessions available during peak times.

Fishing

Species

Walleye, bass, trout, catfish, and panfish. The lake is known for quality walleye fishing.

Shore & Boat

Fish from shore or by boat. Boat rentals available seasonally.

License

Valid Colorado fishing license required for ages 16+.

Paddling

Kayaks & SUP

Kayaking and stand-up paddleboarding popular on calm mornings. Rentals available.

Regulations

Some restrictions on motorized boats. Check current rules before launching.

Trails & Wildlife

Lake Loop Trail

Trail circles the lake with mountain views throughout. Good for walking, running, and biking.

Wildlife

Bald eagles, pelicans, deer, and other wildlife frequent the area. Bring binoculars.

Tips

Arrive early. Parking fills on hot summer weekends. Get there by mid-morning.

Bring sunscreen. Little shade on the beach. Colorado sun is intense.

Buy a pass. Annual pass pays off with regular visits.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much is entry?

Daily vehicle fee applies. Annual passes available for frequent visitors.

Can I bring a boat?

Yes, with restrictions. Inspections required. Check regulations before launching.

Are dogs allowed?

Dogs allowed in some areas on leash. Not permitted on the beach.

Can I camp?

Camping available. Reserve in advance during peak season.

How cold is the water?

Varies by season. Warmest in July-August but still refreshing.
