10 Best Dog-Friendly Restaurants & Patios in Estes Park

Best dog-friendly restaurants in Estes Park? Rock Cut Brewing has the most welcoming vibe for dogs with water bowls and treats. Lumpy Ridge Brewing has a huge outdoor area where dogs can relax. Kind Coffee is perfect for a quick stop with outdoor seating right on Elkhorn Ave. Latitude 105 has mountain views from their dog-friendly patio. Always call ahead on busy weekends.

Rock Cut Brewing Company

$8-15 | 390 W Riverside Dr

The most dog-friendly spot in Estes Park. They genuinely love dogs here—water bowls are always out, and the staff will probably greet your pup before you. Spacious outdoor patio with mountain views. Great craft beer selection brewed on-site. Food trucks rotate through, so check their social media. Dogs are welcome inside when it's slow too.

Order: Flight of house beers, whatever food truck is there. Dog perks: Water bowls, treats, lots of space.

Lumpy Ridge Brewing Company

$8-15 | 1860 N Lake Ave

Huge outdoor beer garden that's perfect for dogs. Plenty of room to spread out, so you're not on top of other tables. They're serious about their craft beer—try the Lump Squatch IPA. Food is elevated pub fare. Located slightly outside the downtown crush, which means easier parking and a more relaxed vibe for nervous pups.

Order: Lump Squatch IPA, loaded nachos. Dog perks: Large outdoor area, less crowded than downtown.

Wheel Bar

$10-20 | 132 E Elkhorn Ave

Classic Western saloon that actually welcomes dogs on their patio. Locals bring their dogs here all the time—it's part of the vibe. Solid bar food, cold beer, and no pretense. The patio faces Elkhorn Ave, so it's great for people-watching while your dog watches other dogs. Gets busy on weekends.

Order: Burger, local craft beer. Dog perks: Locals bring dogs regularly, water available.

Lonigans Saloon

$10-18 | 110 W Elkhorn Ave

The friendliest dive bar in Estes Park. Dogs are absolutely welcome on the outdoor patio. Live music, cheap drinks, and a crowd that won't judge your trail-dirty dog. It's a bit rough around the edges, which is exactly what makes it great. Perfect after a long hike when you both need to decompress.

Order: Whatever's on tap, bar snacks. Dog perks: Casual vibe, no one cares if your dog is muddy.

Kind Coffee

$5-12 | 470 E Elkhorn Ave

Best coffee in Estes Park, and dogs are welcome on the outdoor patio. Prime location right on Elkhorn Ave for people-watching. They roast their own beans on-site. Perfect stop before or after a hike—grab a breakfast burrito and a pour-over while your pup rests. Staff is friendly to four-legged customers.

Order: House-roasted pour-over, breakfast burrito. Dog perks: Water available, outdoor tables.

Latitude 105 Alehouse

$20-30 | Holiday Inn Estes Park

Don't let the hotel location fool you. This place has killer mountain views from the outdoor patio, and dogs are genuinely welcome. They've got 18 rotating craft beer taps and surprisingly good food—the poutine with homemade tots is excellent. Perfect post-hike spot when you want views and your dog needs shade.

Order: Poutine with homemade tots, local craft beer. Dog perks: Shaded patio, water bowls, mountain views.

Wapiti Colorado Pub

$15-25 | 184 E Elkhorn Ave

Gastropub with a dog-friendly patio downtown. Their burger is one of the best in town—local beef, quality toppings. Good beer selection including Colorado breweries. The patio is smaller but dogs are welcome. Gets crowded on summer weekends, so arrive early or late to snag an outdoor table.

Order: Wapiti burger, Colorado craft beer. Dog perks: Downtown location, friendly staff.

Himalayan Curry & Kebob

$20-30 | Downtown

Nepalese restaurant with a dog-friendly patio. The food is genuinely excellent—try the elk kebob for something unique or the jhol momos (dumplings in spiced soup). Extensive vegetarian menu too. The patio is a nice escape from the typical Elkhorn Ave scene. Your dog will appreciate the calmer vibe.

Order: Elk kebob, jhol momos. Dog perks: Quieter patio, water available.

The Barrel

$12-22 | 123 E Elkhorn Ave

Sports bar with a dog-friendly patio right downtown. Good for watching games with your pup while enjoying pub food and cold beer. The outdoor seating is on the Elkhorn Ave sidewalk—prime real estate for dog visibility. Wings, burgers, and bar favorites done right. Casual and dog-welcoming.

Order: Wings, burger, draft beer. Dog perks: Sidewalk seating, high visibility, casual vibe.

Coffee on the Rocks

$4-10 | 510 Moraine Ave

Cozy coffee shop with dog-friendly outdoor seating. Solid espresso drinks and pastries. The location near the Fall River entrance makes it a convenient stop before or after RMNP. Less crowded than downtown coffee spots. Small dogs are sometimes welcome inside—ask first. Good vibes all around.

Order: Latte, fresh pastry. Dog perks: Quiet location, outdoor seating, sometimes inside too.

Tips for Dining with Dogs in Estes Park

Always call ahead on busy summer weekends. Patio space is limited, and some restaurants can only accommodate one or two dogs at a time. Weekday visits are much easier.

Bring water and a collapsible bowl. Not every restaurant has dog bowls ready, and altitude plus hiking means your dog needs to stay hydrated. Some spots offer treats, but don't count on it.

Estes Park is at 7,500 feet. If you or your dog aren't acclimated to altitude, take it easy on food and drink. Dogs can feel altitude sickness too—watch for unusual lethargy.

Note: Dogs are NOT allowed on most trails in Rocky Mountain National Park. The park is very strict about this. Plan your hikes accordingly—your pup will need to wait at pet-friendly spots or in pet-friendly accommodations during park visits.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are dogs allowed inside restaurants in Estes Park?

Colorado health code prohibits dogs inside restaurants. However, all restaurants on this list welcome dogs on their outdoor patios. Some may allow dogs inside during off-peak times—always ask first.

Which restaurant is most dog-friendly?

Rock Cut Brewing is the most actively dog-friendly, with water bowls, treats, and staff who genuinely love dogs. Lumpy Ridge Brewing has the most space for dogs to spread out.

Can I bring my dog to Rocky Mountain National Park?

Dogs are restricted to roadways, parking areas, picnic areas, and campgrounds. They are NOT allowed on any trails, meadows, or backcountry areas. This is strictly enforced.

Where can I hike with my dog near Estes Park?

Try Roosevelt National Forest trails outside RMNP—dogs are allowed on-leash. Hermit Park Open Space has dog-friendly trails. The Riverwalk in town is also dog-friendly.

Do restaurants provide water for dogs?

Some do, but bring your own collapsible bowl and water to be safe. Rock Cut Brewing and Latitude 105 typically have water bowls available.

Best time to visit dog-friendly patios?

Weekday afternoons are easiest. Summer weekends get crowded, and patio space is limited. Arriving early (before noon) or late (after 2pm) helps secure a spot.
