Estes Park Lakes

Alpine lakes in Rocky Mountain National Park

Rocky Mountain National Park contains some of Colorado's most spectacular alpine lakes. From easy-access gems to backcountry destinations, here are the must-see lakes near Estes Park.

Bear Lake

RMNP's Most Popular

Iconic alpine lake at 9,450 feet with easy 0.5-mile loop trail. Stunning reflections of Hallett Peak and Flattop Mountain.

Easy Access 0.5 mi

Dream Lake

Alpine Paradise

Stunning lake with Hallett Peak backdrop. 2.2-mile roundtrip from Bear Lake through forest and past Nymph Lake.

2.2 mi RT Moderate

Emerald Lake

Cirque Basin Gem

Spectacular lake in glacial cirque. 3.6-mile roundtrip from Bear Lake, passing Nymph and Dream lakes.

3.6 mi RT Moderate

Sprague Lake

Family Favorite

Accessible 0.5-mile loop with stunning sunrise views. Wheelchair accessible, great for families and photographers.

Easy Accessible

Sky Pond

Alpine Adventure

Remote alpine lake below Taylor Glacier and the Sharkstooth. 9-mile roundtrip with scramble through Timberline Falls.

9 mi RT Difficult

Lake Haiyaha

Boulder-Strewn Beauty

Dramatic lake surrounded by massive boulders. 4.4-mile roundtrip with less crowds than Dream Lake trail.

4.4 mi RT Moderate

Chasm Lake

Below Longs Peak

Dramatic lake in glacial cirque beneath Longs Peak Diamond Face. 8.4-mile roundtrip challenging hike.

8.4 mi RT Strenuous

Lake Estes

In-Town Recreation

Town lake with 3.75-mile paved trail, kayak rentals, and fishing. Marina with paddleboard and boat rentals.

Boating Fishing
