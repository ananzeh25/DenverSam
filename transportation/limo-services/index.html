Types of Limo Service

Choosing the right vehicle for your needs

Executive Sedan

Lincoln Town Car, Mercedes S-Class, BMW 7-Series, or Cadillac CT6.

  • Capacity: 2-3 passengers
  • Best for: Business travel, airport transfers, couples
  • Features: Leather interior, privacy, climate control
  • Luggage: 2-3 large bags

Executive SUV

Cadillac Escalade, Lincoln Navigator, Mercedes GLS, or Chevrolet Suburban.

  • Capacity: 4-6 passengers
  • Best for: Families, groups, ski trips with gear
  • Features: Spacious, AWD capability, luxury amenities
  • Luggage: 6-8 bags plus ski equipment

Stretch Limousine

Traditional stretch limos, Lincoln stretch, or specialty vehicles.

  • Capacity: 6-10 passengers
  • Best for: Weddings, prom, bachelor/bachelorette parties
  • Features: Bar, entertainment system, partition
  • Luggage: Limited trunk space

Party Bus / Sprinter

Mercedes Sprinter, party buses, or executive vans.

  • Capacity: 10-20+ passengers
  • Best for: Large groups, casino trips, corporate events
  • Features: Standing room, entertainment, bar service
  • Pricing: $200-400/hour typically

When Limo Service Makes Sense

Is it worth the premium?

Good Reasons to Book a Limo

  • Special occasions: Weddings, anniversaries, proposals
  • Business travel: Client meetings, executive arrivals
  • Groups splitting cost: 6 people = $100/person to Vail
  • Reliability matters: Critical flights, important events
  • Comfort priority: Long drives, arriving refreshed
  • No driving after drinking: Casino trips, wine tours

When to Consider Alternatives

  • Solo budget travel: Shared shuttle is $65-85 vs $400+
  • Flexibility needed: Rental car offers more freedom
  • Short distances: Uber/Lyft for Denver metro trips

The Group Math

Limo pricing often makes sense for groups:

  • 2 people to Vail: $250/person (expensive)
  • 4 people to Vail: $125/person (reasonable)
  • 6 people to Vail: $85/person (comparable to shuttle)

What's Included

Understanding limo service amenities

Typically Included

  • Professional, licensed chauffeur
  • Meet-and-greet at airport (with name sign)
  • Flight tracking and delay accommodation
  • Bottled water
  • Phone chargers
  • Luggage assistance
  • Wi-Fi (many vehicles)

Often Extra

  • Gratuity (15-20% standard)
  • Wait time beyond included (usually 1 hour at airport)
  • Additional stops
  • Child car seats
  • Special requests (champagne, decorations)

Booking Tips

  • Book 1-2 weeks ahead for regular service
  • Book 1-2 months ahead for holidays and weddings
  • Confirm all-inclusive pricing upfront
  • Get written confirmation of vehicle type
  • Provide flight details for airport pickups
  • Ask about cancellation policies

Frequently Asked Questions

Colorado limo services

How much does a limo from Denver Airport to Vail cost?

Expect $500-700 for a luxury SUV or $700-900 for a stretch limo. Sedans start around $400. Prices include the 2+ hour drive. Split among 4-6 people, it becomes comparable to individual shuttle tickets.

Is gratuity included in limo pricing?

Usually not. Plan on 15-20% gratuity for your driver. Some companies include it for weddings or packages—always ask when booking.

Can I book a limo for a casino trip?

Yes, popular option for Black Hawk/Central City trips. Round-trip with wait time runs $300-500. Great for groups who want to drink without worrying about driving mountain roads home.

What if my flight is delayed?

Reputable limo services track flights and adjust pickup times automatically. Most include reasonable wait time (30-60 minutes) for delays. Confirm this policy when booking.

How far in advance should I book?

1-2 weeks for regular travel, 4-8 weeks for holidays and ski season weekends, 2-3 months for weddings or special events. Last-minute bookings possible but vehicle selection limited.
