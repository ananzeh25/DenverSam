Denver to Black Hawk Transportation

How do I get from Denver to Black Hawk? Free or cheap casino shuttles are the best option ($0-30 round trip). Driving takes 50-60 minutes (40 miles) via I-70 and Highway 119 through Clear Creek Canyon. Rideshare costs $60-90. Unless you need maximum flexibility, take a shuttle and skip the mountain driving.

Route Overview

Distance: 40 miles
Drive time: 50-60 minutes
Route: I-70 West to Highway 119, through Clear Creek Canyon
Elevation: 5,280 feet (Denver) to 8,500 feet (Black Hawk)

Black Hawk is Colorado's largest casino town, tucked into a narrow canyon in the mountains west of Denver. The winding Highway 119 through Clear Creek Canyon is scenic but slow and can be treacherous in winter. Most visitors opt for casino shuttles—they're cheap, convenient, and mean you don't have to worry about driving after gambling.

Transportation Options

Casino Shuttles (Recommended)

Cost: Free to $30 round trip
Time: 60-75 minutes
Best for: Almost everyone

Several casinos offer shuttle service from Denver:

  • Ameristar Casino: Free shuttle from Denver Tech Center and other locations
  • Monarch Casino: Shuttle service from multiple Denver pickup points
  • Ace Express: $20-30 round trip, multiple pickup locations
  • Black Hawk Shuttle: $20-30 round trip from various Denver spots

Shuttles typically run every few hours with more frequent service on weekends. Check casino websites for current schedules and pickup locations.

Driving

Cost: ~$10 gas
Time: 50-60 minutes
Best for: Those who want flexibility, won't drink

Take I-70 West to Exit 244, then Highway 6 to Highway 119 through Clear Creek Canyon. Free parking at all casinos. Highway 119 has many curves and can be icy in winter—drive carefully. Consider whether you want to drive after gambling and potentially drinking.

Rideshare (Uber/Lyft)

Cost: $60-90 one-way
Time: 50-60 minutes
Best for: Groups splitting costs, spontaneous trips

Works from Denver but expensive for one person. Late night availability from Black Hawk is limited—drivers are scarce in the mountains after midnight. Plan your return trip in advance.

Casino Shuttle Details

Most casino shuttles offer:

  • Comfortable coaches: Not cramped vans
  • Multiple pickups: Denver Tech Center, downtown, various park-and-rides
  • Flexible return: Multiple departure times from Black Hawk
  • No reservations needed: First-come basis (though booking helps)

The smart play: Take a shuttle up, gamble responsibly, enjoy drinks without worrying about driving, and ride home. The shuttle cost is often offset by the free drinks and lack of parking/gas concerns.

Tips

  • Don't drink and drive: Highway 119 is challenging sober—don't risk it after drinking
  • Central City: Adjacent to Black Hawk with more casinos, connected by free shuttle
  • Winter caution: Clear Creek Canyon gets icy; shuttles handle the driving
  • Late returns: Know the last shuttle time; rideshare availability drops late
  • Free parking: If you drive, all casinos have free parking

Frequently Asked Questions

Which casino has the best free shuttle?

Ameristar typically has the most convenient schedule and pickup locations. Check their website for current routes. Monarch also offers good service.

Can I get an Uber back from Black Hawk at 2am?

Probably not reliably. Late-night rideshare availability in Black Hawk is very limited. Use the casino shuttle for returns or arrange transportation in advance.

Is the drive to Black Hawk difficult?

Highway 119 through Clear Creek Canyon is winding with many curves. It's manageable in good weather but can be challenging in winter or at night. Many visitors prefer shuttles for this reason.
