Breckenridge Hiking Trails

14ers, alpine lakes, and above-treeline adventures

Breckenridge offers world-class hiking from the base of town at 9,600 feet to 14,000+ foot peaks. From accessible alpine trails to challenging 14er summits, here are the essential Breck hikes.

Quandary Peak

Colorado's Easiest 14er

6.75-mile roundtrip to 14,265 feet. The most accessible 14er for beginners, though still challenging.

14er 6.75 mi

Mohawk Lakes Trail

Alpine Lakes & Waterfalls

7-mile roundtrip to three stunning alpine lakes with waterfalls and mining history. Breck's best hike.

7 mi RT Moderate

McCullough Gulch

Waterfall Wonder

4-mile roundtrip to a beautiful waterfall and alpine basin. Great for families and accessible scenery.

4 mi RT Easy-Moderate

Spruce Creek Trail

Crystal Lakes Access

Popular trail to Crystal Lake or Lower Crystal Lake. Wildflowers in summer, multiple destinations.

5 mi RT Moderate

Boreas Pass

Historic Railroad

Easy walk on historic railroad grade with wildflowers and mountain views. Great for fall colors.

Various Easy

Blue River Recreation Path

Paved Town Trail

Paved path connecting Breckenridge to Frisco along the Blue River. Easy walk, bike, or stroller route.

Paved Easy
