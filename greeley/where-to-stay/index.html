Where to Stay in Greeley

Quick Info

Options: Primarily chain hotels. Best areas: Near US-34/Highway 85 for convenience. Peak demand: Stampede (late June-July 4th), UNC events. Alternative: Fort Collins (30 min) has more variety.

Greeley's lodging scene consists mainly of national chain hotels clustered near major highways. It's functional rather than charming - you'll find clean, affordable rooms but limited boutique or unique options. For most visitors, the hotels serve their purpose well.

Where to Stay

Highway 34 Corridor: Most hotels cluster along US-34 (10th Street). This area offers easy access to downtown, UNC, and major routes. Chain options include Hampton Inn, Holiday Inn Express, Fairfield Inn, and similar brands.

Near Downtown: Fewer hotel options directly downtown, but staying on Highway 34 puts you within a short drive of downtown restaurants and attractions.

Near Island Grove: Some hotels are convenient to Island Grove Regional Park, useful during the Stampede or other events.

What's Available

Chain Hotels: The dominant option. Hampton Inn, Holiday Inn Express, Best Western, Comfort Inn, Fairfield Inn, and others. Reliable and predictable.

Budget Motels: Several budget options exist. Quality varies - check recent reviews.

Vacation Rentals: Limited Airbnb/VRBO options compared to tourist destinations, but some exist.

Camping: Limited options in Greeley itself. Pawnee National Grassland allows dispersed camping for those seeking outdoor accommodation.

During Stampede

The Greeley Stampede (late June through July 4th) fills hotels and raises prices. If you're visiting during Stampede:

  • Book months in advance
  • Expect higher rates
  • Consider Fort Collins or Loveland as alternatives
  • Some hotels may require minimum stays

Alternative Cities

If Greeley hotels don't meet your needs:

Fort Collins (30 minutes west): More hotel variety, vibrant downtown, additional breweries. Good option if Greeley is booked or you want more nightlife.

Loveland (20 minutes southwest): Between Greeley and the mountains. Some hotel options and closer to Big Thompson Canyon.

Denver area (1 hour south): Endless options if you don't mind the commute.

Tips

Book ahead for events: Beyond Stampede, UNC graduation, football games, and events at Island Grove can fill hotels.

Compare prices: Check hotel websites directly against aggregators. Sometimes booking direct offers better rates or perks.

Free breakfast: Most chain hotels include breakfast - factor this into value calculations.

Parking: Free parking is standard at Greeley hotels.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Greeley hotels expensive?

Generally affordable compared to Denver or resort towns. Rates spike during Stampede and major events.

Should I stay in Greeley or Fort Collins?

Fort Collins has more variety and nightlife. Stay in Greeley if you're focused on Stampede, Pawnee Grassland, or want proximity to WeldWerks.

Are there nice hotels in Greeley?

The nicer chain hotels (Hampton Inn, Fairfield Inn) are well-maintained. True luxury or boutique options are limited.

How early should I book for Stampede?

Several months ahead for best selection. Hotels start filling in spring for the July 4th event.
