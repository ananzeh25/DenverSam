University of Northern Colorado

Quick Info

Founded: 1889. Enrollment: ~9,000 students. Athletics: Bears (Big Sky Conference). Known for: Education programs, performing arts, affordable tuition. Location: Adjacent to downtown Greeley.

The University of Northern Colorado brings college-town energy to Greeley. Founded as a teachers' college, UNC remains known for its education programs while offering a full range of undergraduate and graduate degrees. The campus sits adjacent to downtown, making it easy to combine a campus visit with exploring Greeley's restaurants and breweries.

Campus Highlights

Historic Buildings: The older parts of campus feature attractive brick buildings dating to UNC's early years. Carter Hall and Gunter Hall anchor the historic core.

Michener Library: Named for James Michener, the Pulitzer Prize-winning author who taught at UNC. The library houses his papers and memorabilia.

Campus Commons: Green spaces and plazas give the campus a walkable, pleasant feel. The campus is compact enough to explore on foot.

Performing Arts

UNC has strong music, theater, and dance programs. Public performances throughout the academic year include:

  • Orchestra, band, and choral concerts
  • Theater productions at Norton Theatre
  • Dance performances
  • Guest artist recitals

Check the UNC arts calendar for performance schedules. Tickets are typically affordable, and the quality rivals professional productions.

Bears Athletics

UNC competes in the Big Sky Conference (FCS football) and other Division I conferences. Football games at Nottingham Field bring game-day energy to campus. Basketball, volleyball, and other sports have dedicated followings.

Attending a Bears game is an affordable way to experience college athletics without the crowds and prices of major programs.

Visiting Campus

Prospective students: Admissions offers campus tours throughout the year. Schedule through the university website.

Casual visitors: The campus is open to walk through. Parking is available in visitor lots (check signage for permit requirements during academic year).

Summer: Campus is quieter but still accessible. Some facilities may have reduced hours.

What's Nearby

The campus location makes it easy to combine with other Greeley activities:

  • Downtown Greeley - restaurants, shops, Lincoln Park
  • Coffee shops - student hangouts near campus
  • Breweries - WeldWerks and others

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I visit campus without a tour?

Yes, the campus is open to walk through. Visitor parking is available, though you may need a permit during the academic year.

Is UNC a good school?

UNC is well-regarded, especially for education, nursing, and performing arts. It offers good value with lower tuition than many comparable schools.

Are there things to do on campus for non-students?

Performing arts events, athletic games, and public lectures are open to the community. The Michener Library is worth a visit for book lovers.

How big is the campus?

About 240 acres, compact enough to walk across in 15-20 minutes. It's easy to explore on foot.
