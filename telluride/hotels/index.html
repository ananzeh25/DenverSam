Where to Stay in Telluride

Hotels, lodges, and rentals in Colorado's box canyon

Telluride offers two distinct lodging experiences. Historic Telluride has Victorian character, walkable restaurants, and Main Street charm. Mountain Village delivers ski-in/ski-out convenience with modern condos and slope access. The free gondola connects both in 13 minutes, so you can't really go wrong.

Vacation Rentals

Condos, cabins, and private homes

VRBO

Private Home Rentals

Best for groups and families. Full kitchens save money on dining. Many properties have hot tubs and stunning views. Book months ahead for ski season.

CONDO

Ski-In/Ski-Out Condos

Mountain Village condos with slope access. Ranges from studios to multi-bedroom units. Managed by local property companies with ski valet service.

CABIN

Mountain Cabins

Secluded properties outside town. Best for summer when 4WD roads are clear. Dramatic settings with privacy. Less practical for ski access.

Lodging Tips

What you need to know

Telluride or Mountain Village?

Choose Telluride for restaurants, nightlife, and historic character. Choose Mountain Village for ski-in/ski-out convenience and modern amenities. The free gondola makes either choice work. First-timers often prefer downtown Telluride.

How far in advance should I book?

Peak ski weeks (Christmas, Presidents' Day): 6-12 months. Regular winter weekends: 2-3 months. Summer/festival weekends: 3-6 months. Film Festival: 12+ months. Shoulder seasons allow more flexibility.

How expensive is lodging?

Comparable to Aspen. Expect $300-500/night for mid-range hotels in ski season, $500-1000+ for luxury. Summer is 30-50% cheaper. Vacation rentals can be more economical for groups splitting costs.

What about staying outside Telluride?

Montrose (65 miles) and Ridgway (40 miles) offer budget alternatives. You'll need a car and add driving time, but can save significantly. Good option for festival weekends when Telluride fills up.

Is there parking?

Telluride has limited street parking (free in summer, restricted in winter). Many hotels charge $25-50/night for parking. Mountain Village has structured parking. Consider whether you need a car at all, the gondola provides great transit.
