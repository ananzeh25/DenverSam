Telluride Skiing

World-class terrain in Colorado's most scenic setting

Telluride Ski Resort delivers what serious skiers crave: challenging terrain, stunning scenery, and fewer crowds than the I-70 corridor resorts. With 2,000+ skiable acres and a 4,425-foot vertical drop, it rivals any mountain in Colorado. The trade-off is getting here takes effort, but that remoteness keeps the lift lines short.

Resort Stats

The numbers that matter

ACRES

2,000+ Skiable Acres

Spread across two distinct areas connected by the free gondola. Front side terrain above Telluride, back bowls accessible from Mountain Village.

VERT

4,425 Ft Vertical

Base at 8,725 feet, summit at 13,150 feet. One of the highest lift-served summits in North America. Expect thin air and deep snow.

TRAILS

148 Trails

23% beginner, 36% intermediate, 41% advanced/expert. The terrain breakdown skews expert, but there's plenty for all levels.

SNOW

280" Annual Snowfall

San Juan Mountains deliver consistent powder. Storm cycles often bring multi-foot dumps. Season runs late November through early April.

Signature Terrain

Where the experts go

EXPERT

Revelation Bowl

Telluride's crown jewel. 733 acres of hike-to terrain opened in 2008. Steep chutes, cliffs, and wide-open powder fields. For experts only.

EXPERT

Gold Hill

Above-treeline expert terrain with 12,260-foot access. Steep pitches, natural features, and some of the best snow on the mountain.

EXPERT

Palmyra Peak

Hike-to summit at 13,320 feet. Extreme terrain requiring avalanche gear and partners. Telluride's ultimate challenge.

INTER

Prospect Bowl

440 acres of intermediate-friendly terrain. Wide groomers and gentle glades perfect for building confidence before tackling steeper runs.

BEGIN

Meadows

Beginner paradise in Mountain Village. Gentle terrain, dedicated learning area, and ski school base. Separate from expert traffic.

BUMP

Plunge

The locals' bump run. Steep, sustained moguls with great snow. Accessible from Lift 9, no hiking required.

Practical Info

Planning your ski trip

How much are lift tickets?

Window rates run $200-250+ during peak season. Book in advance online for 10-15% savings. Epic Pass and Epic Day Pass provide the best value for multi-day visits.

When is the best time to ski Telluride?

January through March offers the most consistent snow. February typically has the deepest base. Late March brings spring skiing with warmer temps. Avoid Christmas and Presidents' Day weeks if you hate crowds.

Should I stay in Telluride or Mountain Village?

Mountain Village offers ski-in/ski-out convenience and modern condos. Historic Telluride has more character, restaurants, and nightlife. The free gondola connects both in 13 minutes.

How does Telluride compare to Vail or Aspen?

Telluride has better expert terrain than Vail and fewer crowds than either. The scenery is arguably the best in Colorado. Trade-off: it's harder to reach and has fewer dining/nightlife options than Aspen.

Is there night skiing?

No night skiing at Telluride. Lifts typically run 9am-4pm. The free gondola operates until midnight, so you can still get between town and Mountain Village after dark.
