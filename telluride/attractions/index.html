Telluride Attractions

Must-see experiences in Colorado's box canyon paradise

Telluride's setting does most of the work. Surrounded by 13,000-foot peaks on three sides, the town sits in a box canyon so dramatic that simply walking down Main Street feels like an attraction. But beyond the views, you'll find a free gondola, waterfalls accessible from town, and a historic district that's earned National Landmark status.

GONDOLA

Free Gondola

North America's only free public gondola. 13-minute ride connecting historic Telluride to Mountain Village, rising 1,000 feet with panoramic views of the San Juan Mountains.

Full Guide
WATERFALL

Bridal Veil Falls

Colorado's tallest free-falling waterfall at 365 feet. Visible from town, hikeable via a steep 4WD road. The historic power station at the top still generates electricity.

HIKE

Bear Creek Falls

2.4-mile trail starting right from town. Moderate climb through aspen forest to a 80-foot cascade. The most popular hike in Telluride for good reason.

Hiking Guide
TOWN

Historic Main Street

National Historic Landmark District with Victorian-era buildings. Galleries, restaurants, and boutiques line Colorado Avenue with mountain views in every direction.

Main Street Guide
MUSEUM

Telluride Historical Museum

Mining history, Ute heritage, and ski culture in the 1893 Miners' Hospital. Learn about the town's transformation from gold rush camp to resort destination.

VIEWS

Box Canyon

The dramatic geological formation that defines Telluride. Three sides of 13,000+ foot peaks create one of Colorado's most spectacular natural amphitheaters.

VILLAGE

Mountain Village

Modern ski base 1,000 feet above town. European-style pedestrian plaza with hotels, restaurants, and shops. Connected by the free gondola.

FILM

Telluride Film Festival

Labor Day weekend brings one of the world's most prestigious film festivals. Oscar contenders premiere here annually. Celebrity sightings guaranteed.

Festival Guide
JEEP

4x4 Trails

Imogene Pass, Black Bear Road, and Ophir Pass offer legendary off-road adventures. Some of Colorado's most challenging and scenic 4WD routes.

Jeep Trail Guide
SKI

Ski Resort

2,000+ skiable acres with 148 trails. Expert terrain at Revelation Bowl and Gold Hill rivals anything in Colorado. Relatively uncrowded compared to I-70 resorts.
