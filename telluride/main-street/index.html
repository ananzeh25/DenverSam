Telluride Main Street

Victorian mining town meets mountain resort

Colorado Avenue, known locally as Main Street, is the heart of historic Telluride. This National Historic Landmark District preserves the Victorian architecture from the town's 1880s mining boom while housing modern galleries, restaurants, and boutiques. With 13,000-foot peaks visible at either end, it might be the most dramatically situated main street in Colorado.

The entire 12-block downtown is walkable in 20 minutes, but you'll want longer to explore the shops, grab coffee, and soak in the atmosphere. Most businesses are locally owned, no chain stores here.

Historic Highlights

Buildings that tell the story

1895

New Sheridan Hotel

The grand dame of Main Street. Three-story brick hotel with original Victorian details. Chop House restaurant and historic bar. Where celebrities stay during Film Festival.

1891

Sheridan Opera House

Restored 1913 opera house hosting concerts, films, and events. Intimate 240-seat venue with incredible acoustics. Check the schedule for your visit.

1893

Telluride Historical Museum

Housed in the original Miners' Hospital. Mining artifacts, Ute history, and ski heritage. Essential context for understanding how Telluride evolved.

1899

San Miguel County Courthouse

Classic Western courthouse still in use. Beautiful architecture and prominent location. Worth a photo even if you're not appearing before a judge.

Shopping & Galleries

Local boutiques and art

ART

Art Galleries

A dozen galleries line Main Street featuring Western art, contemporary works, and photography. Gallery walks happen during festivals. Most welcome browsers.

GEAR

Outdoor Shops

Ski shops, hiking gear, and bike rentals. Jagged Edge and Telluride Sports cover equipment needs. Rentals available for all seasons.

STYLE

Boutiques

Mountain-chic clothing, jewelry, and home goods. Between the Covers bookshop is a local institution. No chains, all independent.

GIFT

Colorado Souvenirs

Local crafts, Telluride-branded gear, and artisan foods. Quality options beyond typical tourist shops. Look for locally made items.

Main Street Essentials

Good to know

PARK

Town Park

At the east end of Main Street. Festival venue, playground, picnic areas. Free summer concerts in the gazebo. Where locals gather.

WALK

Walking Distance

12 blocks from the gondola to Town Park. Flat terrain, easy walking. Everything in historic Telluride is accessible on foot.

FREE

Free Bus

The Galloping Goose runs a free shuttle through town. Convenient for tired legs or carrying ski gear. Runs frequently during peak seasons.

VIEW

Mountain Views

Look east for Ajax Peak and Bridal Veil Falls. Look west toward the gondola and San Sophia Ridge. Views in every direction.
