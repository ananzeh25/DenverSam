Montrose Airport to Telluride

The Gateway to Southwest Colorado

Quick Facts: MTJ to Telluride

90 minutes through spectacular San Juan scenery

Airport Code: MTJ (Montrose Regional Airport)

Distance to Telluride: 65 miles

Drive Time: 75-90 minutes

Route: Highway 550 south to Ridgway, Highway 62 west to Placerville, Highway 145 south to Telluride

Why Montrose? Telluride is remote—330 miles from Denver. Flying to Montrose cuts that to a scenic 90-minute drive through the San Juan Mountains.

Why Fly to Montrose

The practical choice for Telluride

Montrose Regional Airport is Telluride's main gateway. While Telluride has its own airport (TEX), Montrose offers more reliable service and better connections.

Flight Options from Montrose

  • Winter season: Direct flights from Denver, Dallas, Houston, Chicago, Phoenix, LA, Newark
  • Summer: Reduced schedule, Denver connections reliable
  • Airlines: United, American, Delta (seasonal)

Transportation Options

Getting from MTJ to Telluride

Shared Shuttles ($55-80/person)

The most popular option. Several companies run scheduled service from MTJ to Telluride.

  • Telluride Express – Primary shuttle operator
  • Mountain Limo – Shuttle and private options
  • Departures timed to flight arrivals
  • Service to Telluride town and Mountain Village
  • Scenic ride through San Juan Mountains

Private Car/SUV ($250-350)

Direct service for families or groups. The per-person cost becomes reasonable with 4+ passengers.

  • Door-to-door service to your lodging
  • No waiting for other passengers
  • Stops possible (Ridgway, Ouray)
  • Child seats available on request

Rental Car ($70-120/day)

Worth considering if you want to explore southwest Colorado. The drive from Montrose to Telluride is spectacular.

  • All major rental companies at MTJ
  • AWD recommended in winter
  • Beautiful drive through Ridgway and San Juans
  • Easy day trips to Ouray, Silverton, Durango
  • Free gondola means you don't need car in Telluride

The Scenic Drive

The route from Montrose to Telluride passes through some of Colorado's most beautiful country:

  1. South on Highway 550 through Ridgway
  2. West on Highway 62 with San Juan mountain views
  3. South on Highway 145 into Telluride box canyon

Optional detour: Continue on 550 to Ouray for hot springs—adds 30 minutes but worth it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Montrose Airport transportation

How long is the drive from Montrose Airport to Telluride?

About 75-90 minutes for the 65-mile drive. The route through Ridgway and the San Juan Mountains is scenic but winding. Winter conditions may add time.

Should I fly to Montrose or Telluride?

Montrose for most travelers. More flights, better reliability, lower costs. Telluride airport is convenient but has limited service, weather issues, and expensive fares.

Is there Uber from Montrose Airport to Telluride?

Very limited. Uber/Lyft coverage in this rural area is sparse. Book a shuttle or private car for reliable service. Don't count on rideshare for a 65-mile mountain trip.

Do I need a car in Telluride?

No. Telluride is small and walkable. A free gondola connects town to Mountain Village. Free buses run throughout both areas. Most visitors don't need a car once there.

Can I stop in Ouray on the way to Telluride?

If you rent a car or book a private transfer that allows stops. Ouray is slightly off the main route but worth the 30-minute detour for hot springs. Shuttles typically go direct.
