Telluride Free Gondola

North America's only free public gondola

The Telluride gondola is more than transportation, it's a signature experience. This 13-minute ride connects historic Telluride to Mountain Village, climbing 1,000 vertical feet with panoramic San Juan Mountain views. And it's completely free. Year-round, every day, for everyone.

The gondola opened in 1996 as part of the ski resort expansion, but Telluride made the unusual decision to keep it free for public use. It runs until midnight in summer, making it possible to dine in Mountain Village and gondola back to town. In winter, skiers use it to access both base areas.

Gondola Stations

Three stops along the route

TOWN

Telluride Station

Elevation: 8,725 ft
Located at the north end of Oak Street in historic Telluride. Easy walk from Main Street and most downtown lodging. Parking available nearby.

MID

San Sophia Station

Elevation: 10,540 ft
Mid-mountain station with ski access in winter. Stunning viewpoint for non-skiers. Some trails accessible from here in summer. Often passed through without stopping.

VILLAGE

Mountain Village Station

Elevation: 9,545 ft
Upper terminus at Mountain Village plaza. Steps from hotels, restaurants, and ski lifts. Allred's restaurant is a short walk. European-style pedestrian village atmosphere.

Gondola Stats

The numbers

TIME

13 Minutes

Total ride time from Telluride to Mountain Village. Cabins load continuously, so there's rarely a wait beyond a few minutes.

VERT

1,790 Ft Rise

From Telluride station to San Sophia station. The views open up dramatically as you clear the treeline.

MILE

2.5 Miles

Total cable length covering the full route. One of the longest free gondola rides in the world.

CAB

8 Passengers

Each cabin holds 8 people. Bikes and dogs are allowed. Ski/snowboard storage available in winter.

Practical Info

What you need to know

What are the operating hours?

Summer (late May-early October): 7am to midnight. Winter: 7am to midnight during ski season. Spring/Fall shoulder seasons may have reduced hours. Check tellurideskiresort.com for current schedule.

Is it really free?

Yes, 100% free. No tickets, no passes, no catches. It's funded by local taxes and the ski resort as a public service. One of the unique things that makes Telluride special.

Can I bring my bike?

Yes. Bikes are allowed in the gondola cabins. Mountain bikers use it to access trails from Mountain Village. Road bikers can make a loop with Hwy 145 and Last Dollar Road.

What about dogs?

Dogs are welcome on leash. The gondola is dog-friendly year-round. Many trails from Mountain Village and San Sophia are also dog-friendly.

What if I'm afraid of heights?

The cabins are enclosed with windows. Some sections cross open terrain with significant exposure, especially near San Sophia. If heights are a serious concern, you can drive between Telluride and Mountain Village instead.

Best time of day to ride?

Sunset rides are spectacular. Morning offers the clearest views before afternoon clouds build. Avoid 3-5pm during ski season when skiers are heading down. Late-night summer rides are surprisingly magical.
