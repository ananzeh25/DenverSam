Telluride Restaurants

Mountain dining from casual to world-class

For a town of 2,500 people, Telluride punches well above its weight in dining. The resort crowd demands quality, and the restaurant scene delivers. Expect prices comparable to Aspen, but also flavors that match the altitude. Reservations are essential during ski season and festival weekends.

Fine Dining

Special occasion restaurants

$$$

Allred's

The gondola destination restaurant at 10,551 feet. Contemporary American with panoramic San Juan views. Ride up for sunset cocktails even if you don't dine. Reserve well ahead.

$$$

Alpino Vino

European-style hut dining on the ski mountain. Intimate wine-paired dinners require a snowcat ride up. Most romantic restaurant in Telluride. Winter only.

$$$

Chop House

Prime steaks and fresh seafood in a refined mountain setting. New Sheridan Hotel location with historic character. Classic American fine dining done right.

$$$

221 South Oak

Creative American cuisine in a Victorian house. Chef-driven menu changes seasonally. More intimate than Allred's with equally impressive food.

Dining Tips

What you need to know

Do I need reservations?

Absolutely, for any sit-down dinner during ski season, weekends, and festival periods. Book fine dining spots a week or more in advance. Casual spots might squeeze you in at the bar.

How expensive is dining in Telluride?

Comparable to Aspen. Expect $20-30 for casual lunches, $50-80 per person for mid-range dinners, and $100+ at fine dining spots. Coffee and baked goods are the best values.

Telluride or Mountain Village for dinner?

Historic Telluride has more options and character. Mountain Village has convenience for slopeside lodging and Allred's. The gondola runs until midnight, so both are accessible.

What about apres-ski drinks?

There 10 at the base of Lift 8, Last Dollar Saloon, and Smuggler's Brewpub are local favorites. Allred's has sunset cocktails with views if you ride the gondola up.
