Telluride Festivals

Colorado's premier destination for film, music, and culture

Telluride transforms into a different town each summer weekend. The box canyon becomes an amphitheater for world-class film premieres, legendary bluegrass performances, and a rotating calendar of cultural events. These festivals are why many visitors first discover Telluride, and why they keep coming back.

Major Festivals

The events that define Telluride

FILM

Telluride Film Festival

Labor Day Weekend (August/September)
One of the world's most prestigious film festivals. Oscar contenders premiere here before anywhere else. Filmmakers and celebrities walk the same streets as festivalgoers. Passes sell out months in advance.

MUSIC

Telluride Bluegrass Festival

Late June (4 days)
The granddaddy of Colorado music festivals since 1974. Sam Bush, Bela Fleck, and legends play Town Park. Camping passes are coveted. The unofficial start of Telluride's summer season.

MUSIC

Blues & Brews

Mid-September (3 days)
Blues, funk, and soul with Colorado craft beer. Town Park venue with mountain backdrop. More accessible than Bluegrass, equally memorable. Great way to catch fall colors.

JAZZ

Telluride Jazz Festival

Early August (3 days)
Jazz, funk, and soul in an intimate mountain setting. Town Park and Mountain Village venues. Smaller crowds than the bigger festivals, but equally talented lineups.

More Festivals

Year-round celebrations

YOGA

Telluride Yoga Festival

July
Four days of yoga, meditation, and wellness workshops. Mountain Village and town venues. Classes for all levels with renowned instructors.

WINE

Telluride Wine Festival

Late June
Grand tastings, winemaker dinners, and seminars. One of the top wine events in Colorado. Pairs perfectly with the mountain setting.

SHROOM

Telluride Mushroom Festival

Late August
Mycology meets mountain town. Forays, workshops, and cooking demos. One of the largest fungi festivals in North America. Surprisingly popular.

COMEDY

Telluride Comedy Festival

August
Stand-up comedy in intimate mountain venues. Mix of established names and rising stars. Multiple shows across town throughout the weekend.

ART

Telluride Plein Air

September
Outdoor painting competition in one of Colorado's most scenic settings. Watch artists work, purchase originals, attend workshops.

HORROR

Telluride Horror Show

October
Genre film festival for horror, sci-fi, and fantasy fans. Independent and international films. Perfect timing for Halloween and fall colors.

Festival Tips

How to do Telluride festivals right

How far in advance should I book?

For Film Festival and Bluegrass, book lodging 6-12 months ahead. Passes sell out even earlier. Blues & Brews and smaller festivals allow 2-3 months lead time. Last-minute is nearly impossible for the big ones.

Where do people stay during festivals?

Town fills up completely. Many festivalgoers camp at Town Park (for music festivals) or stay in Montrose, Ridgway, or Ouray and drive in. Mountain Village condos offer an alternative. VRBO rentals book a year out for Film Festival.

Can I attend Film Festival without a pass?

Limited. Some outdoor screenings in Elks Park are free. The town atmosphere is worth experiencing even without theater access. But the real festival requires a pass or badge.

What should I bring?

Layers for all festivals (mountain weather changes fast). Sunscreen for daytime events. Comfortable walking shoes. Festival camping requires cold-weather gear even in summer. Nights drop into the 40s.

Are there kid-friendly festivals?

Bluegrass and Wine Festival welcome families. Film Festival is adult-focused. The free gondola and hiking keep kids entertained between events. Town Park has playground space.
