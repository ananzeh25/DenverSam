Telluride Summer Activities

Mountain adventures beyond ski season

Summer in Telluride might be better than winter. The snow melts to reveal wildflower meadows, alpine lakes, and endless outdoor possibilities. The festival calendar fills every weekend with music, film, and culture. And the free gondola still runs, connecting town to Mountain Village's hiking and biking trails.

Summer season runs roughly late May through early October, with peak conditions July through September. Expect afternoon thunderstorms, cool evenings, and some of the best mountain weather in Colorado.

Outdoor Adventures

Get into the mountains

HIKE

Hiking

Trails to waterfalls, alpine lakes, and 14,000-foot peaks. Bear Creek and Bridal Veil Falls are accessible from town. Blue Lake and Hope Lake reward longer efforts.

Hiking Guide
BIKE

Mountain Biking

Lift-served downhill from Mountain Village. Cross-country trails through alpine meadows. The free gondola carries bikes. Rentals available in town.

FISH

Fly Fishing

San Miguel River runs through town with brown and rainbow trout. Guided trips access private water. Mountain lakes offer backcountry fishing adventures.

JEEP

Jeep Tours

Imogene Pass, Black Bear Road, and legendary 4x4 routes. Guided tours or rent your own vehicle. Mining history and alpine scenery above treeline.

Jeep Trail Guide
CLIMB

Via Ferrata

Protected climbing route on the mountain. Iron rungs, cables, and bridges with exposure. Guided tours required. Thrilling views for those comfortable with heights.

GOLF

Golf

Telluride Golf Club offers 18 holes at 9,500 feet. Mountain views on every hole. The altitude adds distance to every shot. Tee times book up in peak season.

Water Activities

Rivers and lakes

PADDLE

Stand-Up Paddleboarding

Alta Lakes and other mountain lakes offer calm water. Rentals available in town. Early morning provides the glassiest conditions before afternoon winds.

RAFT

River Rafting

San Miguel and Dolores Rivers offer Class II-IV whitewater. Day trips from Telluride. Best flows in June and early July during snowmelt.

KAYAK

Kayaking

Whitewater kayaking on local rivers. Town Park has a kayak course on the San Miguel. Lake kayaking at Alta Lakes and Trout Lake.

In-Town Activities

No car required

GONDOLA

Free Gondola

Ride to Mountain Village and back just for the views. Runs until midnight in summer. Bring wine for a sunset ride. It's free and spectacular.

Gondola Guide
FEST

Festivals

Bluegrass, Film Festival, Blues & Brews, and more. Almost every summer weekend features some event. Plan around or around to avoid festival crowds.

YOGA

Yoga & Wellness

Mountain yoga classes with canyon views. Spa treatments in town and Mountain Village. The altitude and setting make wellness feel more meaningful.

ART

Galleries & Shopping

Main Street galleries showcase Western and contemporary art. Local boutiques with mountain style. Bookshops, gear stores, and artisan goods.

Summer Tips

What you need to know

What's the weather like in summer?

Highs in the 70s-80s, lows in the 40s-50s. Afternoon thunderstorms are common July-August. Mornings are typically clear and sunny. Bring layers for all conditions.

When is peak wildflower season?

Mid-July through early August typically sees the best wildflower displays. Higher elevation meadows bloom later. Trails to alpine lakes offer the most dramatic colors.

Is summer less expensive than winter?

Generally yes, except during major festivals. Lodging rates drop 30-50% outside festival weekends. Restaurants are less crowded. The town has a more relaxed vibe.

Do I need a car in summer?

Not necessarily. Hiking trails leave from town, the gondola accesses Mountain Village, and the free bus serves local areas. A car helps for Jeep trails and accessing distant trailheads.

What about the altitude?

Town sits at 8,750 feet, trails go higher. Take it easy the first day, hydrate constantly, and avoid alcohol initially. Altitude sickness is real and can ruin a trip if ignored.
