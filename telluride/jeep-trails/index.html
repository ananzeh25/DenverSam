Telluride Jeep Trails

Colorado's most legendary 4x4 routes

The San Juan Mountains around Telluride offer some of the most dramatic off-road driving in North America. Old mining roads climb to 13,000+ feet, cross knife-edge passes, and descend switchbacks that test both vehicle and driver. These aren't trails for beginners, but for experienced off-roaders, they're bucket-list worthy.

Season typically runs late June through September, depending on snowmelt. Most trails require high-clearance 4WD with low-range gearing. Afternoon thunderstorms are common; plan to be off exposed passes by noon.

Legendary Passes

The famous ones

EPIC

Imogene Pass

13,114 ft | Moderate-Difficult
Telluride to Ouray via the second-highest pass road in Colorado. 17 miles of alpine scenery, mining ruins, and exposure. The classic San Juan crossing. Allow 3-4 hours.

EXPERT

Black Bear Pass

12,840 ft | Extreme
One of Colorado's most notorious roads. The descent into Telluride features tight switchbacks, steep drop-offs, and no room for error. One-way downhill only. For experts with nerves of steel.

MOD

Ophir Pass

11,789 ft | Moderate
More accessible than Imogene or Black Bear. Connects Telluride to Highway 550. Good introduction to high-altitude Jeep roads. Stunning fall colors.

HARD

Tomboy Road

11,500 ft | Moderate-Difficult
Starts right from Telluride and climbs past the Tomboy Mine ruins. Connects to Imogene Pass. Historic mining infrastructure along the way.

More 4x4 Routes

Additional adventures

MOD

Alta Lakes Road

11,200 ft | Moderate
Scenic drive to Alta Lakes ghost town. Less technical than the passes. Good for photographers and those new to off-roading.

EASY

Last Dollar Road

10,663 ft | Easy
Connects Telluride to Highway 62 near Ridgway. Passable by most vehicles in good weather. Famous for fall aspen viewing.

MOD

Bridal Veil Road

10,500 ft | Moderate
Short but steep 4WD road to Bridal Veil Falls powerhouse. Starts at the east end of town. Waterfall views guaranteed.

LOOP

San Juan Skyway

Paved | All Vehicles
Not a Jeep trail but worth mentioning. 236-mile scenic loop through Telluride, Silverton, Durango, and more. America's most scenic drive.

Jeep Trail Tips

Essential info for off-roading

Can I rent a Jeep in Telluride?

Yes. Several outfitters rent 4WD vehicles and offer guided tours. Telluride Outside and San Juan Scenic Jeep Tours are established options. Guided tours recommended for first-timers on difficult trails.

When are the trails open?

Typically late June through September, depending on snowmelt. High passes like Imogene may not open until July. Check conditions before heading out. Snow can return as early as late September.

What vehicle do I need?

High-clearance 4WD with low-range gearing for most trails. AWD crossovers won't cut it on Imogene or Black Bear. Ophir and Last Dollar are more forgiving. Full-size SUVs or Jeeps recommended.

Is Black Bear really that dangerous?

Yes. Black Bear has claimed vehicles and lives. The infamous switchbacks leave no margin for error. Only attempt with an experienced driver, proper vehicle, and preferably with a spotter. Many choose guided tours.

What should I bring?

Full tank of gas, water, snacks, warm layers, rain gear, first aid kit, and a charged phone (though service is spotty). Recovery gear and a buddy vehicle recommended for remote trails.

Can I do Imogene Pass in a day?

Yes, but it's a full day. Plan 5-7 hours round trip including stops. Start early to avoid afternoon storms. The route from Telluride to Ouray (or reverse) makes a great loop combined with paved Highway 550.
