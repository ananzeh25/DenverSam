Where to Stay in Cripple Creek

Quick Info

Options: Casino hotels and historic B&Bs. Best value: Casino hotel packages. Atmosphere: Mountain town at 9,500 feet. Alternative: Day trip from Colorado Springs (1 hour).

Cripple Creek offers more overnight options than you might expect for a town of 1,100 residents. Several casinos operate attached hotels, and a handful of B&Bs occupy historic buildings. The mountain setting at nearly 10,000 feet means cool nights year-round.

Casino Hotels

Double Eagle Hotel & Casino

One of the larger properties with attached hotel. Casino floor, restaurants, and mountain views. Gaming packages often include room deals.

Wildwood Casino

Modern casino with hotel accommodations. Indoor pool, restaurant, and parking garage—rare amenities for Cripple Creek.

Bronco Billy's

Historic casino with hotel rooms. Smaller and more intimate than newer properties. Character atmosphere.

Historic Accommodations

B&Bs and Inns

Several Victorian-era buildings operate as bed and breakfasts. Personal service and historic character. Book ahead for weekends.

Considerations

Altitude: At 9,500 feet, some visitors experience mild altitude effects. Take it easy the first night.

Limited dining: Restaurant options are limited, especially late night. Casino restaurants may be your best bet.

Tips

Check casino packages. Gaming packages often include discounted or free rooms.

Day trip is viable. Colorado Springs is only an hour away—many visitors don't overnight.

Book summer weekends early. Limited rooms plus visitor season means availability gets tight.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I stay overnight or day trip?

Day trip works fine for casino gaming. Overnight lets you relax, explore more, and enjoy the mountain setting.

Is Cripple Creek expensive?

Generally affordable. Casino packages can be excellent value. Less expensive than Black Hawk overall.
