Where to Stay in Manitou Springs

Quick Info

Style: Historic inns, boutique hotels, vacation rentals. No chains: No major hotel brands in Manitou proper. Budget option: Stay in Colorado Springs (10 min away). Book early: Summer weekends and holidays fill fast.

Manitou Springs lodging matches the town's quirky character. You won't find a Holiday Inn here—instead, it's Victorian bed and breakfasts, historic hotels, and distinctive vacation rentals. The limited inventory means prices can run higher than nearby Colorado Springs, especially on weekends.

Staying in Manitou puts you within walking distance of downtown shops, restaurants, and the mineral springs. Early risers can walk to the Incline trailhead. The alternative: stay in Colorado Springs and drive 10-15 minutes.

Historic Hotels & Inns

Cliff House at Pikes Peak

The grand dame of Manitou lodging. A National Historic Landmark dating to 1873, completely restored with Victorian elegance. Luxurious rooms, on-site dining, and old-world charm. The most upscale option in town.

Historic Miramont Castle

Not a hotel, but worth mentioning—this 1895 castle offers occasional event stays. Check for special packages if you want a unique experience.

Rockledge Country Inn

A historic bed and breakfast with mountain views. Cozy rooms, full breakfast included, personal service. Good for couples seeking a romantic getaway.

Boutique Hotels & Motels

SunMountain Center

Retreat-style lodging with a New Age vibe matching Manitou's character. Simple rooms, meditation spaces, communal areas. Budget-friendly for the area.

El Colorado Lodge

Adobe-style cabins with Southwestern character. Individual units with kitchenettes. Pet-friendly. Quiet location slightly outside downtown bustle.

Town-N-Country Cottages

Cottage-style units with full kitchens. Good for families or longer stays. Walking distance to downtown but feels more residential.

Vacation Rentals

Airbnb and VRBO listings fill out Manitou's lodging options. You'll find everything from downtown apartments to mountain cabins with views. Vacation rentals often offer better value for groups and families.

Downtown locations: Walk to everything but deal with parking challenges and occasional street noise.

Hillside homes: Better views and more space, but you'll drive to attractions and dining.

Book early: Popular properties book months ahead for summer weekends. Holiday periods are especially tight.

Staying in Colorado Springs

Major hotel chains cluster along I-25 and near Garden of the Gods. Prices run lower than Manitou, with more availability. The tradeoff: you'll drive to Manitou's attractions.

Near Garden of the Gods: Several hotels sit at the park entrance, convenient for both Garden and Manitou activities.

Downtown Colorado Springs: 15-20 minutes from Manitou. More dining options, but you lose the mountain town immersion.

The Broadmoor: Colorado Springs' legendary resort. If budget isn't a concern and you want luxury, this five-star option is 15 minutes from Manitou.

Tips

Book months ahead for summer. Manitou has limited rooms. Peak season fills quickly.

Ask about parking. Street parking is competitive. Properties with dedicated parking save headaches.

Consider midweek. Prices drop Monday-Thursday. The town is quieter too.

Read reviews carefully. Historic buildings have quirks—stairs, small rooms, vintage plumbing. Know what you're getting.

Breakfast matters. B&Bs include breakfast. Hotels don't. In a town with limited early-morning options, this affects your Incline plans.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are there any chain hotels in Manitou Springs?

No major chains operate in Manitou proper. The nearest chains are in Colorado Springs, 10-15 minutes away.

What's the average price for lodging?

Expect $150-250/night for mid-range options. The Cliff House runs higher. Budget options in Colorado Springs start around $100.

Is staying in Manitou worth the premium?

If you want to walk to attractions, experience the town's nightlife, or start the Incline early—yes. If you're just day-tripping, Colorado Springs lodging works fine.

Are pets allowed?

Some properties welcome pets. El Colorado Lodge is notably pet-friendly. Always confirm policies before booking.

When should I book?

For summer weekends and holidays, book 2-3 months ahead. For Coffin Races weekend (late October), book even earlier—it's the biggest event of the year.
