Streets at SouthGlenn

Quick Info

Type: Open-air lifestyle center. Features: Shops, restaurants, movie theater. Location: University Blvd and Arapahoe Rd. Parking: Free. Hours: Vary by store. Events: Seasonal concerts and activities.

Streets at SouthGlenn is Centennial's primary shopping and dining destination—a redeveloped open-air center replacing the former enclosed SouthGlenn Mall. The walkable layout creates a town-center atmosphere with restaurants, retail, and entertainment.

Shopping

Anchor Stores

Macy's anchors the center with department store retail. Additional national and regional retailers throughout.

Specialty Retail

Mix of clothing, home goods, and specialty shops. National chains and local boutiques.

Services

Banks, salons, fitness studios, and professional services round out the tenant mix.

Dining

Sit-Down Restaurants

Full-service restaurants with varied cuisines—American, Italian, Mexican, Asian, and more. Good options for family dinners and date nights.

Fast Casual

Quick-service restaurants for lunch breaks and grab-and-go meals.

Coffee & Dessert

Coffee shops and dessert spots for afternoon breaks or post-movie treats.

Entertainment

Movie Theater

Multi-screen cinema showing first-run films. Stadium seating and concessions.

Seasonal Events

Summer concerts, holiday events, and community activities throughout the year. Check schedule for upcoming events.

Location & Access

By Car

Located at University Boulevard and Arapahoe Road. Easy access from I-25 via Arapahoe Road exit.

Parking

Free surface and structured parking throughout the center. Generally available except during peak holiday periods.

By Transit

RTD bus routes serve the area. Arapahoe at Village Center light rail station is about 2 miles east.

Tips

Weekday visits. Less crowded than weekends, especially for dining.

Outdoor seating. Many restaurants offer patio seating—nice in good weather.

Combine trips. Shopping, dining, and movies in one walkable destination.

Frequently Asked Questions

What happened to SouthGlenn Mall?

The enclosed mall was redeveloped into the current open-air Streets at SouthGlenn format, opening in phases starting around 2009.

Is there a grocery store?

King Soopers is nearby. The center focuses on retail, dining, and entertainment.

Is parking free?

Yes—free parking throughout the center.

Are dogs allowed?

Outdoor areas are dog-friendly. Individual stores have their own policies.

How does it compare to Park Meadows?

Smaller and more casual than Park Meadows. Good for dining and local shopping without the mall experience.
