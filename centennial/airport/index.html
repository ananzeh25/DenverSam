Centennial Airport

Quick Info

Code: KAPA (APA). Type: General aviation (no commercial airlines). Rank: One of the busiest GA airports in the US. Services: Private, corporate, charter. Location: South of Centennial near I-25 and Arapahoe Rd.

Centennial Airport is one of the busiest general aviation airports in the United States. It serves private pilots, corporate aviation, charter flights, and flight training—but has no commercial airline service. For commercial flights, use Denver International Airport (DEN).

Airport Services

Corporate Aviation

FBOs (Fixed Base Operators) provide services for corporate jets and business aviation. Hangars, fuel, maintenance, and passenger services available.

Charter Flights

Charter operators offer on-demand private flights. Popular for business travel and ski trips to mountain destinations.

Flight Training

Multiple flight schools operate at Centennial. Training available from private pilot through advanced ratings.

Aircraft Services

Maintenance, avionics, and aircraft management services for based and transient aircraft.

For Visitors

Private Flights

If arriving by private or charter aircraft, FBOs provide ground transportation, rental cars, and passenger amenities.

No Commercial Service

Centennial has no commercial airline flights. For scheduled airline service, use Denver International Airport (DEN), about 25 miles northeast.

Location Advantage

Closer to south Denver and the Tech Center than DIA. Corporate travelers often prefer Centennial for convenience.

FBOs

Multiple Operators

Several FBOs compete for business, offering fuel, hangar space, and passenger services. Compare services and pricing.

Amenities

Typical FBO amenities include pilot lounges, conference rooms, rental cars, and crew services.

Tips

Not for airlines. This is a general aviation airport—no Southwest, United, or other airlines.

Corporate convenience. Great for business travelers to the south metro and Tech Center.

Scenic flights. Some operators offer scenic flights and mountain tours.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I fly commercially from Centennial?

No—there's no commercial airline service. Use Denver International Airport (DEN) for commercial flights.

What is general aviation?

All civil aviation except scheduled airline service—private planes, corporate jets, charter flights, flight training.

Can I tour the airport?

Security restrictions limit access. Some FBOs may offer tours or open house events.

How far from downtown Denver?

About 15 miles south of downtown via I-25. Closer than DIA for many south metro locations.

Is there a restaurant open to the public?

The Perfect Landing restaurant is located at the airport with runway views.
